# Deliverable Templates

Outlines for each major consulting deliverable. Adapt to the specific
engagement — these are starting structures, not rigid formats.

## 1. Executive Summary

**Audience:** C-suite, board members
**Length:** 1-2 pages
**Tone:** Business-first, minimal technical jargon

```markdown
# Executive Summary: AI Opportunity Assessment for [Company]

## Situation
[2-3 sentences on the business context: why AI, why now, what triggered this
engagement]

## Key Findings
1. **AI Maturity**: [Company] scores [X/5] on the AI Maturity Model, placing
   them in the [stage] category. The primary gaps are in [dimensions].
2. **Top Opportunities**: We identified [N] AI opportunities across
   [departments]. The top 3 by impact-feasibility score are:
   - [Opportunity 1]: Estimated [impact] with [timeline]
   - [Opportunity 2]: Estimated [impact] with [timeline]
   - [Opportunity 3]: Estimated [impact] with [timeline]
3. **Risks**: [Key risks identified, especially any red flags]

## Recommended Approach
[Phase 1: Quick win description — 30-60 days — estimated investment]
[Phase 2: Strategic initiative description — 3-6 months — estimated investment]
[Phase 3: Scale and optimize — 6-12 months — estimated investment]

## Investment & Expected Return
| Phase | Investment | Expected Annual Return | Payback Period |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Phase 1 | $X | $Y | Z months |
| Phase 2 | $X | $Y | Z months |
| Phase 3 | $X | $Y | Z months |

## Next Steps
[Specific, actionable next steps with owners and dates]
```

## 2. Discovery Report

**Audience:** Project stakeholders, technical and business
**Length:** 10-25 pages depending on scope
**Tone:** Thorough, evidence-based, accessible

```markdown
# AI Discovery Report: [Company]

## 1. Company Overview
[From company-briefing.md — company profile, industry, competitive landscape]

## 2. AI Maturity Assessment
[From ai-maturity-assessment.md — scores per dimension, evidence, gaps]
### Dimension Scores
[Table of all 5 dimensions with scores and key evidence]
### Gap Analysis
[Where bottleneck dimensions will block progress]

## 3. Stakeholder Findings
[From stakeholder-discovery.md — synthesized across all interviews]
### Executive Perspective
[What leadership wants and expects]
### Department Pain Points
[Organized by department — prioritized pain points]
### Technical Landscape
[Current architecture, data readiness, integration constraints]
### Frontline Insights
[What workers actually experience daily]

## 4. Risk Assessment
[From red-flags.md — identified risks with severity and mitigation]

## 5. Opportunity Matrix
[From opportunity-matrix.md — all opportunities scored and categorized]
### Quick Wins (High Impact, High Feasibility)
[Detailed description, estimated impact, approach for each]
### Strategic Bets (High Impact, Lower Feasibility)
[Detailed description, estimated impact, what's needed to improve feasibility]
### Deprioritized
[Listed with reasoning for deprioritization]

## 6. Recommended Roadmap
[Phased implementation plan with dependencies and milestones]

## 7. Investment Summary
[High-level cost estimates by phase]

## Appendices
- A: Interview summaries
- B: Competitive AI landscape
- C: Technology assessment details
- D: Regulatory considerations
```

**Audience:** Legal, procurement, project sponsors
**Length:** 5-15 pages
**Tone:** Precise, unambiguous, contractual

```markdown

**Client:** [Company]
**Consultant:** [Your firm]
**Date:** [Date]
**Version:** [Version]

## 1. Background & Objectives
[Brief context — what problem this project addresses and why]

## 2. Scope of Work
### In Scope
[Explicit list of what's included — be specific]
- [Deliverable/activity 1]
- [Deliverable/activity 2]
- [Deliverable/activity 3]

### Out of Scope
[Explicit list of what's NOT included — prevents scope creep]
- [Exclusion 1]
- [Exclusion 2]

## 5. Timeline

| Phase | Start | End | Key Milestones |

[How deliverables are reviewed and accepted]

[Process for scope changes — how they're requested, evaluated, priced]
```

## 4. Proposal

**Audience:** Decision-makers, sometimes non-technical
**Length:** 8-15 pages
**Tone:** Persuasive, professional, outcome-focused

```markdown
# Proposal: [Project Title]
**Prepared for:** [Company] | **Prepared by:** [Your firm] | **Date:** [Date]

## The Challenge
[Client's problem in their language — mirror back what they told you]

## Our Understanding
[Demonstrate that you listened — summarize their situation, pain points,
and goals. Reference specific details from discovery]

## Proposed Solution
[What you'll build/implement and why this approach]
### Approach
[Methodology, key architectural decisions, technology choices]
### Why This Approach
[Evidence for why this is the right solution — industry precedent,
technical rationale, alignment with their maturity level]

## What You Get
[Concrete deliverables and outcomes — focus on business value]

## Timeline
[Visual or tabular timeline with major milestones]

## Team
[Brief bios of key team members — relevant experience, not full resumes]

## Investment
[Pricing with tiers if applicable]
| Option | Scope | Investment | Timeline |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Essential | [Core scope] | $X | [Weeks] |
| Recommended | [Core + enhancements] | $Y | [Weeks] |
| Comprehensive | [Full scope] | $Z | [Weeks] |

## Expected ROI
[Financial projections — be honest and show your math]

## Why Us
[Brief differentiators — relevant experience, domain expertise, methodology]

## Next Steps
[Clear, specific actions to move forward]
1. [Action] by [date]
2. [Action] by [date]
```

**Tone:** Quantitative, conservative, transparent about assumptions
