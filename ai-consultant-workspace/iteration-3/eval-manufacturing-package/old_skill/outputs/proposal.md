# Proposal: AI-Driven Predictive Maintenance & Operational Intelligence

**Prepared for:** TitanWorks | **Date:** March 19, 2026

---

## The Challenge

TitanWorks is losing $2 million every month — $24 million per year — to unplanned equipment downtime across its 4 factories. Today, only 40% of critical equipment has sensor monitoring. The other 60% depends on manual inspections that cannot reliably predict failures before they happen. Meanwhile, experienced maintenance technicians hold critical diagnostic knowledge that is not captured in any system.

Eighteen months ago, a digital transformation initiative did not deliver expected results. The organization is understandably cautious about the next technology investment. The CFO has made clear that any path forward requires hard ROI evidence, not promises.

## Our Understanding

Based on our assessment, TitanWorks has:

- **A clear, quantified problem** — $2M/month in unplanned downtime with a known dollar baseline
- **A foundation to build on** — 40% of critical equipment already has sensors generating data, and the Azure migration 6 months ago provides cloud infrastructure
- **Enthusiastic operational leadership** — plant managers across all 4 factories want predictive maintenance solutions
- **A talent gap that shapes the approach** — with zero in-house data science capability, any solution must minimize the need for AI specialists or be fully managed
- **Organizational caution that demands a phased approach** — the previous initiative's failure means trust must be rebuilt with visible, measurable results before larger commitments
- **An AI maturity score of 1.7/5.0** — placing TitanWorks in the Foundation stage, which means the engagement must combine immediate value delivery with capability building

## Proposed Solution

A three-phase engagement that starts with a high-confidence quick win on existing infrastructure, expands coverage as value is proven, and builds toward full operational AI capability.

### Approach

**Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Prove It**
Deploy a SaaS predictive maintenance platform on the 40% of equipment that already has sensors. Build the Azure IoT data pipeline from factory floor to cloud. Launch a GenAI maintenance knowledge base for technicians. Deliver measurable downtime reduction within 90 days.

**Phase 2 (Months 4-9): Expand It**
Extend sensor coverage to high-priority uninstrumented equipment. Scale PdM to additional equipment and factories. Begin building internal data engineering capability. Establish AI governance framework.

**Phase 3 (Months 9-18): Scale It**
Achieve full sensor coverage across all critical equipment. Deploy visual inspection AI on highest-volume production lines. Build predictive maintenance agent workflow (automated work order generation). Hire AI/ML lead for long-term internal capability.

### Why This Approach

1. **SaaS-first**: With no in-house data science team, a managed platform delivers value immediately without a multi-month custom build
2. **Phased investment**: Each phase has go/no-go criteria, so TitanWorks never commits to the next phase without evidence from the current one
3. **Azure-native**: Leverages the recent Azure migration investment rather than introducing a new platform
4. **Industry-proven**: Predictive maintenance in manufacturing has 10+ years of documented ROI across hundreds of deployments — this is not experimental technology
5. **Maturity-appropriate**: Recommendations match TitanWorks' current maturity (1.7/5.0) — foundation building plus targeted quick wins, not an AI moonshot
