# Executive Summary: AI Opportunity Assessment for TitanWorks

## Situation

TitanWorks is losing $2 million per month to unplanned equipment downtime across its four factories, representing 8% of annual revenue. With sensors on only 40% of critical equipment and no predictive analytics capability, the maintenance organization operates reactively. A recent Azure cloud migration and enthusiastic plant managers create favorable conditions for an AI-powered solution, but the shadow of a failed digital transformation initiative 18 months ago requires this engagement to be disciplined, measurable, and phased.

## Key Findings

1. **AI Maturity: 1.68/5.0 (Foundation Stage).** TitanWorks has partial data infrastructure (sensors, ERP) and a cloud platform (Azure), but zero AI/ML talent, no AI governance, and a cautious organizational culture. The primary bottleneck is talent (Score: 1/5); the organization wants AI but cannot currently execute it internally. The engagement must build foundations alongside delivering results.

2. **Top Opportunities:** We identified 9 AI opportunities. The top 3 by impact and feasibility:
   - **Predictive Maintenance on instrumented equipment**: Deploy a commercial platform on the 40% of equipment with sensors. Estimated $240K-$400K/month savings. 8-12 weeks to first value. Buy, not build.
   - **Maintenance Knowledge Base (GenAI)**: RAG-powered assistant giving technicians instant access to equipment knowledge. Reduces mean time to repair by 15-25%. Estimated $50K-$100K/month. 6-8 weeks to first value.
   - **Full Fleet Sensor Expansion**: Extend predictive maintenance to remaining 60% of equipment after proving ROI. Estimated $360K-$600K/month additional savings. 6-12 month horizon.

3. **Risks:** Two high-severity flags: (a) the failed digital transformation creates a trust deficit requiring visible, fast wins and clear differentiation from the previous effort; (b) zero AI talent means all capability must be brought externally initially, with a structured internal hiring plan. No critical engagement-stopping flags were found. Five positive indicators support proceeding: plant manager enthusiasm, quantifiable problem, Azure platform, existing sensors, and an engaged (not dismissive) CFO.

## Recommended Approach

| Phase | Timeline | What Happens | Investment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Phase 1: Prove It** | Months 1-3 | Predictive maintenance pilot on top 10 assets. GenAI knowledge base. Data pipeline. AI governance. | $190K-$250K |
| **Phase 2: Scale It** | Months 4-9 | Expand to all instrumented equipment. Plan sensor expansion. Maintenance agent. Hire data engineer. | $250K-$350K |
| **Phase 3: Own It** | Months 9-18 | Full fleet sensors. Internal ML hire. Self-sustaining capability. Secondary use cases. | $500K-$1.5M+ |

Each phase has an explicit go/no-go decision gate. No commitment to Phase 2 is required until Phase 1 results are measured.
