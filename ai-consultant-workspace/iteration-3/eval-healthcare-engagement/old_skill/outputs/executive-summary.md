# Executive Summary: AI Opportunity Assessment for MedTech Solutions

## Situation

MedTech Solutions operates a chain of specialty clinics generating $80M in annual revenue with approximately 500 employees. The company currently relies on paper-based patient intake and the CEO has expressed a desire to use AI to modernize operations. With a 12-person IT team and a hybrid on-premises/AWS infrastructure, MedTech Solutions has the foundational elements to begin an AI transformation -- but critical groundwork must come first.

## Key Findings

1. **AI Maturity: 1.75 out of 5.0 (Foundation Stage)**. MedTech Solutions scores in the Foundation stage across five assessed dimensions: Data (1.5), Infrastructure (2.0), Talent (1.5), Governance (1.5), and Culture (2.5). The organization's leadership appetite for AI (the highest dimension) outpaces its current data, talent, and governance foundations. The primary bottleneck is data -- paper-based processes mean the most valuable operational data is not machine-readable. This maturity level calls for a foundation-building engagement that includes quick wins to demonstrate value while building the prerequisites for larger AI initiatives.

2. **Top Opportunities**: We identified 9 AI opportunities across clinical operations, revenue cycle, and patient experience. The top 3 by impact-feasibility score are:
   - **Digital Patient Intake with AI** (Impact: 5, Feasibility: 4): Replace paper intake with a digital platform. Eliminates manual data entry across all clinics, reduces transcription errors, improves patient experience, and creates the digital data foundation required for all downstream AI. Buy solution; 30-60 day pilot. Estimated investment: $50K-$100K for implementation plus $2-5/patient visit for platform.
   - **Ambient Clinical Documentation** (Impact: 5, Feasibility: 4): Deploy an AI scribe that listens to patient encounters and generates clinical notes. Saves clinicians an estimated 2-3 hours per day on documentation. Buy solution (Nuance DAX, Abridge, or similar); 30-60 day pilot with 3-5 clinicians. Estimated investment: $150-300/clinician/month.
   - **Patient Communication Automation** (Impact: 4, Feasibility: 4): AI-powered appointment reminders, pre-visit instructions, and patient messaging. Reduces no-shows by 5-15% and frees front desk staff from phone-based scheduling. Buy solution; 30-45 day deployment. Estimated investment: $30K-$60K annually.

3. **Risks**: No engagement-killing red flags were identified. Key risks that must be addressed: (a) budget needs to be specified before scoping (currently vague), (b) no AI governance exists in a HIPAA-regulated environment -- must be established before deploying AI tools, (c) paper-based processes in 2026 may indicate deeper organizational resistance to technology change, and (d) 12-person IT team capacity must be assessed to ensure they can support new initiatives.

## Recommended Approach

**Phase 1: Quick Wins + Foundation (Month 1-3)** -- Estimated investment: $150K-$300K
- Deploy digital patient intake at one pilot clinic, then expand
- Pilot ambient clinical documentation with 3-5 clinicians
- Deploy patient communication automation across clinics
- Establish AI governance framework and AI use policy
- Conduct full data architecture assessment

**Phase 2: Expand and Operationalize (Month 3-6)** -- Estimated investment: $200K-$400K
- Expand Phase 1 deployments across all clinic locations
- Begin AI-assisted medical coding pilot (potential to recover 2-5% of revenue from reduced coding errors and missed charges)
- Evaluate prior authorization automation vendors
- Begin internal AI capability building (training, potential hire)

**Phase 3: Scale and Differentiate (Month 6-12)** -- Estimated investment: $300K-$600K
- Scale coding and prior auth automation to production
- Deploy scheduling optimization
- Add AI agent capabilities to existing deployments
- Evaluate advanced use cases (clinical knowledge base, cross-clinic analytics)
