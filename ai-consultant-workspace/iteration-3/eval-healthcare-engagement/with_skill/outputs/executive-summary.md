# Executive Summary: AI Opportunity Assessment for MedTech Solutions

## Situation

MedTech Solutions operates a chain of specialty clinics (~500 employees, $80M
revenue) that relies heavily on paper-based processes for patient intake and,
likely, other operational workflows. The CEO has expressed interest in using AI
to modernize operations. This assessment evaluates MedTech's readiness for AI
adoption and identifies the highest-value opportunities to pursue.

## Key Findings

1. **AI Maturity: 1.75 out of 5.0 (Foundation Stage)**. MedTech Solutions is in
   the earliest stage of AI readiness. The primary gaps are in data (paper-based
   processes), talent (no AI/ML staff), and governance (no AI policy or
   framework). The positive indicators are executive sponsorship (CEO-driven)
   and existing AWS cloud infrastructure. At this maturity level, the
   recommended engagement is foundation-building — data digitization,
   infrastructure planning, and targeted quick wins — with a 6-12 month horizon
   for meaningful AI capabilities.

2. **Top Opportunities**: We identified 8 AI opportunities across clinical
   operations, revenue cycle, and patient engagement. The top 3 by
   impact-feasibility score are:
   - **Patient Intake Digitization with AI** (Impact: 4, Feasibility: 4):
     Replace paper intake with digital + AI-assisted data capture. 30-60 day
     time to value. Estimated 50-70% reduction in intake processing time.
     This also creates the data foundation for all subsequent AI initiatives.
   - **AI-Assisted Medical Coding** (Impact: 5, Feasibility: 3.5): AI-powered
     coding suggestions to reduce claim denial rates by 30-50%. At $80M
     revenue, a 2-3% denial reduction represents $1.6M-$2.4M in annual impact.
     Requires digitized clinical data as a prerequisite.
   - **Ambient Clinical Documentation** (Impact: 5, Feasibility: 2.5): AI scribe
     technology saving clinicians 2-3 hours/day on documentation. Strategic bet
     requiring EHR integration and HIPAA-compliant vendor selection. 3-6 month
     time to value at pilot scale.

3. **Risks**: Two high-severity risks require attention: (a) paper-based
   processes mean no structured data exists for AI to work with — data
   digitization must precede or accompany any AI deployment; (b) the CEO's
   expectations need calibration — "AI to modernize operations" is a 12-18
   month journey at this maturity level, not a quick fix. Four medium-severity
   risks (budget clarity, compliance involvement, GenAI risk planning, and
   potential shadow AI usage) are manageable with proper engagement framing.

## Recommended Approach

**Phase 1: Foundation and Quick Win (Month 1-3) — Estimated Investment: $80K-$150K**
Deploy digital patient intake across a pilot clinic with AI-assisted data
capture. Conduct AI readiness assessment, shadow AI audit, and establish
baseline AI governance. Begin revenue cycle data analysis for Phase 2 readiness.

**Phase 2: Revenue Cycle AI and Clinical Pilot (Month 3-9) — Estimated Investment: $200K-$400K**
Deploy AI-assisted medical coding. Pilot ambient clinical documentation at one
clinic site. Expand digital intake to all locations. Build internal AI
capability with designated champion(s) on the IT team.

**Phase 3: Scale and Optimize (Month 9-18) — Estimated Investment: $300K-$600K**
Scale ambient documentation across all clinics. Deploy prior authorization
automation. Evaluate patient communication agent. Establish ongoing AI
governance and monitoring.
