# Executive Summary: AI Opportunity Assessment for MedTech Solutions

**Prepared:** March 2026
**Status:** Preliminary — based on pre-engagement research. To be finalized after stakeholder discovery.

---

## Situation

MedTech Solutions operates a chain of specialty clinics generating $80M in annual revenue with approximately 500 employees. The company's CEO has expressed interest in leveraging AI to modernize operations. Currently, core workflows such as patient intake remain paper-based, creating operational inefficiency, data quality issues, and a growing competitive gap as peer organizations digitize and adopt AI-powered tools.

The healthcare industry is in a period of rapid AI adoption. Competitors and industry leaders are deploying ambient clinical documentation, AI-powered coding, and patient communication agents. Organizations that delay modernization face compounding disadvantages in operational efficiency, clinician retention, and patient experience.

## Key Findings

1. **AI Maturity: Foundation Stage (1.75/5.0).** MedTech Solutions has the executive sponsorship and cultural willingness to pursue AI, but lacks the digital workflow foundations, data infrastructure, and specialized talent required for advanced AI deployment. The primary gap is in data — paper-based processes mean that valuable patient interaction data is not being captured digitally.

2. **Six AI opportunities identified across clinical and operational domains.** The top three by combined impact and feasibility are:
   - **Digital patient intake** — eliminates the paper bottleneck, improves patient experience, creates structured data. Achievable in 6-10 weeks with existing vendor solutions. Estimated annual value: $200K-$500K.
   - **Ambient clinical documentation** — saves clinicians 2-3 hours/day in documentation time, improving capacity, satisfaction, and retention. Pilot-ready in 3-4 months. Estimated annual value: $500K-$1.5M.
   - **AI-powered medical coding** — reduces coding errors 30-50%, accelerates charge capture, and decreases claim denials. Estimated annual value: $300K-$1M.

3. **No critical red flags, but several items need attention.** Budget expectations need clarification, CTO alignment must be confirmed, and compliance/legal should be engaged early given HIPAA requirements. The persistence of paper-based processes warrants investigation to understand root causes.

## Recommended Approach

We recommend a phased engagement with clear decision points, starting small and expanding as value is demonstrated:

| Phase | Timeline | Focus | Estimated Investment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Phase 0: Discovery** | 4 weeks | Complete stakeholder interviews, validate maturity assessment, finalize opportunity priorities, deliver findings | $25K-$50K |
| **Phase 1: Quick Win + Foundation** | Months 1-3 | Deploy digital patient intake across all clinics; assess data architecture and plan integration layer | $75K-$150K |
| **Phase 2: Clinical AI Pilots** | Months 3-6 | Pilot ambient documentation with select clinicians; evaluate and pilot medical coding AI; begin data platform build | $150K-$300K |
| **Phase 3: Scale & Optimize** | Months 6-12 | Roll out proven pilots organization-wide; launch patient communication and prior authorization automation; mature governance | $200K-$500K |

## Investment & Expected Return

| Phase | Investment | Expected Annual Return | Payback Period |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
