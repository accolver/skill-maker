## Detailed Stakeholder Profiles

### Tier 1: Executive Sponsors (Engage Weekly)

#### CEO
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Initiative** | Ultimate sponsor, budget authority |
| **Primary Interest** | Competitive positioning, revenue growth, operational efficiency |
| **Likely Concerns** | ROI justification, timeline to results, organizational disruption |
| **AI Understanding** | Likely high-level; influenced by media and peer discussions |
| **Engagement Style** | Outcome-focused, wants big picture with clear metrics |
| **Communication Preference** | Monthly executive summaries, quarterly board-ready updates |
| **Potential Resistance** | Low (initiated the request) |
| **Key Question to Ask** | "What does success look like to you personally in 12 months?" |
| **Win Condition** | Visible modernization, measurable ROI, positive patient feedback |

#### CTO
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Initiative** | Technical sponsor, primary point of contact |
| **Primary Interest** | Technical excellence, team development, infrastructure modernization |
| **Likely Concerns** | Team capacity, technical debt, integration complexity, security |
| **AI Understanding** | Likely solid technical understanding; may have specific opinions |
| **Engagement Style** | Detail-oriented, wants to understand the "how" |
| **Communication Preference** | Bi-weekly technical reviews, direct access for urgent issues |
| **Potential Resistance** | Low-Medium (may have concerns about external resources vs. building internally) |
| **Key Question to Ask** | "What does your team need most to succeed with this initiative?" |
| **Win Condition** | Team capability growth, clean architecture, no security incidents |

### Tier 2: Key Decision Influencers (Engage Bi-Weekly)

#### CMO / Medical Director
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Initiative** | Clinical workflow authority, patient safety advocate |
| **Primary Interest** | Clinical workflow efficiency, patient safety, provider satisfaction |
| **Likely Concerns** | Clinical disruption, patient data accuracy, provider adoption |
| **AI Understanding** | Variable; may be skeptical of AI in clinical settings |
| **Engagement Style** | Evidence-based, wants clinical validation |
| **Communication Preference** | Monthly clinical impact reviews |
| **Potential Resistance** | Medium (protective of clinical workflows, rightfully cautious) |
| **Key Question to Ask** | "Where do your providers lose the most time on non-clinical tasks?" |
| **Win Condition** | Reduced provider burden, maintained/improved care quality |

#### COO (if role exists)
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Initiative** | Operational efficiency champion, multi-clinic coordination |
| **Primary Interest** | Operational metrics, standardization, scalability |
| **Likely Concerns** | Disruption to daily operations, staff training burden, timeline |
| **Engagement Style** | Process-oriented, metrics-driven |
| **Communication Preference** | Bi-weekly operational updates with KPIs |
| **Potential Resistance** | Low-Medium (wants improvement but concerned about disruption) |
| **Key Question to Ask** | "Which clinic runs most efficiently today, and what makes it different?" |
| **Win Condition** | Standardized processes, improved metrics, smooth transitions |

#### CFO
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Initiative** | Budget gatekeeper, ROI validator |
| **Primary Interest** | Financial returns, cost control, revenue optimization |
| **Likely Concerns** | Total cost of ownership, budget overruns, uncertain ROI |
| **Engagement Style** | Numbers-driven, wants clear financial projections |
| **Communication Preference** | Monthly financial reviews, phase gate budget approvals |
| **Potential Resistance** | Medium (will scrutinize investment closely) |
| **Key Question to Ask** | "What financial metrics would make this a clear win?" |
| **Win Condition** | On-budget delivery, measurable financial returns |

### Tier 3: Implementation Partners (Engage as Needed)

#### IT Team Leads
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Initiative** | Implementation team, ongoing operations owners |
| **Primary Interest** | Skill development, manageable workload, good technology choices |
| **Likely Concerns** | Added workload, being replaced by consultants, technology choices made without input |
| **AI Understanding** | Variable across the team |
| **Engagement Style** | Collaborative, want to be included in technical decisions |
| **Communication Preference** | Weekly project standups, direct involvement in design |
| **Potential Resistance** | Medium-High (may feel threatened or overwhelmed) |
| **Key Question to Ask** | "What would make your job easier tomorrow?" |
| **Win Condition** | New skills, reduced maintenance burden, professional growth |

#### Clinic Directors / Managers
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Initiative** | Local change champions, clinic-level adoption owners |
| **Primary Interest** | Smooth clinic operations, staff happiness, patient satisfaction |
| **Likely Concerns** | Disruption to patient flow, staff learning curve, system reliability |
| **Engagement Style** | Practical, want to see it work in their environment |
| **Communication Preference** | Pre-rollout briefings, post-go-live daily check-ins |
| **Potential Resistance** | Medium (cautious about change affecting daily operations) |
| **Key Question to Ask** | "What's the one thing that would most improve your clinic's daily operations?" |
| **Win Condition** | Smoother operations, happier staff, better patient feedback |

#### Front Desk Staff
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Initiative** | Primary users of digital intake, patient-facing change |
| **Primary Interest** | Easier daily workflow, less repetitive work, clear instructions |
| **Likely Concerns** | Job security, technology difficulty, patient frustration during transition |
| **Engagement Style** | Hands-on training, clear documentation, accessible support |
| **Communication Preference** | In-person training, quick reference guides, help desk access |
| **Potential Resistance** | Medium-High (most directly affected by intake changes) |
| **Key Question to Ask** | "What's the most frustrating part of your day?" |
| **Win Condition** | Less paper, fewer repetitive tasks, patients arrive prepared |

#### Clinical Staff (Nurses, Medical Assistants)
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Initiative** | Users of improved clinical workflows |
| **Primary Interest** | Patient care quality, efficient workflows, less documentation |
| **Likely Concerns** | System reliability, accuracy of digital intake data, learning curve |
| **Engagement Style** | Practical demonstrations, pilot participation |
| **Communication Preference** | Brief huddle updates, "what's changing for you" summaries |
| **Potential Resistance** | Low-Medium (benefit from better data quality) |
| **Key Question to Ask** | "How often do you have to re-ask patients for info that should already be in the chart?" |
| **Win Condition** | Complete, accurate patient information before visit starts |

## Change Champion Network

### Proposed Champion Structure

```
Executive Sponsor (CTO)
    │
    ├── Clinical Champion (Senior Nurse or MA)
    │       └── Responsibilities: validate clinical workflows, peer advocacy
    │
    ├── Administrative Champion (Senior Front Desk Lead)
    │       └── Responsibilities: intake process expertise, staff support
    │
    ├── Technical Champion (Senior IT Team Member)
    │       └── Responsibilities: technical advocacy, internal troubleshooting
    │
    └── Clinic Champion (1 per pilot clinic)
            └── Responsibilities: local coordination, feedback collection
```

### Champion Selection Criteria
- Respected by peers
- Open to technology change (early adopter tendencies)
- Good communicator
- Available for 10-15% time commitment during implementation
- Willing to provide candid feedback

### Champion Enablement
- Briefed 2 weeks before broader communication
- Included in design review sessions
- Given early access to systems for feedback
- Recognized publicly for their contributions
- Monthly champion network meeting for cross-clinic learning

## Communication Plan

### Key Messages by Audience

| Audience | Core Message |
|----------|-------------|
| Executives | "We're investing in our future -- better patient experience, more efficient operations, competitive advantage." |
| IT Team | "We're building your capabilities and modernizing your toolkit. You'll lead the ongoing operations." |
| Clinic Directors | "We're making your clinics run smoother with less paperwork and better data." |
| Front Desk | "We're eliminating the most tedious parts of your job and giving you better tools." |
| Clinical Staff | "Patients will arrive with complete, accurate information so you can focus on care." |
| Patients | "We're making your visit easier -- less paperwork, shorter wait times." |

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- Do NOT lead with "AI" when talking to clinical or front desk staff (leads to fear)
- Do NOT promise "no disruption" (there will be a learning curve; be honest)
- Do NOT skip over front desk staff input (they know the process better than anyone)
- Do NOT present this as a cost-cutting/headcount-reduction initiative
- Do NOT assume the CTO speaks for the entire IT team
