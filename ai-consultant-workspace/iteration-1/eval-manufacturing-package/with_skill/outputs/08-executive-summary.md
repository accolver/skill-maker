# Executive Summary: AI-Driven Downtime Reduction for TitanWorks

## Situation

TitanWorks is losing $2M per month ($24M/year) to unplanned equipment downtime across 4 factories. With 40% of critical equipment already fitted with sensors and a recent Azure migration complete, TitanWorks has the foundational assets to deploy AI-driven predictive maintenance. However, a prior "digital transformation" initiative failed 18 months ago, and the organization rightly demands evidence-based returns before committing to a new investment.

## Key Findings

1. **AI Maturity Score: 1.55 / 5.0 (Foundation Stage).** TitanWorks is early in its AI journey. The primary gaps are talent (zero data science capability) and governance (no AI policy or processes). Strengths include existing sensor infrastructure, Azure cloud readiness, and enthusiastic plant leadership. The recommended approach accounts for these constraints: buy proven technology rather than building custom solutions.

2. **Top 3 Opportunities by Impact and Feasibility:**
   - **Predictive Maintenance** (Score 9/10): Deploy SaaS-based anomaly detection on sensor-equipped equipment. Directly addresses the $24M/year downtime problem. Time to first measurable result: 60-90 days.
   - **Maintenance Knowledge Base** (Score 7/10): GenAI assistant for technicians, reducing diagnostic time and preserving institutional expertise. Complements predictive maintenance by reducing repair time when failures do occur.
   - **Sensor Expansion** (Score 7/10): Retrofit the remaining 60% of unmonitored equipment to unlock full predictive maintenance coverage. Strategic investment that multiplies the value of the AI platform.

3. **Risks Identified:** The failed prior initiative creates organizational skepticism that must be managed through fast, measurable wins and phased commitments. The complete absence of internal data science talent means TitanWorks must buy rather than build, and invest in capability development alongside technology deployment.

## Recommended Approach

| Phase | Description | Duration | Investment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Phase 1 | Predictive maintenance pilot at best-instrumented factory + data foundation assessment | Months 1-3 | $320,000 |
| Phase 2 | Expand to all 4 factories + GenAI knowledge base + sensor expansion planning | Months 3-6 | $380,000 |
| Phase 3 | System optimization + sensor retrofit (top 50 machines) + quality inspection pilot | Months 6-12 | $350K-$1.05M |

Phase gates after Phase 1 and Phase 2 allow the CFO to evaluate results before committing to subsequent phases.

**Sensitivity Analysis Highlights:**
- At half the expected downtime reduction (15% vs. 30%), payback is 6 months with 206% 3-year ROI
- Combined worst-case scenario (half effectiveness, higher costs, slow ramp) still yields 91% 3-year ROI and 10-month payback
- Break-even requires only 5.7% downtime reduction; industry benchmarks range from 30-50%
- There is no realistic scenario where this investment loses money
