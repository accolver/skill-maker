## Detailed Stakeholder Profiles

### Tier 1: Manage Closely (High Influence, High Interest)

#### CEO
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Project** | Executive Sponsor |
| **Known Position** | Strong advocate -- initiated the AI modernization vision |
| **Likely Motivations** | Competitive advantage, operational efficiency, growth enablement, investor/board optics |
| **Likely Concerns** | ROI timeline, disruption to operations, reputational risk |
| **Engagement Strategy** | Monthly executive steering updates; ROI-focused communication; quick wins to validate vision; frame AI in business outcome terms, not technical terms |
| **Communication Style** | High-level, outcome-oriented, visual dashboards preferred |
| **Risk** | May have unrealistic expectations of AI capabilities |
| **Key Question to Answer** | "What tangible business results will we see and when?" |

#### CTO
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Project** | Key Partner / Technical Decision-Maker |
| **Known Position** | TBD -- first call next week |
| **Likely Motivations** | Modernize infrastructure, build team capabilities, deliver on CEO vision, career advancement |
| **Likely Concerns** | Team capacity, technical feasibility, integration complexity, security, being set up to fail |
| **Engagement Strategy** | Weekly working sessions; transparent about challenges; involve in architecture decisions; position as co-architect of the strategy; respect their knowledge of internal systems |
| **Communication Style** | Technical depth appreciated; wants to understand the "how" |
| **Risk** | May feel threatened if consultant is perceived as replacing their role |
| **Key Question to Answer** | "How will this make my team more capable and my systems more robust?" |

#### CMO / Medical Director
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Project** | Clinical Gatekeeper |
| **Known Position** | TBD -- to be identified |
| **Likely Motivations** | Clinical quality, physician satisfaction, patient outcomes, reduced administrative burden |
| **Likely Concerns** | Clinical workflow disruption, patient safety, AI replacing clinical judgment, physician pushback |
| **Engagement Strategy** | Monthly clinical advisory meetings; frame AI as physician empowerment tool; demonstrate time savings; involve in clinical AI decisions; respect clinical expertise |
| **Communication Style** | Evidence-based; peer-reviewed references; patient outcome focus |
| **Risk** | Could veto clinical AI initiatives if not properly engaged |
| **Key Question to Answer** | "Will this help my physicians spend more time with patients and less on paperwork?" |

#### Compliance / Privacy Officer
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Project** | Regulatory Gatekeeper |
| **Known Position** | TBD -- to be identified |
| **Likely Motivations** | Maintaining compliance, reducing risk exposure, demonstrating due diligence |
| **Likely Concerns** | HIPAA violations, patient consent, AI decision transparency, audit trail, vendor BAAs |
| **Engagement Strategy** | Involve from Day 1; share compliance-by-design approach; include in vendor evaluations; provide documentation proactively; make them an ally, not a blocker |
| **Communication Style** | Detail-oriented; wants written policies and procedures; regulatory references |
| **Risk** | Could slow or block initiatives if not engaged early |
| **Key Question to Answer** | "How do we ensure every AI system meets our compliance obligations?" |

### Tier 3: Keep Informed (Lower Influence, High Interest)

#### IT Team Leads
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Project** | Implementers / Technical Partners |
| **Likely Motivations** | Skill development, interesting projects, career growth, reduce toil |
| **Likely Concerns** | Workload increase, skill gaps, job security, being sidelined by consultants |
| **Engagement Strategy** | Embedded in implementation; knowledge transfer; hands-on roles; credit their contributions publicly |
| **Key Question to Answer** | "How will I grow my skills and how does this affect my day-to-day?" |

#### Clinic Operations Managers
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Project** | Operational Champions / Adoption Leaders |
| **Likely Motivations** | Smoother operations, less firefighting, happier staff and patients |
| **Likely Concerns** | Workflow disruption, training burden, accountability for adoption metrics |
| **Engagement Strategy** | Include in design sessions; pilot clinic selection; empower as local champions; provide training resources and support |
| **Key Question to Answer** | "Will this actually make my clinic run better, or just add more work?" |

#### Front Desk / Intake Staff
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Project** | Primary End Users (Phase 1) |
| **Likely Motivations** | Easier workflows, less repetitive work, patient satisfaction |
| **Likely Concerns** | Job displacement, learning new systems, workflow disruption |
| **Engagement Strategy** | Observe their current work; involve in usability testing; communicate that AI handles tedium so they can focus on patients; generous training and go-live support |
| **Key Question to Answer** | "Am I going to lose my job, or is this going to make my job better?" |

#### Physicians / Providers
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Project** | Critical End Users (especially Phase 2+) |
| **Likely Motivations** | Less documentation time, better tools, clinical quality |
| **Likely Concerns** | Workflow disruption, AI accuracy, liability, loss of clinical autonomy |
| **Engagement Strategy** | Physician champions identified early; demonstrate time savings; human-in-the-loop for clinical AI; respect clinical judgment; pilot with early adopters |
| **Key Question to Answer** | "Does this save me time and help me take better care of patients?" |

#### Billing / Revenue Cycle Team
| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Role in Project** | End Users (Phase 1-2) |
| **Likely Motivations** | Fewer denials, faster processing, less manual work |
| **Likely Concerns** | System accuracy, workflow changes, accountability |
| **Engagement Strategy** | Include in requirements and testing; demonstrate error reduction; phased rollout with validation |

## Stakeholder Risk Indicators

Watch for these signals during the engagement:

| Signal | Indicates | Response |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Stakeholder stops attending meetings | Disengagement or disagreement | 1:1 conversation to understand concerns |
| New stakeholders introduced without context | Political dynamics or scope expansion | Brief new stakeholders; assess impact on scope |
| Requests to bypass compliance review | Schedule pressure or process fatigue | Reinforce importance; find ways to accelerate without skipping |
| Physician complaints reaching CEO directly | Adoption issues or clinical workflow problems | Immediate clinical advisory session; adjust approach |
| Budget questions increase in frequency | Financial concerns or competing priorities | Proactive ROI reporting; CFO engagement |
| IT team working overtime | Capacity problem | Discuss resource augmentation with CTO |
