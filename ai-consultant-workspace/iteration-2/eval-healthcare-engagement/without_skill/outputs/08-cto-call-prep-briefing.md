## Conversation Strategy

### Opening (set the tone)
- Express genuine interest in their specialty clinic model
- Acknowledge that the CTO role in healthcare is uniquely challenging (clinical + operational + regulatory)
- Make clear: "I'm here to listen and learn, not sell"
- Position this call as the beginning of a partnership, not a pitch

### Key Reframes to Have Ready

**If CTO says:** "The CEO wants us to use AI for everything"
**Reframe:** "That enthusiasm is great. Our job together is to channel it into the 2-3 things that will have the biggest impact fastest. What would you pick?"

**If CTO says:** "We don't have the skills for AI"
**Reframe:** "That's actually normal. The approach we take is designed to build your team's capabilities as we go. They'll own these systems long after we leave."

**If CTO says:** "We tried something like this before and it didn't work"
**Reframe:** "Tell me about that -- what happened? Understanding what didn't work is the most valuable thing for us to get right this time."

**If CTO says:** "I'm not sure we have budget for this"
**Reframe:** "Let's not worry about the total number yet. If we can show the CEO a $400K initiative that saves $800K/year, that conversation gets easier. Can we start by finding that first win?"

**If CTO says:** "Our EHR vendor says they have AI built in"
**Reframe:** "EHR-native AI is definitely part of the picture. Let's look at what they offer vs. what you actually need -- there's usually a gap, and that's where targeted solutions add the most value."

### Questions to Avoid (Too Early)
- Don't ask for detailed budget numbers -- ask about "range" or "investment appetite"
- Don't propose specific vendors or products -- it's too early and undermines trust
- Don't criticize their current tech stack -- respect what got them here
- Don't promise specific savings numbers -- save that for after discovery
- Don't discuss competitor references unless they ask

### Signals to Watch For
- **Enthusiasm vs. obligation:** Is the CTO excited or just executing the CEO's directive?
- **Technical depth:** How deep does the CTO go on infrastructure questions?
- **Openness vs. defensiveness:** Are they sharing freely or guarding information?
- **Political cues:** Who do they reference? Who do they avoid mentioning?
- **Pain indicators:** Where do they show the most energy or frustration?

## Healthcare Industry Context (Be Ready to Reference)

### Specialty Clinic AI Trends
- **Ambient clinical documentation** is the hottest area (DAX, Abridge, Suki)
- **Patient intake digitization** is table stakes -- most competitors have done it
- **Prior authorization automation** is a major pain point industry-wide
- **Revenue cycle AI** is mature and has strong ROI data
- **Clinical decision support** is growing but has regulatory complexity

### Comparable Organization Benchmarks
- Organizations this size typically spend 3-5% of revenue on IT ($2.4M-$4M for MedTech)
- AI modernization programs at similar orgs typically run $1.5M-$3M over 24 months
- First-year ROI of 150-300% is achievable with the right initiative selection
- Average healthcare organization has 15-20 distinct software applications
- EHR satisfaction in specialty practices averages 5-6/10

### Common EHR Systems in Specialty Clinics
- **Epic** -- enterprise, expensive, robust APIs, strong app marketplace
- **athenahealth** -- cloud-native, good APIs, mid-market sweet spot
- **eClinicalWorks** -- common in multi-specialty, variable integration capability
- **NextGen** -- specialty-focused, decent integration
- **Greenway / Intergy** -- specialty practices, more limited API ecosystem
- **ModMed (Modernizing Medicine)** -- specialty-specific (dermatology, orthopedics, etc.)
- **DrChrono** -- smaller/newer, API-friendly

*The EHR they use significantly impacts our integration approach and timeline.*

## Potential Red Flags to Probe

| Red Flag | Why It Matters | How to Probe |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| CTO doesn't know their AWS spend | Infrastructure may be ad hoc, not managed | "Walk me through your cloud journey -- how did you get to where you are today?" |
| No mention of HIPAA or compliance | May be a gap or may be assumed | "How does your compliance program work today?" |
| IT team described only as "support" | May lack development/engineering capability | "What does a typical week look like for your team?" |
| No data analytics or reporting | Data maturity may be very low | "How do clinic managers track their KPIs today?" |
| EHR described with frustration | Integration will be painful; may need EHR strategy | "If you could change one thing about your EHR, what would it be?" |
| CEO and CTO vision misaligned | Political risk; need to align before proceeding | "How would you describe the CEO's vision vs. your own priorities?" |
