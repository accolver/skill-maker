## Phase 1: Foundation and Quick Wins (Month 1-3)

**Goal:** Digitize core patient workflows, demonstrate immediate AI value, create structured data foundation for advanced initiatives.

### Workstreams

**1.1 Digital Patient Intake (Month 1-2)**
- Evaluate and select digital intake vendor (Phreesia, Yosi Health, Clearwave, or similar)
- Configure intake forms for specialty-specific workflows across clinic types
- Integrate with EHR for automatic patient record population
- Pilot at 1-2 clinics in Month 1, full rollout by end of Month 2
- Expected outcome: Eliminate paper forms, reduce check-in time by 50%+, enable structured data capture

**1.2 AI Patient Communication Agent (Month 1-3)**
- Evaluate and select patient engagement platform (Luma Health, Artera, Hyro, or similar)
- Configure appointment reminders (SMS, voice, email), pre-visit instructions, and post-visit follow-up
- Integrate with scheduling system and EHR
- Pilot at 2-3 clinics in Month 2, full rollout by end of Month 3
- Expected outcome: Reduce no-show rate by 10-30%, free front-desk staff from routine phone calls

**1.3 AI Governance Foundation (Month 1-3)**
- Draft and adopt an AI use policy (data handling, vendor evaluation, PHI protection)
- Establish vendor security review process aligned with HIPAA requirements
- Define AI project approval workflow (who reviews, who approves)
- Brief compliance/legal team on AI strategy and regulatory requirements
- Expected outcome: Governance framework in place before any AI touches patient data

**1.4 Data Landscape Assessment (Month 2-3)**
- Inventory all data assets: EHR, billing systems, scheduling, HR, operational databases
- Map data flows between systems and locations
- Identify data quality issues, gaps, and silos
- Document integration capabilities (APIs, exports, vendor support)
- Expected outcome: Clear picture of data maturity to inform Phase 2 planning

## Phase 2: Clinical AI Pilot and Capability Building (Month 3-6)

**Goal:** Prove clinical AI value with ambient documentation pilot, deepen data integration, begin building internal AI capability.

### Workstreams

**2.1 Ambient Clinical Documentation Pilot (Month 3-5)**
- Identify physician champion and pilot cohort (5-10 clinicians at 1-2 locations)
- Evaluate and select documentation vendor (Nuance DAX, Abridge, Nabla)
- Configure specialty-specific note templates and workflows
- Pilot for 6-8 weeks with structured feedback collection
- Measure documentation time savings, note quality, and clinician satisfaction
- Expected outcome: Validated ROI for full deployment decision

**2.2 Data Integration (Month 4-6)**
- Connect EHR, digital intake, billing, and scheduling data streams
- Establish data quality monitoring for integrated data flows
- Build foundational reporting layer across connected systems
- Expected outcome: Unified data access enabling analytics and advanced AI

**2.3 AI Governance Maturation (Month 3-6)**
- Formalize AI model/tool review and approval process
- Establish monitoring protocols for deployed AI systems
- Create incident response procedures for AI-related issues
- Train relevant staff on AI governance requirements
- Expected outcome: Governance framework mature enough to support production clinical AI

**2.4 Internal Capability Building (Month 3-6)**
- Identify 1-2 IT team members for AI upskilling (AI literacy, vendor management, data integration)
- Evaluate need for dedicated AI/data hire or advisory retainer
- Establish vendor management practices for AI tools
- Expected outcome: Internal capacity to support and manage AI systems

## Phase 3: Scale and Advanced AI (Month 6-12)

**Goal:** Scale proven AI across the organization, deploy advanced capabilities, establish sustainable AI operations.

### Workstreams

**3.1 Clinical Documentation Full Rollout (Month 6-8)**
- Expand ambient documentation to all clinicians across all locations
- Refine specialty-specific templates based on pilot learnings
- Establish support model and ongoing training
- Expected outcome: Organization-wide documentation savings

**3.2 Medical Coding AI (Month 7-10)**
- Evaluate and deploy AI-assisted medical coding (Waystar, AKASA, 3M)
- Integrate with EHR documentation and billing system
- Validate coding accuracy improvement and denial rate reduction
- Expected outcome: 30-50% reduction in coding errors, reduced claim denials

**3.3 Prior Authorization AI Agent Pilot (Month 8-12)**
- Evaluate prior auth automation vendors (Cohere Health, Infinitus)
- Pilot with highest-volume payers and procedure types
- Measure processing time reduction and approval rates
- Expected outcome: 40-60% reduction in prior auth staff time

**3.4 Operational Analytics (Month 9-12)**
- Deploy centralized analytics dashboard across all clinic locations
- Patient volume, wait times, staff utilization, revenue metrics, quality indicators
- Enable data-driven operational decisions at site and executive level
- Expected outcome: Multi-site visibility and data-driven management

### Milestones

| Milestone | Target Date | Success Criteria |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Documentation rolled out to all clinicians | Month 8 | Full adoption with support model in place |
| Coding AI deployed | Month 10 | Denial rate reduction validated |
| Prior auth pilot launched | Month 9 | Pilot with 2-3 payers active |
| Analytics dashboard live | Month 11 | All locations reporting |
| **Year 1 Review** | **Month 12** | **Full ROI assessment, Year 2 planning** |

### Investment: $285K-$680K implementation + $110K-$270K/year ongoing
### Expected Annual Return: $550K-$1.1M (incremental)

## Dependencies

### Cross-Phase Dependencies

```
Phase 1: Digital Intake  ──────>  Phase 2: Data Integration  ──────>  Phase 3: Analytics
Phase 1: Data Assessment ──────>  Phase 2: Documentation Pilot ─────>  Phase 3: Full Rollout
Phase 1: AI Governance   ──────>  Phase 2: Governance Maturation ───>  Phase 3: Coding/Auth AI
Phase 1: Communication   ──────>  (Independent -- ongoing value)
```

### External Dependencies

| Dependency | Phase Affected | Risk Level | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| EHR vendor cooperation (APIs, integration) | All phases | High | Assess in CTO call; select AI vendors with existing EHR integrations |
| Physician champion identified | Phase 2 | Medium | Begin identification in Phase 1; proceed with operational AI if none found |
| Budget approval per phase | All phases | Medium | Go/no-go gates with clear ROI evidence at each phase boundary |
| IT team capacity | All phases | Medium | Vendor/buy solutions minimize internal burden; consider staff augmentation |
| HIPAA compliance for AI vendors | Phase 1+ | Low-Medium | Include in vendor selection criteria; require BAAs |

## Risk Mitigation Summary

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Built Into Roadmap |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Budget insufficient for full roadmap | Medium | High | Phased approach with standalone value per phase; Phase 1 alone has strong ROI |
| EHR integration challenges | Medium | High | Vendor selection prioritizes EHR compatibility; Phase 1 data assessment surfaces issues early |
| Physician resistance to documentation AI | Medium | Medium | Pilot approach limits exposure; physician champion required before Phase 2 |
| IT team overwhelmed | Medium | Medium | Buy strategy minimizes internal effort; phased rollout prevents simultaneous demands |
| Data quality insufficient | Medium | Medium | Phase 1 digitization addresses root cause; data assessment identifies specific gaps |
| Vendor selection misfire | Low | Medium | Pilot-then-scale approach limits commitment; go/no-go gates enable course correction |
