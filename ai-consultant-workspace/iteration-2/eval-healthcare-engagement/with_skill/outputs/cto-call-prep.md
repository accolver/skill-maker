# CTO Call Preparation: MedTech Solutions

**Scheduled:** Next week
**Duration:** 60-90 minutes recommended
**Objective:** Validate pre-engagement hypotheses, assess technical landscape, get budget signals, and establish rapport for the engagement

---

## Call Agenda (Suggested)

| Time | Topic | Objective |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0-5 min | Introduction and framing | Set expectations for the call; confirm their goals |
| 5-15 min | Strategic context | Understand CTO's vision, priorities, and relationship to CEO's mandate |
| 15-30 min | Technical landscape | EHR, data architecture, AWS usage, integration capabilities |
| 30-45 min | Data and AI readiness | Data maturity, current analytics, any AI/automation experience |
| 45-55 min | Team, budget, and constraints | IT capacity, budget signals, timeline expectations |
| 55-65 min | Opportunity discussion | Share preliminary hypotheses, get CTO's reaction |
| 65-75 min | Next steps | Agree on stakeholder interview plan and engagement cadence |

---

## Opening Frame

"Thank you for making time for this. Before we met, I spent time researching MedTech Solutions and the specialty healthcare AI landscape so we can make the most of our conversation. I have some hypotheses about where AI could create value for you, but first I want to understand your perspective -- your priorities, your technical environment, and where you see the biggest opportunities and constraints."

This framing accomplishes three things:
1. Signals you did your homework (builds trust)
2. Positions you as a listener, not a salesperson
3. Sets up the conversation to validate your hypotheses, not start from scratch

## Question Sequence

### Block 1: Strategic Context (10 min)

**Q1:** "The CEO mentioned wanting to use AI to modernize operations. How do you interpret that vision? What does a successful AI initiative look like from your perspective?"

*Listen for:* CTO/CEO alignment, technical vs. business framing, specific pain points vs. general ambition

**Q2:** "What are your top 3 technology priorities for this year? Where does AI fit relative to those?"

*Listen for:* Whether AI is a top priority or competing with other initiatives (e.g., EHR migration, security upgrades, infrastructure modernization)

**Q3:** "What's driving the urgency to explore AI now -- competitive pressure, board direction, operational pain, or something else?"

*Listen for:* External vs. internal drivers; urgency level; whether this is reactive or proactive

### Block 2: Technical Landscape (15 min)

**Q4:** "What EHR system are you running? Is it cloud-hosted or on-prem? What version?"

*Listen for:* EHR vendor (determines integration options), deployment model, whether they're on a modern version

**Q5:** "You're running a mix of on-prem and AWS. What's your cloud strategy -- actively migrating, or is hybrid the long-term plan? What AWS services are you using today?"

*Listen for:* Cloud maturity (just EC2/S3 vs. managed services), migration timeline, technical debt

**Q6:** "How does data flow between your systems -- EHR, billing, scheduling, across clinic locations? Is there an integration layer or is it mostly point-to-point?"

*Listen for:* Integration maturity, data silos, whether cross-system data access exists

**Q7:** "Do you have a data warehouse or analytics platform, or does reporting happen directly against operational databases?"

*Listen for:* Data infrastructure maturity, analytics capability, whether anyone is doing data-driven decision-making

### Block 3: Data and AI Readiness (10 min)

**Q8:** "I noticed the patient intake process is still paper-based. How much of your other operational data is digitized and structured?"

*Listen for:* Scope of paper/manual processes beyond intake, data quality awareness, digitization in progress or planned

**Q9:** "Have you deployed any automation or AI in any form -- even informal use of ChatGPT by staff?"

*Listen for:* Any prior AI/automation experience (positive or negative), shadow AI usage, grassroots interest

**Research-informed Q:** "Specialty clinics are increasingly adopting ambient clinical documentation tools like Nuance DAX and Abridge. Has your clinical team expressed interest?"

*Listen for:* Clinical awareness of AI tools, physician interest/resistance, whether this has already been discussed

### Block 4: Team, Budget, and Constraints (10 min)

**Q10:** "How is your 12-person IT team currently allocated? What capacity exists for new projects without additional hires?"

*Listen for:* Whether the team is drowning in operations, any innovation capacity, bandwidth for AI projects

**Q11:** "Is there budget allocated for AI initiatives, or are we building the business case? What investment range is the organization comfortable with for a first initiative?"

*Listen for:* Any dollar figure or range. If they deflect, use this anchor: "For companies your size, a typical first initiative runs $50K-$150K for implementation. Does that align with what you're thinking?"

**Q12:** "Who has final approval authority for AI spend? Is it the CEO, you, a committee, or board?"

*Listen for:* Decision-making process, procurement timeline, how fast they can move

### Block 5: Opportunity Validation (10 min)

Share 2-3 preliminary hypotheses and gauge the CTO's reaction:

**Hypothesis 1:** "Based on what I've seen, digitizing patient intake seems like the obvious first step -- it's a solved problem with mature vendors, and it creates the data foundation for everything else. Does that resonate?"

**Hypothesis 2:** "Patient communication automation -- appointment reminders, pre-visit instructions, follow-up -- is another quick win that can show value in 60-90 days. Is that an area of interest?"

**Hypothesis 3:** "Longer term, ambient clinical documentation has the highest ROI potential for specialty clinics -- saving clinicians 2-3 hours a day. Is that on your radar?"

*Listen for:* Which hypotheses excite them vs. which get pushback, new opportunities they raise, political or practical barriers you didn't anticipate

### Closing: Next Steps (10 min)

**Agree on:**
1. Additional stakeholder interviews needed (recommend: CEO, operations lead, billing manager, IT lead, clinic manager, lead clinician)
2. Who facilitates introductions to these stakeholders
3. Timeline for discovery phase (typically 2-3 weeks)
4. Whether to schedule a follow-up with the CTO to review findings before the broader presentation

## Red Flags to Watch For During the Call

| Signal | What It Means | How to Respond |
| --- | --- | --- |
| CTO disagrees with CEO's AI vision | Misalignment between sponsor and implementer | Explore the gap; understand CTO's concerns; may need to facilitate alignment |
| "We tried AI before and it didn't work" | Previous failure with potential unresolved blame | Ask: "What happened? What would you do differently?" Diagnose before proceeding |
| "Budget isn't really my area" / deflection | Budget not committed or CTO not empowered | Ask to include CEO in a follow-up; don't scope without budget signals |
| CTO is technically checked out | CTO may be non-technical or disengaged | Assess whether IT lead is the real technical decision-maker |
| "Our EHR vendor doesn't support that" | Integration blocker | Probe: is it a technical limitation or a commercial one? Workarounds? |
| Resistance to sharing technical details | Trust issue or political concern | Slow down; build rapport; emphasize confidentiality |
