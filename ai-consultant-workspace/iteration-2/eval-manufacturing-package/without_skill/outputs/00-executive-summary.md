# TitanWorks Predictive Maintenance Program: Executive Summary

**Client**: TitanWorks Manufacturing (2,000 employees, $300M revenue, 4 factories)
**Date**: March 19, 2026
**Engagement**: Predictive Maintenance Strategy & Implementation Planning

---

## The Problem

TitanWorks loses **$2M per month ($24M/year)** to unplanned equipment downtime — representing **8% of annual revenue**, well above the industry benchmark of 2-5%. Only 40% of critical equipment has sensor monitoring; the rest relies on manual inspection rounds. The IT team has zero data science capability, and a previous digital transformation initiative failed 18 months ago, eroding organizational confidence.

## The Opportunity

**$12M-$15.6M per year** in downtime is addressable through predictive maintenance (50-65% of total). Additional value of $2.1M-$4.2M/year comes from reduced spare parts inventory, extended equipment life, and lower emergency overtime costs. The total addressable opportunity is **$14M-$20M per year**.

## Our Recommendation

A **hybrid build/buy approach** deployed in **three independently viable tiers**, starting with a single-factory pilot. This strategy directly addresses TitanWorks' specific constraints: zero data science capability (use proven commercial platform), failed prior initiative (prove value in 90 days before scaling), and CFO scrutiny (hard decision gates between each tier).
