# Proposal: AI-Powered Predictive Maintenance & Operational Intelligence

**Prepared for:** TitanWorks | **Date:** March 19, 2026

---

## The Challenge

TitanWorks is losing $2M every month — $24M per year — to unplanned equipment downtime across its 4 factories. When critical equipment fails unexpectedly, the consequences cascade: production lines stop, orders are delayed, maintenance teams scramble for emergency repairs, expedited parts are overnighted at premium prices, and overtime labor costs spike. Beyond the direct costs, customer confidence erodes with every missed delivery.

The current approach — monitoring 40% of critical equipment with sensors and relying on manual inspections for the rest — is not keeping pace with the complexity and demands of the operation. Experienced maintenance technicians spend more time firefighting emergencies than preventing them, and as senior technicians retire, their diagnostic expertise leaves with them.

Eighteen months ago, TitanWorks invested in a "digital transformation" initiative that did not deliver the expected results. We understand the skepticism this creates. This proposal is designed to be fundamentally different: narrower scope, measurable outcomes, faster value, and explicit go/no-go gates that put TitanWorks in control at every stage.

## Our Understanding

Based on our assessment of TitanWorks, we have identified the following key factors:

- **Quantified problem:** $2M/month in unplanned downtime provides a clear, measurable baseline against which any solution must demonstrate returns
- **Partial sensor infrastructure:** 40% of critical equipment is instrumented with sensors — enough data to start, but gaps that must be addressed over time
- **Azure foundation:** The recent migration to Azure provides a modern cloud platform that can support AI workloads with the right services activated
- **Capable but constrained IT team:** 25 enterprise IT professionals who understand the business systems but do not have data science or ML skills — a partner-enabled approach is essential
- **Enthusiastic operations leadership:** Plant managers see the problem daily and want solutions — they are natural project champions
- **Financially cautious CFO:** Appropriately demanding hard ROI evidence before committing — a discipline that will actually protect this initiative from the fate of its predecessor
- **AI maturity score of 1.55/5:** TitanWorks is in the "Foundation" stage — the right approach is targeted quick wins with a commercial platform, not a broad enterprise AI build-out

## Proposed Solution

We propose a **phased predictive maintenance and operational intelligence program** that starts small, proves value fast, and scales based on results — not projections.

### Approach

**Phase 1 (Month 1-3): Prove It Works — Single Factory Pilot**
- Deploy a commercial predictive maintenance platform on already-instrumented equipment in one factory
- Build the OT-to-Azure data pipeline that connects sensor data to the cloud
- Launch a GenAI-powered maintenance knowledge base for technician support
- Establish baseline metrics and begin measuring improvement

**Phase 2 (Month 4-9): Expand What Works**
- Retrofit sensors on the highest-priority un-instrumented equipment
- Roll out predictive maintenance to remaining 3 factories
- Enhance the knowledge base with agent capabilities
- Build internal analytics capability through training and selective hiring

**Phase 3 (Month 9-18): Scale and Differentiate**
- Deploy computer vision quality inspection on priority production lines
- Implement demand forecasting integrated with ERP
- Establish an internal AI operations function
- Evaluate advanced capabilities (digital twin, supply chain optimization)

### Why This Approach

1. **Industry-validated methodology:** Predictive maintenance consistently delivers 30-50% reduction in unplanned downtime across manufacturing (McKinsey, Deloitte, IoT Analytics). This is not experimental technology — it is proven at scale.

2. **Commercial platform, not custom build:** TitanWorks' current maturity level makes a custom ML build inappropriate. A commercial platform provides faster deployment, vendor-managed model operations, and lower risk. Internal capability builds over time.

3. **Single-factory pilot limits risk:** If Phase 1 does not deliver measurable results in 90 days, total investment is $150K-$250K — approximately 4-5 days of the current downtime cost. The downside is capped; the upside is $6M+/year.

4. **Each phase delivers standalone value:** If TitanWorks stops after Phase 1, they still have a working predictive maintenance system in one factory and a functional maintenance knowledge base. No phase depends on completing subsequent phases to be valuable.

5. **Learned from the past:** This engagement has hard success metrics defined upfront, phased investment with go/no-go gates, narrow initial scope, and the CFO as a co-architect of success criteria. These structural differences directly address what typically goes wrong in digital transformation initiatives.

## How This Is Different From Last Time

| Previous Initiative | This Engagement |
| --- | --- |
| Broad "digital transformation" scope | Single problem: unplanned downtime ($2M/month) |
| Success metrics defined loosely or after the fact | Success metrics defined before work begins (downtime hours, MTTR, cost per incident) |

| Results expected "eventually" | Measurable results within 90 days or stop |
