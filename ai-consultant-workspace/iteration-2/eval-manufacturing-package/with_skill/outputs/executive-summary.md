# Executive Summary: AI Opportunity Assessment for TitanWorks

## Situation

TitanWorks is losing $2M per month ($24M annually) to unplanned equipment downtime across its 4 factories — approximately 8% of annual revenue. Only 40% of critical equipment is monitored by sensors; the rest depends on manual inspection. With a 25-person enterprise IT team that has no data science capability, a recent Azure migration, and organizational caution following a failed digital transformation initiative 18 months ago, TitanWorks needs a focused, measurable, low-risk approach to AI adoption that delivers financial returns quickly enough to rebuild confidence in technology-led improvement.

## Key Findings

1. **AI Maturity: 1.55/5 (Foundation stage).** TitanWorks has foundational cloud infrastructure (Azure) and enthusiastic operational leadership, but significant gaps in data accessibility (fragmented OT/IT data), talent (zero AI/ML skills), and governance (no AI policies). The right approach at this maturity level is a targeted commercial platform pilot — not a broad enterprise AI build-out.

2. **Top Opportunities identified (8 total, 2 recommended for immediate action):**
   - **Predictive Maintenance** (Impact: 5, Feasibility: 4): Addresses the $24M/year downtime problem directly. Commercial platforms consistently deliver 30-50% downtime reduction. Conservative 25-30% improvement yields $6M-$7.2M annual savings. Time to value: 60-90 days.
   - **Maintenance Knowledge Base** (Impact: 3, Feasibility: 4): GenAI-powered technician assistant that reduces mean time to repair by 20-30% and preserves institutional knowledge. Estimated $1.2M-$2M/year benefit. Time to value: 30-60 days.
   - **Sensor Expansion + Factory Rollout** (Impact: 5, Feasibility: 3): Extends predictive maintenance to remaining 60% of equipment across all factories. Estimated $3M-$6M additional annual savings. Time to value: 6-9 months.

3. **Key Risks:** The failed digital transformation has created organizational skepticism. The CFO's demand for hard ROI numbers is warranted and healthy. Zero data science talent means TitanWorks must partner, not build. The OT/IT data gap is the primary technical prerequisite.

## Recommended Approach

| Phase | Scope | Timeline | Investment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Phase 1: Prove It** | Predictive maintenance pilot on instrumented equipment in 1 factory + maintenance knowledge base MVP | Month 1-3 | $175K - $250K |
| **Phase 2: Expand** | Sensor expansion + 4-factory rollout + enhanced knowledge base + team capability building | Month 4-9 | $475K - $850K |
| **Phase 3: Scale** | Computer vision quality inspection + demand forecasting + internal AI operations team | Month 9-18 | $550K - $900K |

Each phase delivers standalone value. Go/no-go decisions at each gate. No phase requires commitment to subsequent phases.
