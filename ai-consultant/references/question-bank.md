# Stakeholder Question Bank

Role-specific questions for discovery interviews. Select 8-12 questions per
interview — don't ask all of them. Adapt based on what you learn from prior
interviews.

## Executive Sponsor (CEO / COO / CTO)

**Strategic context:**
- What are your top 3 business priorities for this year?
- Where does AI fit in your strategic plan?
- What would a successful AI initiative look like in 12 months?
- What's driving the urgency to explore AI now?

**Budget and commitment:**
- Is there budget allocated for AI initiatives, or are we building the case?
- What's the investment range you're comfortable with for a first initiative?
- Who has approval authority for AI spend?

**Risk and expectations:**
- What concerns you most about AI adoption?
- Have you had previous AI/ML initiatives? What happened?
- How do you define ROI for technology investments?
- What's your timeline expectation for seeing results?

**Organizational:**
- Who are the key stakeholders who need to be involved?
- Are there any internal politics or dynamics I should be aware of?
- How does the organization typically handle technology change?

## Department Heads (Operations, Sales, Customer Service, Finance)

**Pain points:**
- What are the most time-consuming manual processes in your department?
- Where do errors or quality issues most frequently occur?
- What decisions do your team members make repeatedly that could benefit from better data?
- What information do you wish you had but currently don't?

**Current state:**
- Walk me through a typical day/week for your team
- What tools and systems does your team use daily?
- Where does your team spend time on work that feels repetitive or low-value?
- How do you currently measure team performance?

**AI awareness and existing tools:**
- Has your team experimented with any AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, etc.)?
- Are any enterprise AI tools licensed for your department (Copilot, Gemini, Salesforce Einstein, etc.)? If so, what's the actual adoption rate?
- What AI opportunities has your team discussed?
- What would you automate first if you could?
- Are there industry competitors doing something with AI that caught your attention?

**Data and processes:**
- What data does your department generate or consume?
- How do you share information with other departments?
- What reports or analyses do you produce regularly?
- Are there seasonal patterns or peak periods that stress your current processes?

## IT / Data Team (CTO, VP Engineering, Data Engineers)

**Infrastructure:**
- What's your current cloud footprint? (AWS, Azure, GCP, on-prem)
- Describe your data architecture — where does data live and how does it flow?
- What's your API strategy? REST, GraphQL, event-driven?
- Do you have CI/CD pipelines? Container orchestration?

**Data readiness:**
- Do you have a data warehouse or data lake?
- What's the state of your data quality? Any known issues?
- Is there a data catalog or metadata management system?
- How do teams currently access data for analytics?
- What data sources are siloed or hard to access?

**ML/AI infrastructure and existing AI tools:**
- Have you deployed any ML models to production? What's the workflow?
- Do you have experiment tracking, model registry, or feature stores?
- What GPU or specialized compute is available?
- How would you serve a real-time ML model today?
- What enterprise AI tools are currently licensed? (Microsoft Copilot, GitHub Copilot, Google Gemini/Duet AI, ChatGPT Enterprise, Amazon Q, Salesforce Einstein, etc.)
- What's the actual utilization rate of those licenses? Who's using them and who isn't?
- Are employees using AI tools outside of IT's purview (shadow AI)? What tools?
- Has anyone assessed whether existing AI licenses could be expanded to cover more use cases?

**Security and compliance:**
- What are your data security requirements?
- How do you handle PII and sensitive data?
- What compliance frameworks do you operate under? (SOC2, HIPAA, etc.)
- What's your approach to third-party vendor security review?
- Are there data residency requirements?

**Integration:**
- What are the critical systems that any AI solution would need to integrate with?
- What's the typical integration timeline for new tools?
- Are there any systems that are particularly difficult to integrate with?
- What's your approach to API versioning and backward compatibility?

## Frontline Workers (Individual Contributors, Team Leads)

**Daily workflow:**
- Walk me through your typical workday — what tasks take the most time?
- What parts of your job feel repetitive or tedious?
- Where do you most often get stuck waiting for information or approvals?
- What tools do you use most? Which do you find frustrating?

**Pain points:**
- What's the most annoying part of your job that you think technology could fix?
- Where do you see errors or quality issues most often?
- What information do you frequently have to look up or ask someone about?
- Are there tasks where you feel you're doing something a computer should be doing?

**AI attitudes:**
- Have you used any AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, Grammarly, etc.)?
- What excites you about AI? What concerns you?
- If AI could help with one thing in your daily work, what would it be?
- Would you be willing to learn new tools if they saved you significant time?

**Process knowledge:**
- Are there undocumented processes or "tribal knowledge" that only certain people know?
- What happens when someone is out sick — what breaks or slows down?
- Where do handoffs between people or teams cause problems?
- What quality checks or reviews do you perform manually?

## Interview Tips

**Preparation:**
- Review the company briefing before every interview
- Tailor question selection based on what you already know
- Prepare 1-2 company-specific questions showing you've done your research

**During the interview:**
- Listen for the pain behind the answer, not just the stated problem
- Ask "why" 3 times to get to root causes
- Note the emotional intensity — strong frustration signals high-value opportunities
- Capture specific numbers when possible ("How many hours per week?", "How many errors per month?")
- Watch for contradictions between what execs say and what frontline workers describe

**After the interview:**
- Document findings within 24 hours while fresh
- Cross-reference with other interviews for patterns
- Update the opportunity list immediately
- Flag any new red flags discovered
