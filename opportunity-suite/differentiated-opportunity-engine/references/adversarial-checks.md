# Adversarial Checks

Use this reference when the user's request feels glamorous, over-constrained, or
legally sensitive.

## 1. Preference conflict

If the user says they want `micro-SaaS`, `passive income`, or `content`, do not
treat that as the answer.

Ask:

- Does the user actually have the distribution, buyer access, or workflow
  repetition to support that path?
- Is there a less glamorous path that is more credible right now?
- Would a service, diagnostic, partnership, or edge-building phase materially
  improve the odds?

If the user's preferred path is weak, say so directly.

## 2. Impossible constraint bundles

Watch for combinations like:

- No audience
- No sales
- No service work
- No content creation
- Very little time
- Very little money
- Fast revenue requirement

When these pile up, the correct answer may be `conditional` or `no_go`.

Do not invent a magical AI business to satisfy every constraint at once.

## 3. Content exception

Do not over-correct against content.

If the user's strongest edge is audience trust, community access, or direct
distribution, then a content-led or community-led business can be correct.

When content is the best path, require:

- A real audience or trusted distribution wedge
- Monetization beyond vague future sponsorships
- A product, data, community, or lead-capture mechanism tied to that audience

## 4. Confidentiality and employer conflict

Domain knowledge is not the same as permission to use:

- Employer data
- Customer data
- Internal pricing or process docs
- Confidential roadmaps
- Proprietary prompts or templates

Screen for:

- IP ownership issues
- Non-compete or non-solicit risk if relevant
- Regulated data handling
- Whether the user can legally contact or monetize the obvious buyers

If the answer is unclear, downgrade the opportunity or flag it as requiring
legal review.

## 5. Regulated and licensed work

In legal, compliance, healthcare, finance, tax, immigration, and similar spaces:

- Separate operations support from licensed advice
- Keep humans in the loop for substantive outputs
- Avoid claims that imply authority the user does not legally hold
- Prefer workflow, documentation, evidence, or preparation support over
  pretending to automate professional judgment

## 6. Numeric validation discipline

Validation plans should include numbers whenever possible:

- Conversation count
- Pilot target count
- Time window
- Revenue or conversion target
- Stop or pivot threshold

`Talk to some users` is too weak.

## 7. Rejected paths

For the chosen best bet, name at least one tempting path that loses and why.

Common examples:

- Generic AI wrapper
- Pure content play
- Software-first build
- Broad consulting offer
- Illegal or non-compliant shortcut
