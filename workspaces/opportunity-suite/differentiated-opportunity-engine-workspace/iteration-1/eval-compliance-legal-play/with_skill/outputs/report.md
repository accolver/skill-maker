## Best Bet

The best near-term path is the hybrid ladder:
`productized compliance operations retainer -> internal automation -> lightweight client portal`.

Why this is the best bet:

- It fits your time budget better than custom consulting because the work can be
  narrowly scoped and standardized.
- It gets to revenue faster than software or template-product plays.
- It uses AI in a meaningful way: drafting recurring artifacts, summarizing
  source documents, organizing evidence, and generating task reminders and
  status outputs.
- It does not require becoming a public content creator because early
  distribution can come from warm contacts and referrals.
- It creates an asset later. Even if you never build full SaaS, the internal
  operating system, templates, prompts, checklists, and workflows become a
  defensible process asset.

Pressure test:

- Demand clarity: Strong. Small firms reliably feel pain around recurring
  compliance admin, exam prep, and documentation upkeep.
- Distribution fit: Strong enough. Warm contacts are a credible wedge for first
  pilots.
- Execution difficulty: Moderate. The main challenge is tightly constraining
  scope so it fits 8 to 10 hours a week.
- Compliance and platform risk: Manageable if you position this as compliance
  operations support, workflow management, and documentation assistance rather
  than legal advice; require client review and approval for substantive outputs.
- Service-first before software: Yes. This should absolutely start as a
  service-first wedge. Building software first would slow learning and increase
  risk.

Recommended offer structure:

- Offer: Monthly "Compliance Ops Support" retainer.
- Buyer: Small financial firms with lean internal teams.
- Deliverables: Compliance calendar management, evidence collection workflow,
  draft policy update redlines, vendor review summaries, mock exam prep packet
  assembly, recurring task tracking.
- Pricing approach: Fixed monthly retainer for a narrow scope, plus a one-time
  onboarding/setup fee.
- Delivery rule: AI drafts, humans approve. No autonomous filing, no
  unsupervised advice, no claims of legal review unless performed by qualified
  counsel.

## 7-Day Validation Plan

Day 1:

- Write a one-page offer sheet with the exact scope, exclusions, turnaround
  times, and positioning language.
- Draft a simple disclaimer stating that the service supports compliance
  operations and documentation workflows, not legal advice.

Day 2:

- Make a list of 15 warm contacts and adjacent referrers.
- Send 5 short outreach messages asking for a 20-minute conversation about
  recurring compliance operations pain, not a product pitch.

Day 3:

- Run 2 to 3 discovery calls.
- Ask what tasks repeatedly slip, what prep work is most painful before reviews,
  and what internal work they wish could be delegated safely.

Day 4:

- Convert the top pain point into a fixed-scope pilot offer.
- Example pilot: "30-day compliance ops cleanup and recurring-task setup" with
  clearly bounded deliverables.

Day 5:

- Build the concierge backend using simple tools: intake form, task tracker,
  document checklist, AI-assisted summary prompts, and a status update template.
- Prepare 2 sample outputs you can show privately: a mock evidence checklist and
  a mock remediation summary.

Day 6:

- Ask for the pilot sale from the best-fit contact.
- If direct sale feels too early, ask for a paid diagnostic or readiness sprint
  as the first transaction.

Day 7:

- Review signals.
- Decide whether to continue, tighten the niche, or pivot to the mock audit
  sprint if buyers prefer one-time work over a retainer.

## Build Spec

Initial build target: an internal operator console, not public SaaS.

- Core workflow:
  1. Client intake form captures firm type, recurring obligations, upcoming
     review dates, current tools, and document locations.
  2. Backend task board maps obligations into recurring tasks and owners.
  3. AI layer drafts summaries, remediation notes, document-request lists, and
     status updates.
  4. Evidence vault links each task to proof artifacts and completion notes.
  5. Client-facing output is a simple shared status view or monthly packet, not
     a fully self-serve app.

- Minimum features:
  1. Client profile and obligations registry.
  2. Recurring compliance calendar.
  3. Evidence checklist by obligation.
  4. Prompt library for summaries, policy redlines, and exam-prep packets.
  5. Monthly status report generator.

- AI acceleration points:
  1. Summarize long policies, notices, or vendor documents.
  2. Draft first-pass issue logs and remediation checklists.
  3. Convert notes from calls into follow-up tasks and status updates.
  4. Compare prior and current policy drafts to highlight changes for client
     review.

- Guardrails:
  1. Human review before any client-facing substantive recommendation.
  2. Clear separation between operational support and legal interpretation.
  3. Do not ingest or retain sensitive client data beyond what is necessary for
     the workflow.
  4. Use tools and storage with security and confidentiality controls
     appropriate for regulated clients.

- Build order:
  1. Spreadsheet or Airtable plus secure document storage.
  2. Lightweight automation for reminders, summaries, and status packets.
  3. Only after repeated use: a narrow portal for client task status and
     document requests.

## Kill Criteria

- Stop or pivot if fewer than 3 of the first 10 warm contacts confirm this is a
  painful, budgeted problem.
- Stop the retainer offer if no one will pay for a fixed-scope pilot or
  diagnostic within 14 days.
- Pivot to one-time audit/remediation sprints if buyers like the expertise but
  resist ongoing retainers.
- Avoid building client-facing software until at least 2 paying clients
  repeatedly request the same workflow and deliverables.
- Abandon any version of the offer that requires you to act like outside
  counsel, handle filings you are not licensed to perform, or assume regulatory
  liability beyond an operational support role.
