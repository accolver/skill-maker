# Best Bet

The best path is the hybrid ladder:
`revenue recovery concierge -> internal tooling -> micro-SaaS` for small HVAC
service businesses.

Why this is the best near-term path:

- It gives you the fastest route to cash because owners will pay for booked jobs
  and recovered estimates sooner than they will pay for a standalone app.
- It compounds into software because the same workflow repeats across shops:
  missed calls, unsold estimates, stale follow-up, and service agreement
  renewals.
- It uses your actual edge: HVAC workflow familiarity plus warm access to owners
  through your brother.
- It avoids the biggest early mistake: building a micro-SaaS before you know
  which workflow pain is painful enough to buy.

Recommended initial offer:

- A fixed-fee or performance-linked "revenue recovery system" for HVAC owners.
- Scope: missed-call text-back, estimate follow-up sequences, call summaries,
  lead tagging, simple dashboarding, and weekly owner review.
- Promise: recover lost jobs from leads they already paid to acquire.

Pressure test:

- Demand clarity: Strong. Owners already understand missed calls and unsold
  estimates as expensive problems.
- Distribution fit: Strong. Your brother can open warm conversations with local
  owners.
- Execution difficulty: Moderate. You can start manually with lightweight
  tooling before deep integrations.
- Compliance and platform risk: Manageable if you use legitimate SMS/email
  consent practices and avoid overclaiming AI autonomy.
- Should service come before software: Yes. Strongly yes. The service-first
  wedge improves learning speed, gets you paid, and reveals which one or two
  workflows deserve productization.

Business model progression:

- Phase 1: Done-for-you setup plus monthly management.
- Phase 2: Semi-productized internal operator console and templates.
- Phase 3: Narrow self-serve or light-touch SaaS for one proven workflow, likely
  estimate follow-up or missed-call recovery.

# 7-Day Validation Plan

## Day 1

- Write a one-sentence offer: "We help HVAC shops recover lost revenue from
  missed calls and unsold estimates using fast follow-up automation and weekly
  pipeline cleanup."
- Define one concrete outcome metric: booked jobs recovered, estimates
  reactivated, or maintenance memberships renewed.
- Ask your brother for a list of 8-12 owners he can introduce you to.

## Day 2

- Conduct 3 short interviews with owners.
- Ask only practical questions: where leads get lost, how estimates are followed
  up, how often calls are missed, and what systems they already use.
- Look for repeated pain and current workarounds.

## Day 3

- Turn the strongest pain into a simple paid pilot offer.
- Create a one-page pilot outline with scope, expected result, timeline, and
  price.
- Target a low-friction pilot price, such as a setup fee plus one month, rather
  than a long contract.

## Day 4

- Run 3-5 more owner conversations using the pilot offer.
- Try to close 1 pilot or at least a clear next-step meeting with access to real
  workflow data.
- Note objections carefully, especially around CRM access, texting, and staff
  adoption.

## Day 5

- Build the lightest possible delivery stack for one pilot.
- Examples: Twilio or existing SMS tool, AI-generated call summaries, estimate
  follow-up drafts, status tracking in Airtable or a simple internal app.
- Keep humans in the loop.

## Day 6

- Launch the concierge pilot for one shop or run a realistic mock workflow using
  your brother's business.
- Measure baseline and first response metrics: leads contacted, responses,
  bookings, reopened estimates.

## Day 7

- Review results and patterns.
- Decide which workflow is strongest for productization.
- If one workflow clearly wins, outline a 30-day productized service and the
  first thin software layer.

Success signals by day 7:

- At least 5 owner conversations completed.
- At least 2 owners say the pain is urgent and budget-worthy.
- At least 1 pilot verbally committed or actively scoped.

# Build Spec

## Initial offer to build

- Product name placeholder: HVAC Revenue Recovery System.
- First workflow to support: estimate follow-up and missed-call recovery.
- Target buyer: owner of a small HVAC service business with weak follow-up
  discipline and no dedicated ops lead.

## Concierge MVP

- Intake: export open estimates, missed calls, and recent leads.
- AI layer: summarize calls, draft follow-ups, classify lead status, suggest
  next action.
- Operator layer: human review before messages go out.
- Reporting: weekly recovered revenue report with simple counts and dollar
  value.

## Thin internal tool before SaaS

- Contact and job pipeline view.
- Queue of leads needing follow-up.
- AI-generated follow-up suggestions with approve/send workflow.
- Outcome tracking by lead source, job type, and status.
- Basic client-specific templates for service agreement renewal, estimate
  nudges, and missed-call text-back.

## Eventual micro-SaaS wedge

- Start with one narrow module, not a full HVAC CRM.
- Best candidate: estimate follow-up copilot with pipeline visibility,
  reminders, message drafting, and close-rate reporting.
- Positioning: recover more sold work from the quotes you already produced.

## Recommended pricing path

- Phase 1 service: setup fee plus monthly fee, or pilot fee plus performance
  bonus.
- Phase 2 productized service: standardized monthly package.
- Phase 3 software: low monthly SaaS fee with optional managed add-on.

## What not to build first

- Do not build a full dispatch system.
- Do not build generic chatbots for websites.
- Do not build broad "AI for contractors" software.
- Do not spend weeks integrating every HVAC stack before one paid pilot exists.

# Kill Criteria

- Kill or pivot this path if you cannot get at least 5 warm owner conversations
  within 2 weeks despite your brother's network.
- Kill or pivot if owners agree the problem exists but will not pay even for a
  low-risk pilot.
- Kill or narrow the scope if every shop uses a different stack and integration
  burden dominates the value.
- Kill or pivot away from missed-call recovery specifically if estimate
  follow-up or membership renewals prove much more urgent in interviews.
- Kill the SaaS step for now if the service delivers value only through heavy
  manual customization that does not repeat across accounts.
- Continue only if you see one repeated workflow, one clear buyer, and one
  measurable ROI story.
