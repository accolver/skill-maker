# Best Bet

You should not start with a standalone product if first revenue in 30 days is
non-negotiable.

The honest answer is: under your stated constraints, a product-first path is
unrealistic. With 3 hours a week, no outbound, no content, and no appetite for
client work, you have removed the three usual ways a solo operator gets early
customers. Your domain edge is real, but your distribution engine is effectively
turned off.

The only plausible near-term path is a very small hybrid:

- sell one warm-intro concierge pilot around a narrow distributor ERP pain point
- use AI to do most of the delivery manually behind the scenes
- productize only after one buyer pays and repeats the need

The best narrow wedge is
`exception triage / SOP retrieval / recurring ops issue resolution` for
distributors because:

- it is painful and frequent
- you likely know the workflow already
- AI can genuinely reduce time-to-resolution
- the first version can be delivered manually with almost no build

If you refuse even that amount of selling and customer-specific work, then yes,
you are kidding yourself about 30-day revenue from AI. In that case, optimize
for a slower asset-later path and stop pretending it is a fast-income plan.

# 7-Day Validation Plan

Day 1:

- Write a 1-page offer for one narrow problem: for example, "I help distributor
  ops teams cut ERP exception handling time for X workflow using a lightweight
  AI-assisted process."
- Define one paid pilot outcome, one workflow boundary, one price, and one
  deliverable.

Day 2:

- Make a list of 10 warm contacts only: ex-clients, ERP consultants, VARs,
  implementation managers, distributor operators.
- Do not do cold outreach. Ask for 3 introductions or 3 direct reactions.

Day 3:

- Send a plain message offering a 20-minute call to pressure-test the pain and
  pilot.
- Goal: get 3 conversations, not 30.

Day 4:

- In calls, validate one repeated workflow pain, current manual workaround,
  volume, and cost of delay.
- Ask directly whether they would pay for a 2-week pilot.

Day 5:

- If at least one buyer leans in, mock the workflow manually with AI tools you
  already have.
- Produce a sample output using fake or sanitized data.

Day 6:

- Ask for a paid pilot with a fixed price and fixed scope.
- Keep scope tiny enough to fit your 3-hour weekly limit.

Day 7:

- Decide.
- If you get one paid pilot or one very strong partner-sponsored test, continue.
- If you get polite interest but no concrete next step, do not build product
  yet.

# Build Spec

- Offer shape: one narrow concierge workflow, not a platform.
- Delivery model: manual-first with AI assistance.
- Suggested first workflow: exception triage, SOP lookup, ticket summarization,
  or data-mapping assistance.
- Inputs: exported tickets, SOP docs, sample transactions, issue logs.
- Outputs: prioritized exception summaries, suggested next actions, linked SOP
  answers, escalation flags.
- Minimal stack: existing LLM, prompt templates, spreadsheet or Airtable, simple
  ingestion script, optional lightweight UI only after pilot demand.
- Success metric: measurable time saved per issue or reduced resolution lag.
- Packaging rule: no custom software until a repeated workflow appears across at
  least 2 buyers or 1 partner channel.

# Kill Criteria

- No access to at least 3 warm conversations in 7 days.
- No buyer agrees the chosen workflow is painful, frequent, and worth paying to
  reduce.
- No willingness from a buyer or partner to run a paid pilot in the next 30
  days.
- The workflow requires deep system integration before value can be shown.
- The time needed to deliver the pilot exceeds your 3-hour weekly limit.
- You still refuse any direct selling or customer-specific delivery after
  validation; if so, stop pursuing fast revenue and treat this as a longer-term
  product build only.
