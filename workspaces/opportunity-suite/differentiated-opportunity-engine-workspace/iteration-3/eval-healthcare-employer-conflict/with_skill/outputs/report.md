# Differentiation Profile

- Skills and domain experience: Staff engineer in healthcare claims with direct
  operational knowledge of where provider groups lose money on denials and prior
  authorization; likely strong workflow, data, automation, and systems thinking.
- Assets and access advantages: Deep category fluency in denial and prior-auth
  economics, payer-policy friction, and workflow failure modes.
- Distribution and network leverage: Weak for immediate monetization under
  current constraints because you cannot use employer customers, do not want
  consulting, and do not want sales calls.
- Constraints: No PHI, no employer customer contact, no consulting, no sales
  calls, about 4 hours per week, must be fully legal.
- Credibility signals: Seniority in a hard operational niche; firsthand
  knowledge of expensive reimbursement failure patterns.
- Unfair advantages: Better problem selection than a generic AI builder, but
  only where the business can be built from public information, synthetic
  examples, user-provided non-PHI inputs, and channels unrelated to your
  employer.

## Feasibility

`feasibility_verdict: conditional`

This is legal and plausible, but not as a fast cash consulting business. Your
strongest insight is real, but much of the obvious monetization path is blocked
by confidentiality, employer-conflict, and distribution constraints. The viable
path is an async, self-serve product built on public payer rules, synthetic
examples, and workflow support rather than clinical or legal judgment.

Binding constraints:

- You cannot use PHI or any employer/customer-confidential data.
- You should avoid any overlap with your employer's customers, internal
  materials, or proprietary workflows unless counsel says otherwise.
- No consulting and no sales calls remove the easiest near-term revenue path.
- Four hours per week means the offer must be narrow, async, and low-ops.

# Best Bet

Best bet: **Start with an async denial/pre-auth workflow kit aimed at
independent RCM consultants and small outsourced billing teams, then productize
only if async demand appears.**

What to sell first:

- A paid pack of denial reason maps, document-gathering checklists, prior-auth
  packet checklists, payer-policy research prompts, root-cause review
  worksheets, and synthetic worked examples.
- Optional add-on: a tiny web app that turns a denial code/reason plus payer
  name plus specialty into a recommended evidence checklist and policy-research
  path, with an explicit human-review disclaimer.

Narrow ICP:

- Independent RCM consultants and 2-20 person billing shops serving outpatient
  specialties.

One measurable pain/outcome metric:

- Reduce time to prepare a clean appeal or prior-auth packet from roughly hours
  of manual policy lookup to less than 20 minutes of guided prep.

Why this beats the obvious alternatives:

- It beats consulting because you do not want service delivery or calls.
- It beats software-first because with 4 hours/week and no distribution, a full
  app before paid demand is too slow.
- It beats a broad healthcare AI product because your legal edge is narrow:
  workflow support from public rules, not broad automation over sensitive data.
- It beats pure content because content alone is unlikely to monetize quickly
  without an audience.

What would change the recommendation:

- If you become willing to do a few async pilots with non-overlapping buyers,
  the hybrid path becomes much stronger.
- If you gain a partner channel, the subscription/data product gets more
  attractive.
- If your employment agreement clearly permits an adjacent product and you can
  verify non-overlap, you can widen the buyer set.

# 7-Day Validation Plan

1. Review your employment agreement and any moonlighting/IP/conflict language
   for 45 minutes. Success target: identify whether there are explicit
   restrictions on adjacent healthcare software, templates, or outside business
   activity.
2. Define one narrow wedge. Success target: choose exactly one specialty + one
   workflow, for example cardiology prior-auth packet prep or orthopedic denial
   appeal prep.
3. Build a minimal paid offer page and checkout. Success target: one landing
   page, one sample artifact, one price point between $99 and $299, one clear
   disclaimer that no PHI should be uploaded and outputs require human review.
4. Produce 3 sample assets using only public materials and synthetic data.
   Success target: one checklist, one policy-research prompt, one worked
   synthetic example.
5. Run async demand tests in non-employer channels. Success target: post in 3
   relevant communities or directories and send 20 async messages or emails to
   independent RCM operators, consultants, or billing-shop owners with no sales
   calls offered.
6. Measure willingness to pay. Success target: at least 5 meaningful replies and
   either 2 preorders or 1 paid pilot within 7 days.
7. If demand appears, ship the tiniest tool. Success target: a simple form-based
   workflow assistant that outputs a denial/appeal evidence checklist without
   storing PHI.

# Build Spec

- Product scope: Async workflow support for denial and prior-auth prep, not
  medical necessity advice, legal advice, payer representation, or automated
  adjudication.
- Inputs: payer name, denial reason category, specialty, procedure category,
  site of service, and other non-PHI structured fields.
- Data sources: public payer policies, CMS guidance, NCCI edits, LCDs/NCDs, fee
  schedules, and user-provided non-confidential workflow metadata.
- Outputs: evidence checklist, missing-document checklist, policy lookup links,
  draft issue tree, and a human-review note.
- Compliance boundaries:
  - No PHI collection in v1.
  - No employer/customer data.
  - No claims of guaranteed reimbursement outcomes.
  - Human review required before use.
- AI use: summarize public policy documents, map denial categories to likely
  documentation gaps, and generate structured checklists.
- Suggested implementation:
  - Static landing page
  - Stripe or Lemon Squeezy checkout
  - Notion/PDF/Google Doc asset delivery for v1
  - Small form app later using user-entered non-PHI fields
- Productization trigger: only build the app after either 5 paid sales of the
  kit or 3 repeat buyers asking for faster guided workflow.

# Kill Criteria

- Stop if your employment terms or counsel review suggest meaningful IP,
  moonlighting, non-solicit, or adjacency risk.
- Stop if you cannot define a version that uses only public data and non-PHI
  inputs.
- Stop if 20 async outreaches plus 3 community posts produce fewer than 5
  meaningful replies in 7 days.
- Stop if you get zero preorders and zero paid pilots at a $99-$299 entry price
  after the first validation week.
- Stop product build if buyers mainly ask for bespoke consulting, payer calls,
  or direct appeal handling rather than self-serve workflow support.
- Pivot distribution if interest is real but direct async channels underperform;
  in that case pursue reseller or partner channels instead of more product work.
