# Differentiation Profile

- Skills and domain experience: Former fintech compliance operator with direct
  experience helping teams prepare regulator responses, manage compliance
  workflows, and translate regulatory asks into internal action.
- Assets and access advantages: Practical knowledge of how small firms struggle
  with evidence gathering, draft preparation, and deadline management during
  supervisory or regulator interactions.
- Distribution and network leverage: A few warm contacts in fintech and adjacent
  compliance circles provide a credible starting channel for discovery calls and
  pilot offers.
- Constraints: The offer must stay on the right side of regulated-role
  boundaries, cannot imply delegated legal or compliance authority, and should
  avoid relying on confidential employer data or proprietary materials.
- Credibility signals: Real operator background in a painful, high-stakes
  workflow that buyers already spend money on.
- Unfair advantages: Better taste than a generic AI builder on what regulator
  responses actually need: issue framing, evidence trails, escalation triggers,
  and review discipline.

Feasibility verdict: `conditional`

Binding constraints:

- Do not market this as a replacement for a designated compliance officer, MLRO,
  general counsel, or licensed advisor.
- Keep a human decision-maker in the loop for all substantive interpretations,
  approvals, and final submissions.
- Use the product for drafting, evidence collection, issue tracking, and
  workflow orchestration, not legal conclusions or formal sign-off.
- Avoid reusing confidential employer/customer templates or regulator
  correspondence unless you have clear rights to do so.

# Best Bet

Best bet: `Response prep concierge -> evidence workflow automation -> product`

Why this beats the obvious alternative:

- It beats `software first` because you do not yet know the most repeatable
  regulator-response sub-workflow, and small firms will buy trusted help faster
  than unproven software.
- It beats `lightweight outsourced compliance officer` positioning because that
  framing invites legal and regulatory overreach. The safer sell is workflow
  support, drafting assistance, and exam-readiness operations with explicit
  client approval.
- It beats `pure content` because you have warm buyer access and domain
  knowledge, but no stated audience moat.

Concrete first offer:

- Offer: `48-hour regulator response prep sprint`
- ICP: Seed to Series B fintechs, EMIs, broker-dealers, crypto/compliance-heavy
  startups, or small regulated financial firms with no deep in-house compliance
  operations bench.
- Measurable pain/outcome metric: Reduce time-to-first-draft from several days
  of internal scramble to 24-48 hours, with a complete evidence request list,
  draft response structure, and owner tracker.
- Productization trigger: After 3 paid pilots, if at least 60% of the workflow
  is the same across engagements and at least 2 clients ask for ongoing
  readiness tooling, build the reusable workspace.

Safe positioning:

- Say: `AI-assisted compliance operations support`,
  `regulator response preparation`, `evidence collection`,
  `drafting assistance`, `review workflow`.
- Do not say: `outsourced compliance officer`, `automated legal advice`,
  `fully handles regulator responses`, `acts as your compliance function`.
- Add a standing rule that final outputs require named client review and
  approval, and route substantive interpretation to the client's officer,
  consultant, or counsel.

# 7-Day Validation Plan

1. Write a one-page offer memo and landing page for the
   `48-hour regulator response prep sprint` with the explicit human-review
   boundary. Success target: completed by day 1.
2. Contact 12 warm or warm-adjacent prospects in small fintech/compliance
   circles. Success target: 6 replies and 4 calls booked within 7 days.
3. On those 4 calls, test three urgent scenarios: live regulator request, exam
   readiness, and remediation follow-up. Success target: at least 3 of 4 confirm
   this is painful, urgent, and currently messy.
4. Ask each prospect whether they would pay for a scoped pilot priced at
   $2k-$5k. Success target: at least 2 prospects say yes to a paid pilot or
   serious procurement discussion.
5. Build one manual demo pack using synthetic or fully sanitized example
   materials: intake form, evidence checklist, draft outline, escalation rules,
   and approval step. Success target: demo shown in all 4 calls.
6. Validate repeatability by mapping the workflow into 6-10 standard steps and
   tagging which steps AI can assist. Success target: at least 60% of steps
   appear reusable across 3 conversations.
7. Identify one compliance advisor or law firm partner for edge cases. Success
   target: 1 referral or partnership conversation scheduled by day 7.

# Build Spec

- Product shape: A lightweight workspace, not an autonomous compliance agent.
- Core modules:
- Request intake form that classifies the incoming regulator ask by type,
  deadline, entities involved, and evidence needed.
- Evidence tracker with owners, due dates, missing-item flags, and source links.
- Drafting assistant that proposes response outlines and first-pass language
  from approved source materials.
- Review controls requiring human sign-off before export.
- Issue log and remediation tracker for follow-up items.
- Red-flag escalation rules for anything requiring legal interpretation, SAR/AML
  judgment, licensing questions, or formal officer approval.
- AI boundaries:
- Use AI for summarization, drafting, gap detection, and checklist generation.
- Do not let AI make final assertions of compliance, choose legal positions, or
  submit on the client's behalf.
- Data boundaries:
- Start with secure document handling, retention limits, and explicit client
  consent.
- Do not train on client materials by default.
- MVP stack:
- Secure form intake, document store, LLM drafting layer, task tracker, approval
  workflow, and exportable response packet.

# Kill Criteria

- If fewer than 4 of 12 outreach messages get any reply in 7 days, the
  warm-channel demand is weaker than assumed.
- If fewer than 2 of 4 discovery calls confirm urgent willingness to pay, do not
  build software yet.
- If all interested buyers actually want a named compliance officer or legal
  sign-off, kill this positioning because it pushes you into regulated-role
  overreach.
- If the workflow differs so much across prospects that less than 40% is
  reusable, avoid SaaS and keep it as bespoke consulting or drop it.
- If secure data handling, insurance, or review obligations make delivery
  operationally heavy before first revenue, narrow the offer further to
  exam-readiness documentation support instead.

Rejected path:

- `Lightweight outsourced compliance officer for small firms`: rejected because
  it implies delegated regulated authority and ongoing professional judgment
  that should remain with the client's designated officer, licensed advisor, or
  counsel.
