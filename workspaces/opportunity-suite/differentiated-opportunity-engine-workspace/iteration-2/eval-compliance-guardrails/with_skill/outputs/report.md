# Differentiation Profile

- Skills and domain experience: Former compliance consultant for small financial
  firms; strong knowledge of compliance workflows, exam prep, policy/process
  gaps, documentation expectations, and buyer language in a regulated niche.
- Assets and access advantages: A few warm contacts in the target market;
  pattern recognition around recurring compliance pain points; ability to spot
  where AI can speed document review, evidence gathering, issue logging, and
  draft preparation.
- Distribution and network leverage: Warm introductions are enough to test a
  narrow offer without building an audience. This is materially better than a
  cold-start founder with generic AI skills.
- Constraints: Only 8 to 10 hours per week; almost no desire to become a content
  creator; wants to stay fully legal; likely cannot afford a long software build
  before validation.
- Credibility signals: Prior direct work in the niche; understands the
  operational reality of small firms rather than speaking in generic AI terms.
- Unfair advantages: The combination of compliance domain fluency, warm access,
  and a strong aversion to hype pushes toward a narrow, trust-based offer that
  uses AI behind the scenes rather than selling “AI” as the product.

## Feasibility Screen

- Feasibility verdict: `conditional`
- Why: The opportunity is credible only if it is framed as compliance operations
  support, documentation prep, evidence collection, and workflow acceleration,
  not legal advice, regulatory sign-off, or pretending to replace a licensed
  compliance function.
- Binding constraints:
  - Limited weekly time means the first offer must be fixed-scope and
    repeatable.
  - Regulated-work boundaries mean substantive conclusions should stay with the
    client’s CCO, compliance lead, or external counsel.
  - If any prior employer materials, templates, or client data are involved,
    they must not be reused unless you clearly own the rights.

## Best Bet

Pursue `Compliance evidence concierge -> standardized portal/workflow product`.

Concrete first offer:

- “Annual compliance review and exam-prep evidence pack” for small financial
  firms or outsourced compliance providers.
- Deliverables:
  - A document request checklist tailored to the firm type
  - An evidence inventory and missing-items tracker
  - A draft issue log with questions for management review
  - A policy/process cross-reference sheet
  - A renewal/calendar checklist
- Pricing model: fixed fee for a tightly scoped prep sprint, not open-ended
  advisory time.

Narrow ICP:

- Small RIAs, broker-dealer-adjacent small firms, or outsourced compliance
  consultants serving firms with under roughly 25 employees and no sophisticated
  internal compliance operations tooling.

One measurable pain/outcome metric:

- Reduce owner or compliance lead time spent assembling review/exam materials by
  at least 30 percent in a 2-week prep cycle.

Why this beats the obvious alternative:

- Broad consulting loses because 8 to 10 hours per week is not enough to run a
  bespoke practice well.
- Software-first loses because the workflow needs validation and trust before a
  buyer will adopt tooling.
- Content-first loses because you do not want to be a content creator and you do
  not need that path given your warm contacts.

Compliance and legal guardrails:

- Position the service as operational preparation, evidence organization,
  workflow support, and draft generation.
- Do not market it as legal advice, compliance sign-off, regulatory approval, or
  outsourced CCO work unless you are properly authorized and insured for that
  role.
- Keep a human decision-maker at the client for substantive judgments.
- Use clean-room materials only: no prior employer IP, no reused
  client-confidential data, no confidential templates unless you own them.
- Put these boundaries in the engagement letter.

What would change the recommendation:

- If warm contacts convert poorly, a partner-channel version sold to existing
  compliance consultants may be better than direct-to-firm sales.
- If buyers repeatedly want only templates and not hands-on help, the
  asset-later library can move up.
- If regulated scope questions become hard to manage, narrow further into
  document collection and evidence tracking only.

## 7-Day Validation Plan

### Day 1

- Define one fixed-scope offer and one ICP.
- Draft a one-page offer sheet with explicit non-legal scope language.
- Success target: 1 offer sheet, 1 sample deliverable outline, 1
  engagement-scope paragraph.

### Day 2

- Make a list of 12 warm or warm-adjacent contacts: former clients, consultants,
  compliance operators, or introducers.
- Success target: 12 named prospects or introducers with contact route
  identified.

### Day 3

- Send 8 outreach messages asking for a 20-minute problem-validation call, not a
  sales pitch.
- Success target: at least 4 replies and 3 calls booked within 5 days.

### Day 4 to Day 5

- Run 3 to 5 calls.
- Ask about the last annual review or exam-prep cycle, where time was lost, what
  artifacts were painful to assemble, and whether a fixed-fee prep pack would
  have been useful.
- Success targets:
  - At least 3 calls completed
  - At least 2 repeated pain points across calls
  - At least 2 buyers or partners asking for follow-up

### Day 6

- Build a concierge mockup using AI-assisted internal workflows:
  - evidence request checklist
  - missing-items tracker
  - issue-log draft
  - policy cross-reference draft
- Success target: 1 realistic sample pack created in under 3 hours.

### Day 7

- Make 2 direct pilot asks for a paid fixed-fee sprint or 2 partner-channel asks
  to outsourced compliance consultants.
- Success targets:
  - At least 2 pilot asks made
  - At least 1 serious follow-up conversation on scope or price

## Build Spec

- Delivery model first: manual-plus-AI concierge, not SaaS.
- Internal workflow stack:
  - secure document intake and foldering
  - checklist generation from firm profile
  - artifact-to-requirement cross-reference table
  - issue-log draft generation with confidence labels
  - renewal and evidence calendar output
- Productization trigger:
  - After 3 paid pilots, if at least 70 percent of the workflow is repeatable
    across clients, build a lightweight portal.
- First product features:
  - client intake questionnaire
  - reusable evidence-request templates
  - missing-items tracker
  - issue-log workspace
  - policy/evidence cross-reference view
  - exportable packet for client review
- AI role:
  - summarize uploaded docs
  - draft checklists and issue logs
  - suggest missing evidence categories
  - normalize naming and metadata
- Human-in-the-loop requirement:
  - all substantive conclusions must be reviewed by the client’s responsible
    compliance person or approved specialist.

## Kill Criteria

- Stop direct-to-firm version if fewer than 3 of 8 outreach messages get
  replies.
- Stop or pivot the first offer if 5 validation calls produce no repeated
  painful workflow and no one requests a follow-up.
- Stop productization if the first 3 pilots share less than 70 percent common
  workflow steps.
- Stop the current positioning if buyers consistently interpret the service as
  legal advice or ask for licensed sign-off you cannot provide.
- Stop using any template or dataset that may rely on prior employer or client
  IP unless ownership is confirmed in writing.

## Rejected Paths

- Generic compliance AI copilot: Rejected because it is weakly differentiated,
  legally sensitive, and too easy to claim more than it can safely do.
- Pure content business: Rejected because you do not want to be a content
  creator and you do not need to rely on audience-building to get initial
  demand.
- Broad fractional compliance consulting: Rejected because it does not fit 8 to
  10 hours per week unless narrowed so aggressively that it becomes the
  recommended productized prep offer anyway.
