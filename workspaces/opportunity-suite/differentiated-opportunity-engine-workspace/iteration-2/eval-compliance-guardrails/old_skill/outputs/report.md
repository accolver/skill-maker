# Differentiation Profile

- Skills and domain experience: Former compliance consultant for small financial
  firms; strong understanding of recurring compliance workflows, exam prep,
  documentation gaps, vendor oversight, marketing review sensitivities, and how
  small firms actually operate under time pressure.
- Assets and access advantages: A few warm contacts in the target niche;
  practical knowledge of the documents, evidence trails, and remediation tasks
  small firms struggle to maintain.
- Distribution and network leverage: Warm outbound to former clients, referral
  partners, and operators who already trust your judgment is a credible path to
  first revenue without content creation.
- Constraints: Only 8 to 10 hours per week, minimal desire to build an audience,
  and a strong requirement to stay fully legal.
- Credibility signals: Prior consulting background in a regulated niche where
  buyers care about trust, precision, and discretion.
- Unfair advantages: You know which compliance work is painful, repetitive,
  urgent, and under-documented. You also know where AI can speed internal prep
  without crossing into unauthorized legal advice or assuming supervisory
  responsibility.

# Best Bet

Pursue **Compliance Evidence Concierge -> AI-Assisted Workflow Tool**.

This is the best fit because it matches your strongest edge, can reach paid
demand quickly through warm contacts, does not require content creation, and
gives AI a real role in delivery economics. The near-term offer should be a
**strictly scoped compliance operations support service** for small financial
firms, not legal advice and not outsourced supervisory responsibility.

Recommended initial offer:

- Target buyer: Small RIA or similar small financial firm with an overstretched
  owner, CCO, COO, or operations lead.
- Promise: "We help you get compliance evidence, recurring tasks, and
  remediation tracking organized so your team is exam-ready and less exposed to
  missed follow-ups."
- Deliverables: Evidence request checklist, centralized task tracker, document
  organization structure, issue/remediation log, AI-assisted summaries of source
  documents, and a monthly follow-up cadence.
- Pricing direction: Fixed-fee setup plus optional monthly retainer.

Why this beats the alternatives:

- Faster than building templates or software first.
- More durable than one-off audits because it can become recurring.
- Lower legal risk than selling interpretive regulatory briefings.
- Better path to product than a pure consulting offer because repeated workflow
  patterns are obvious and automatable.

Legal guardrails:

- Do not market this as legal advice, securities law interpretation, or
  outsourced CCO services.
- Keep scope on operations support, evidence collection, task management,
  documentation hygiene, and escalation support.
- Require client approval for any substantive compliance judgment.
- Use an engagement letter that states you support the firm's compliance
  operations but final decisions remain with the firm's designated compliance
  leadership and counsel.

# 7-Day Validation Plan

## Day 1

- Write a one-page offer with scope, exclusions, deliverables, ideal client
  profile, and legal guardrails.
- Pick one narrow niche first, such as small RIAs with lean internal operations.

## Day 2

- Build a simple sample pack: evidence checklist, remediation tracker, monthly
  compliance calendar, and example AI-assisted summary output.
- Keep it in tools you can run part-time, such as Google Docs, Notion, Airtable,
  or spreadsheets.

## Day 3

- Reach out to 5 warm contacts with a direct note asking for 20-minute feedback
  calls.
- Goal: learn whether exam readiness, evidence organization, or recurring
  remediation follow-up is the sharper pain.

## Day 4

- Run 2 to 3 calls and test a concrete offer: fixed-fee setup plus optional
  monthly follow-up.
- Ask what they currently use, where audits/exams create scramble, and what they
  would pay to make that process cleaner.

## Day 5

- Refine the offer around the most painful repeated task.
- If there is interest, ask for 1 pilot customer at discounted founding pricing
  in exchange for feedback and permission to anonymize the workflow learnings.

## Day 6

- Deliver a mini pilot manually using AI for document summarization, checklist
  drafting, and issue normalization.
- Measure time saved, missing-document detection, and clarity of the remediation
  queue.

## Day 7

- Decide whether to continue based on willingness to pay, repeatability of the
  workflow, and whether buyers clearly value the operational support without
  expecting legal advice.

# Build Spec

Build the minimum system as a **concierge workflow**, not a full SaaS product.

Core workflow:

- Intake form for firm profile, existing policies, recurring obligations, and
  current tooling.
- Document intake area with clear naming conventions and folders.
- AI-assisted document summarizer that extracts obligations, dates, owners, and
  evidence gaps from uploaded materials.
- Master compliance operations tracker with tasks, owners, due dates, evidence
  status, and remediation notes.
- Monthly review view that highlights overdue items, missing evidence, and
  unresolved follow-ups.
- Client-facing output: a clean evidence pack and action log.

Suggested tool stack:

- Front end: No-code or lightweight portal only if needed later; start with
  shared docs and spreadsheets.
- Data layer: Airtable, Notion database, or spreadsheet.
- Automation: Zapier, Make, or simple scripts.
- AI layer: LLM-assisted summarization, extraction, and draft checklist
  generation with human review.

Non-negotiable product constraints:

- Human review before anything is sent to the client.
- No autonomous regulatory interpretation.
- No claims of guaranteeing compliance.
- Clear audit trail showing AI-generated drafts versus human-approved outputs.

What to productize later:

- Reusable evidence request workflows.
- Due-date and attestation tracking.
- Recurring issue/remediation dashboard.
- Standardized policy and document summary extraction.

# Kill Criteria

- Fewer than 2 of 5 warm contacts confirm this is an active pain worth paying to
  reduce.
- No one will commit to a paid pilot within 30 days, even at a modest founding
  price.
- Buyers consistently expect legal interpretations or delegated compliance
  accountability that you do not want to provide.
- The work cannot be delivered in your 8 to 10 hour weekly constraint without
  custom effort each time.
- AI does not materially reduce prep time, document triage time, or follow-up
  tracking effort.
- The workflow turns out to be too bespoke across firms to standardize into a
  repeatable service or future tool.
