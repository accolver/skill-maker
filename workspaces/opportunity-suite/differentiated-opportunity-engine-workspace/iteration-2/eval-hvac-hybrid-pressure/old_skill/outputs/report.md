## Best Bet

The best path is `Revenue Recovery Concierge -> Automation -> Micro-SaaS`.

Why this is the best near-term choice:

- It reaches paid demand fastest because owners already know lost calls, stale
  estimates, and lapsed memberships cost them money.
- It fits your actual distribution: warm intros and a family pilot, not audience
  building.
- It keeps AI in a useful role instead of a gimmick: summarization, follow-up
  drafting, leak detection, prioritization, and workflow automation.
- It compounds into an asset because the service creates reusable playbooks,
  data models, integrations, benchmarks, and eventually product requirements.
- It avoids the worst trap in HVAC AI right now: launching a generic AI
  receptionist or chatbot with weak differentiation and heavy trust/compliance
  concerns.

Pressure test:

- Demand clarity: Strong. Owners understand recovered revenue faster than they
  understand abstract automation.
- Distribution fit: Strong. Your brother can likely produce the first pilot and
  several intros.
- Execution difficulty: Moderate. Start manually with exports, call logs, and
  simple messaging before building software.
- Compliance and platform risk: Low to moderate if you avoid making the AI
  customer-facing in regulated or high-stakes ways at first and get consent for
  call/message usage.
- Service-first before software: Yes. This should begin as a service. Building
  software first would increase risk and reduce learning speed.

Recommended initial offer:

- Promise: "We find and recover revenue hiding in your existing calls,
  estimates, and maintenance customer list."
- Format: 2-week paid sprint or monthly retainer.
- Price target: Start around $1,500-$3,500 setup for a sprint or $1,000-$2,500
  per month for ongoing recovery/ops support, depending on shop size and scope.
- Deliverables: Leak audit, recovered-opportunity list, automated follow-up
  sequences, owner dashboard, and weekly revenue recovery report.

## 7-Day Validation Plan

1. Day 1: Interview your brother for 60-90 minutes. Map the top 5 revenue leaks,
   current tools, where data lives, and what an owner would gladly pay to fix.
2. Day 1: Define one narrow offer. Do not sell "AI for HVAC." Sell one
   outcome-focused offer such as "recover unsold estimates and lapsed
   maintenance revenue in 14 days."
3. Day 2: Pull sample data from your brother's business. Use whatever is
   easiest: CSV exports, call logs, estimate lists, membership rosters, dispatch
   notes.
4. Day 2: Produce a manual audit for the brother's shop and estimate the dollar
   value of one or two obvious leaks.
5. Day 3: Create a one-page offer and a 5-slide proof deck with before/after
   workflow, leak examples, expected ROI, pricing, and pilot scope.
6. Day 3: Write a short intro message your brother can send to 10 owners he
   knows. Goal is 5 conversations, not mass outreach.
7. Day 4: Run 3-5 owner calls. Ask about missed calls, estimate follow-up,
   maintenance renewals, current software, and what they have already tried.
8. Day 4: Try to close one paid pilot. Discount is fine if they allow deep
   access, feedback, and a case study.
9. Day 5: Deliver the pilot manually. Build a spreadsheet or simple dashboard
   showing open opportunities, next actions, and recovered revenue.
10. Day 6: Add light automation only where it reduces your weekly labor
    immediately: transcription summaries, follow-up drafting, call
    categorization, and daily owner reports.
11. Day 7: Review results. Decide whether the repeatable wedge is missed-call
    recovery, estimate follow-up, maintenance renewals, or dispatcher
    assistance. Use that result to narrow the future micro-SaaS.

## Build Spec

Goal: A concierge-first internal tool that supports a service today and becomes
a micro-SaaS later.

Core user:

- HVAC owner or office manager.

Inputs:

- Call logs or recordings.
- CRM/job management exports.
- Estimate status list.
- Maintenance agreement customer list.
- Optional technician notes and booking outcomes.

Version 0: concierge delivery

- Manual data import via CSV.
- Opportunity queue with categories: missed call, open estimate, lapsed
  membership, unbooked follow-up.
- AI-assisted summaries and suggested next steps.
- Manual SMS/email/call task generation.
- Weekly revenue recovery report.

Version 1: service software

- Connectors for common HVAC systems where feasible.
- Daily sync of opportunities.
- Owner dashboard with counts, dollar value, aging, and status.
- Follow-up templates by opportunity type.
- AI-generated call and estimate summaries.
- Audit log for what was sent and what converted.

Version 2: micro-SaaS wedge

- Self-serve onboarding for one narrow workflow first.
- Benchmarking across shops using anonymized aggregate metrics.
- ROI dashboard tied to booked jobs or recovered revenue.
- Seat-based or location-based subscription plus optional service add-on.

Recommended stack principle:

- Use the fastest boring stack you already know.
- Keep AI behind the workflow, not as the product headline.
- Preserve raw data and human-review steps so you can refine prompts and logic
  from real outcomes.

## Kill Criteria

- Stop or pivot if you cannot get at least 5 warm owner conversations from your
  brother's network within 2 weeks.
- Stop or pivot if fewer than 2 owners describe the problem as urgent and tied
  to revenue.
- Stop or pivot if no one will pay for a pilot, even at a discounted founder
  rate, after seeing a concrete leak audit.
- Stop or pivot if delivery depends on brittle access to systems you cannot
  reliably integrate with or export from.
- Stop or pivot if the service does not reveal a repeatable workflow after 2-3
  pilots.
- Stop or pivot if the value ends up being generic admin labor rather than a
  measurable revenue recovery outcome.
