# 7-Day Validation Plan: Enterprise Procurement Analytics for Fortune 500 Teams

## Situation

- Category: enterprise procurement analytics for large-company sourcing, spend,
  and supplier-management teams.
- Starting point: no product, no live customers, and only one weak second-degree
  intro.
- Core constraint: this is a high-friction enterprise sale, so validation should
  focus on evidence of painful problems and willingness to keep engaging, not on
  immediate revenue.

## Best Bet

The strongest near-term wedge is **procurement savings discovery for indirect
spend categories**.

Why this is the best bet:

- Fortune 500 procurement leaders already care about cost reduction, compliance,
  maverick spend, and supplier consolidation.
- Analytics products can be validated before full software exists by using
  spreadsheet or slide-based audits.
- A narrow promise is easier to test than a broad "procurement intelligence
  platform."
- Buyers can react to quantified findings even if the product is still manual
  behind the scenes.

Initial positioning:

"We help enterprise procurement teams identify overlooked savings and compliance
leakage in indirect spend using faster category-level analytics than their
current BI and consulting workflows."

## What Counts As Real Validation

For this market, real early validation is not website traffic or vague interest.
The strongest 7-day signals are:

- procurement leaders agreeing to problem interviews;
- multiple people describing the same painful workflow without prompting;
- prospects sharing sample data, reports, or current process details;
- a buyer agreeing to a follow-up around a manual analysis or pilot concept;
- at least one warm path to a real budget owner.

## 7-Day Goal Summary

- Outreach sent: **35-50 targeted contacts**.
- Warm intro attempts from existing weak connection: **1-2**.
- Problem interviews booked: **4** minimum.
- Problem interviews completed: **3** minimum.
- Repeated pain pattern threshold: **2 of 3 interviews** mention a similar
  urgent issue.
- Follow-up meetings for a manual audit concept: **2**.
- Sample artifact requests made: **3**.
- Sample artifact received: **1**.
- Disqualifying target: if fewer than **2** interviews are booked after **50**
  well-targeted touches, treat this as weak initial pull and reconsider segment
  or wedge.

## Target Buyer and Narrow ICP

Do not start with "Fortune 500 procurement teams" broadly. Start with a narrower
ICP:

- Titles: VP Procurement, Chief Procurement Officer direct reports, Head of
  Strategic Sourcing, Director of Procurement Excellence, Category Director for
  indirect spend.
- Company profile: Fortune 500 or near-enterprise companies with large indirect
  spend and evidence of transformation pressure.
- Trigger conditions: public cost-efficiency initiatives, shared services, ERP
  migration, supplier rationalization, or procurement digital transformation.
- Categories to mention in discovery: IT spend, MRO, temp labor, facilities,
  freight, professional services.

## 7-Day Plan

### Day 1: Build the outreach list and message

Outputs:

- list of **35-50** target contacts across **15-20** companies;
- one outreach email and one LinkedIn message;
- one intro-forwardable note for the weak second-degree connection.

Actions:

- Build a spreadsheet with company, title, likely pain, and reason they fit.
- Prioritize people likely to already care about savings leakage or analytics
  bottlenecks.
- Draft one message focused on a concrete learning ask, not a product pitch.

Numeric goals:

- **15** accounts researched.
- **35** contacts minimum identified.
- **1** intro request sent.

### Day 2: Launch first outreach wave

Actions:

- Send the first **20-25** highly personalized emails or LinkedIn messages.
- Ask for a **20-minute** problem interview.
- Mention one concrete hypothesis, such as slow savings visibility across
  indirect categories.

Numeric goals:

- **20-25** outbound messages sent.
- **3** replies target.
- **1-2** meetings booked target.

### Day 3: Follow up and widen slightly

Actions:

- Follow up with all non-responders from day 2.
- Send another **15-25** messages.
- If response is weak, tighten the wedge further to one use case: indirect spend
  leakage, supplier fragmentation, or contract compliance gaps.

Numeric goals:

- cumulative outbound: **35-50**.
- cumulative positive replies: **4**.
- cumulative interviews booked: **2-4**.

### Day 4: Run the first interviews

Interview agenda:

- current process for spend visibility and savings tracking;
- where analysis breaks down;
- what data is hard to get or trust;
- when issues reach executive attention;
- what they have tried already.

Rules:

- Do not pitch software in the first 10 minutes.
- Ask for examples, spreadsheets, dashboards, or prior consulting outputs.
- End by testing interest in a manual findings review.

Numeric goals:

- **2** interviews completed.
- **2** concrete pain statements captured word-for-word.
- **1** request for a sample artifact.

### Day 5: Synthesize and test a manual audit offer

Actions:

- Convert interview notes into a one-page manual audit concept.
- Offer a no-build deliverable such as: "We will review one spend category and
  return 3-5 likely savings or leakage hypotheses within 5 business days."
- Send this to interviewees who showed strongest pain.

Numeric goals:

- **1** one-page offer created.
- **2** follow-up audit offers sent.
- **1** second meeting booked.

### Day 6: Run more interviews and pressure-test urgency

Actions:

- Complete remaining interviews.
- Ask explicit commitment questions:
- "If this found credible savings opportunities, who would need to care?"
- "Would you share a redacted export or current dashboard to test this?"
- "What would make this worth a pilot this quarter?"

Numeric goals:

- **3** total interviews completed minimum.
- **2** prospects express a clear next step.
- **1** prospect shares data, a template, or a live problem artifact.

### Day 7: Decide go / adjust / stop

Review against thresholds:

- Go deeper if you have **4+ booked interviews**, **3+ completed**, **2+ similar
  pains**, and **2** follow-up conversations or one artifact shared.
- Adjust the wedge if interest exists but pain is diffuse.
- Pause if outreach volume reached **50** well-targeted contacts and you still
  have fewer than **2** booked interviews or no repeated urgent pain.

## Recommended Outreach Angle

Use an insight-led message, not a platform pitch.

Example:

"I am researching how large procurement teams find savings leakage in indirect
spend when data is split across ERP, AP, and sourcing workflows. I am not
selling a tool yet. I am trying to learn where current analytics break down and
whether a faster outside-in audit would be useful. Would you be open to a
20-minute conversation?"

## Decision Thresholds

Strong validation:

- **4+** interviews booked in 7 days;
- **3+** completed;
- **2+** buyers independently describe the same painful workflow;
- **2+** ask to continue;
- **1** shares data or a real artifact.

Moderate validation:

- **2-3** interviews booked;
- pain exists but varies by role or category;
- no artifact shared yet, but buyers agree the problem is expensive.

Weak validation:

- fewer than **2** interviews booked after **35-50** targeted outreaches;
- buyers push the problem to generic BI or consultants;
- no one agrees to share examples or schedule a second step.

## Bottom Line

For this starting point, the realistic 7-day objective is **not sales**. It is
to prove that a narrow procurement analytics pain is urgent enough for
enterprise buyers to spend more time and expose real workflow evidence. The best
bet is a **manual savings-discovery wedge in indirect spend**, with success
defined by **3 completed interviews, 2 repeated pains, and 2 follow-up
discussions**.
