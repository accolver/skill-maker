## Validation Thesis

This is a `no_go` opportunity today because the user lacks three prerequisites
at once: edge, time, and distribution. The immediate goal is not to validate
end-demand for the business. The goal is to test whether the user can create
enough buyer access and operating capacity in 7 days to justify reopening the
opportunity.

The single biggest unknown is `buyer-access risk`: can the user create a
credible path to qualified conversations despite having no distribution today?
This matters most because without buyer access, there is no realistic way to
test willingness to pay, sharpen the offer, or build edge fast enough for the
opportunity to become actionable.

## Key Unknowns

1. Buyer-access risk: can the user get in front of the right buyers within 7
   days?
2. Time-allocation risk: can the user protect enough recurring time to run a
   real test instead of one-off activity?
3. Edge-creation risk: can the user create a narrow credibility wedge that earns
   attention from those buyers?

## 7-Day Plan

### Day 1

- Pick one narrow buyer and one painful workflow to target.
- Block at least `4 hours/week` on the calendar for the next `4 weeks`.
- Build a list of `20` qualified prospects and `5` possible warm connectors,
  community operators, or channel partners.

### Day 2

- Create one simple credibility asset tied to the chosen buyer and workflow.
- Good options: a teardown, checklist, benchmark, short audit template, or "3
  mistakes" memo.
- The asset must make a concrete claim that shows emerging edge, not generic
  content.

### Day 3

- Send `20` targeted outreach messages or make `5` warm intro asks plus `10`
  direct messages.
- Offer one specific next step: a `15-minute` problem call or a short custom
  teardown.
- Track who responds, who declines, and what language gets attention.

### Day 4

- Run the first `2-3` qualified conversations.
- Confirm urgency, current workaround, and whether the credibility asset is
  strong enough to earn a follow-up.
- Ask for the strongest feasible commitment: a booked follow-up, permission to
  review data/workflow, or a scoped teardown request.

### Day 5

- Continue outreach until at least `5` qualified conversations are completed or
  clearly impossible.
- Tighten the buyer definition and message based on objections and response
  patterns.
- Drop any outreach angle that produces low-quality interest.

### Day 6

- Ask the best prospects for a concrete next step tied to real effort.
- Preferred signals: a scheduled review session, requested audit/teardown,
  introduction to the actual operator, or agreement to discuss a paid pilot
  after the teardown.
- Ask one channel/contact source for a repeatable path to more buyers.

### Day 7

- Review the evidence against the thresholds below.
- Decide `go`, `revise`, or `kill`.
- If the result is not `go`, keep the broader opportunity in `no_go` status
  until a prerequisite is changed.

## Success Criteria

### Go

- `5+` qualified conversations completed within 7 days.
- Calendar shows `4+ hours/week` protected for the next `4 weeks`.
- At least `2` prospects ask for a concrete next step such as a teardown,
  review, or scoping conversation, or `1` channel source agrees to provide
  repeatable buyer access.
- The user can clearly state one narrow wedge that buyers respond to.

### Revise

- `3-4` qualified conversations completed.
- Protected time is in place, but response quality is mixed.
- At least `1` concrete next step is earned, but the wedge or channel still
  looks weak.
- Action: narrow the buyer, sharpen the credibility asset, and rerun the test.

## Kill Criteria

- Fewer than `3` qualified conversations after `20` targeted outreach attempts
  and `5` intro/channel asks.
- No recurring `4 hours/week` can be protected.
- No prospect agrees to any concrete next step.
- Every interested prospect requires broad custom work or unrelated services,
  which means the wedge is still missing.

## Why Not The Lower-Signal Alternative

The weaker alternative would be generic idea interviews or content posting to
see if anyone reacts. That would produce activity but not change the actual
constraint. In this case, the strongest feasible signal is not waitlist growth
or opinion gathering. It is proof that the user can create real buyer access and
earn concrete follow-up commitments despite starting with no edge, no time, and
no distribution.
