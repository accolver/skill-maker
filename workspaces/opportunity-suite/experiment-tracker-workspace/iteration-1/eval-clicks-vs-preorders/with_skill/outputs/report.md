## Experiment Summary

The experiment tested demand by sending a newsletter that drove roughly 800
clicks to an offer with a stated preorder price. The send generated strong
top-of-funnel engagement and a lot of positive qualitative feedback, but it
produced zero preorders.

What this means: interest in the topic or message is real, but willingness to
buy at the current price is unproven and currently unsupported. The gap is not
awareness; it is conversion to paid commitment.

## Signal Quality

- Vanity signal: 800 clicks show attention, not purchase intent.
- Weak signal: praise is encouraging, but vague positive feedback is lower
  quality unless it comes from buyers who commit.
- Strong signal: zero preorders is a strong negative signal against the current
  offer at the stated price, because paid commitment is the highest-quality
  validation and none occurred.
- Contradiction: message-market resonance appears decent at the curiosity level,
  but the pricing and/or offer is failing to convert that curiosity into
  commitment.

## Decision State

`pivot`

The current wedge is not dead, but the current packaging at this stated preorder
price is not validated. Evidence points away from continuing the exact same
offer and toward changing the offer/pricing structure to learn what buyers would
actually commit to.

## Next Experiment

Run one pricing-and-offer sensitivity experiment with the same audience: present
the same core product in three concrete preorder options, such as a lower entry
price, the current price with a stronger bonus or guarantee, and a higher-touch
premium option, then measure which version gets actual preorder commitments.

Success criterion: at least one option generates real paid conversions or enough
explicit buyer objections to identify the main blocker, especially whether the
problem is price, offer framing, trust, or urgency.
