# Experiment Summary

30 founder interviews produced clear top-of-funnel signal but no activation.
Pain appears real for a meaningful subset of the market, yet the funnel breaks
after interest.

## Current State

- 12 of 30 interviews reported the pain as severe (40%).
- 5 of 30 asked for a demo (17%).
- 2 of 30 started trials (7%).
- 0 of 30 activated (0%).

This suggests the core problem is credible, but the product is not yet getting
users through evaluation into successful first value.

## Key Interpretation

The main bottleneck is not awareness of the problem. It is activation during or
immediately after trial start.

Two concrete reasons surfaced:

- 1 prospect was blocked by legal concerns around data sharing.
- 1 prospect found onboarding confusing.

Even though each issue was only explicitly mentioned once, both are plausible
activation killers in a very small sample. Legal friction can stop trials before
meaningful setup. Confusing onboarding can prevent users from reaching value
even after starting.

## Recommended Next Experiment

Run an activation-focused experiment that removes both trust and onboarding
friction for new trial users.

### Experiment

Offer a guided, low-risk pilot to the next 5 to 10 qualified prospects with:

- a no-sensitive-data or synthetic-data demo path
- a short legal/trust FAQ or security one-pager
- a concierge onboarding session with a single defined activation milestone

### Hypothesis

If legal risk and onboarding confusion are the primary blockers, then reducing
those frictions will increase the rate from trial start to activation.

### Success Metric

- Primary: at least 2 activations from the next 5 to 10 trials
- Secondary: fewer prospects citing legal uncertainty or setup confusion as the
  reason for stalling

### What To Measure

- trial start to activation conversion
- where users stall in onboarding
- whether legal objections happen before or after seeing the trust materials
- whether concierge onboarding consistently gets users to first value

## Bottom Line

The market signal is promising enough to keep going, but the product is
currently failing at activation. The next experiment should focus narrowly on
removing legal and onboarding friction rather than collecting more general
discovery interviews.
