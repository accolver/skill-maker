# Experiment Summary

- 30 founder interviews were completed.
- 12 of 30 said the pain is severe, which is meaningful demand signal but not
  yet majority conviction.
- 5 requested a demo, showing some buying curiosity beyond interview-level
  interest.
- 2 started trials, which is stronger signal because prospects took a real next
  step.
- 0 activated, so the product is currently failing to convert trial interest
  into successful initial use.
- 1 prospect reported legal blocked data sharing.
- 1 prospect reported onboarding was confusing.

The state is: there is real pain, but the current bottleneck is not demand
generation. The funnel breaks between trial start and activation.

# Signal Quality

- Strong signal: 12 founders describing severe pain indicates repeated workflow
  pain.
- Strong signal: 2 trial starts show some prospects are willing to try the
  product in practice.
- Weak signal: 5 demo requests are directional, but they do not matter if users
  cannot activate.
- Contradictory signal: severe pain exists, yet activation is zero.
- Contradictory signal: one prospect hit legal friction, while another hit
  onboarding confusion, so the exact primary blocker is not yet isolated.

# Decision State

`continue`

The wedge still has evidence of real pain and some willingness to engage, so
this is not a stop or pivot case. It is also too early to call for `narrow`
because the evidence points more to a funnel bottleneck than to one winning
segment. The right move is to continue while focusing specifically on the
activation barrier.

# Next Experiment

Run a concierge activation experiment with 5 new trial users where onboarding is
manually guided and no sensitive data is required upfront.

Success criteria:

- At least 3 of 5 complete activation.
- Track whether failures are still caused by legal/data-sharing constraints or
  by product/setup confusion.

Why this is the next best experiment:

- It isolates the biggest uncertainty: whether zero activation is mainly caused
  by onboarding friction or by legal/data-sharing requirements.
- Manual guidance removes onboarding confusion as a variable.
- Using a no-sensitive-data path tests whether users can reach value before
  procurement or legal review blocks progress.
