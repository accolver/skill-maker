## Profile Summary

The strongest claimed edge is mostly employer-restricted rather than freely
usable. Access to internal templates, pricing logic, and process docs is real
informational advantage, but it is not independently owned and should not be
treated as portable moat.

There is still some usable edge: the user likely has real govtech domain
familiarity and broad market understanding. That is commercially relevant only
to the extent it can be separated from confidential employer information and
turned into original judgment, not copied materials.

Overall assessment: the profile has some usable domain edge, but the most
specific and potentially valuable assets described are restricted.

## Usable Edge

- Govtech market familiarity at a general, non-confidential level
- Domain judgment shaped by working inside the market
- Potential ability to recognize common workflow, procurement, and pricing pain
  points without reusing employer materials

## Restricted Edge

- Internal templates from the employer
- Employer pricing logic or pricing formulas
- Internal process documentation and operating playbooks
- Specific know-how that depends on confidential internal materials rather than
  public observation or independently created expertise

## Fake Or Overstated Edge

- "I know the market really well" is not, by itself, a moat unless it produces
  owned customer access, trusted distribution, or clearly legal reusable product
  insight
- Employer-mediated access to internal knowledge can sound stronger than it is
  because it is not independently owned or safely monetizable

## Weak Spots

- No evidence of owned distribution such as an audience, sales channel, or warm
  buyer list
- No evidence of independently controlled network access to buyers outside the
  employer context
- No proof that the user can legally reuse any of the most specific operating
  knowledge they referenced
- No concrete credibility signals beyond current market exposure

## Follow-Up Questions

- Do you have direct buyer relationships you personally own outside your
  employer?
- What parts of your market knowledge come from public observation versus
  confidential internal materials?
- Have you created any original frameworks, content, or tools that do not rely
  on employer IP?
- Can you name specific pains you understand well without referencing internal
  templates, docs, or pricing logic?
- Do you control any distribution channel to govtech buyers today?
