## Employer-Restricted Edge Evaluation

### Scenario

The subject works at a govtech company and has access to:

- internal templates
- pricing logic
- process documentation
- strong market knowledge

No input files were provided.

### Assessment

This appears to be an **employer-restricted edge**.

The strongest claimed advantages come from non-public employer resources and
context gained through employment. Internal templates, pricing logic, and
process documents are company-controlled assets and are typically confidential
or access-restricted. That means they should not be treated as portable,
independently usable advantage in a clean-room setting.

The market knowledge claim is more mixed:

- General industry knowledge, public procurement patterns, and non-confidential
  expertise may be portable.
- Specific insights derived from confidential internal strategy, customer data,
  pricing methods, or proprietary operating playbooks are not portable.

### Conclusion

Overall result: **Restricted**.

Reason: the edge substantially depends on employer-confidential materials and
know-how that should be assumed unavailable for external reuse unless explicitly
authorized and separable from proprietary information.

### Boundary For Safe Use

Potentially usable without restriction:

- publicly observable market structure
- general domain expertise
- personal skills in sales, operations, and product judgment

Not safely usable without permission:

- internal templates copied or adapted from employer materials
- proprietary pricing logic or pricing formulas
- internal process docs or implementation playbooks
- confidential customer, procurement, or competitive intelligence

### Recommended Label

`employer_restricted_edge`
