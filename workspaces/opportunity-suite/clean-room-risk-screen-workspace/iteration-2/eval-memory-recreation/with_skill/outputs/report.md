# Risk Summary

This plan is high risk. Recreating an employer's internal templates, pricing
logic, or process documentation from memory for a same-niche govtech side
business is not a valid clean-room approach. The main risks are employer
IP/confidentiality misuse, reuse of confidential pricing and process know-how,
possible customer-access and solicitation issues, and unsafe positioning if the
side business implies official authority or proven internal-grade methods it
does not independently own.

# Allowed

- Use generalized industry knowledge, professional judgment, and publicly known
  govtech concepts that are not specific to the employer's internal materials.
- Research public procurement rules, public agency pain points, public RFPs,
  public budgets, public vendor materials, and other lawfully available sources.
- Create brand-new templates, workflows, and documentation from scratch using
  public information and independent reasoning.
- Offer narrow support such as research, organization, drafting assistance, or
  evidence gathering where the customer retains decision-making responsibility.

# Conditional

- Building a same-niche business is only lower risk if all deliverables are
  newly created and can be traced to public sources, independent experience, or
  fresh original work rather than employer materials.
- Pricing may be developed only from public pricing signals, customer discovery,
  competitor research, and independent cost modeling, not by mirroring
  remembered internal pricing logic.
- Process documentation may be created only at a generalized level and only if
  it does not reproduce distinctive internal steps, thresholds, formats, or
  playbooks from the employer.
- Outreach is only acceptable if it avoids restricted customer relationships,
  non-solicitation issues, and misuse of employer contacts or pipeline
  information.

# Blocked

- Recreating employer internal templates from memory.
- Reproducing or paraphrasing confidential pricing logic, formulas, decision
  rules, approval thresholds, or rate structures from memory.
- Rebuilding internal process docs, playbooks, SOPs, or implementation methods
  that are specific to the employer's operations.
- Using employer customer lists, pipeline knowledge, buyer preferences,
  non-public procurement intelligence, or restricted relationship access.
- Marketing the side business as if it has the employer's authority, proprietary
  methods, or proven internal systems when it does not independently own them.

# Safer Path

- Start with a clean-room offer based on public-data research products for
  agencies or vendors in the niche.
- Build original templates from a blank page, justified by public regulations,
  public RFP language, and firsthand independent experience rather than
  remembered employer artifacts.
- Create generalized workflow support tools that help clients prepare
  information, organize evidence, or draft inputs for human review instead of
  automating regulated or authority-bound judgment.
- Develop pricing from scratch using public competitor signals, interviews, and
  your own delivery model.
- Keep records of source material for important assets so you can show they were
  built from public or independently created inputs.
