# Offer Thesis

The fastest path to paid validation is a narrow **hiring-process audit** sold to
boutique recruiting firms that already feel pain in candidate outreach
performance. It is easier to scope, easier to say yes to over a warm intro, and
faster to deliver than a done-with-you outreach sprint because it does not
require execution capacity, system access, or shared operational ownership.

Primary offer shape: **Diagnostic / audit**.

Why this shape beats the closest alternative: The done-with-you outreach sprint
is the obvious alternative, but it is slower to validate because buyers must
trust the founder with live workflow changes, tool access, messaging decisions,
and ongoing collaboration. The audit creates a paid first step with lower risk
for the buyer and lower delivery burden for the founder, while still proving
whether the pain is urgent enough to support a later sprint upsell.

# ICP

- Buyer: Founder or managing partner of a boutique recruiting firm
- Segment: 2-15 person recruiting agencies running founder-led or small-team
  outbound candidate outreach
- Why now: Reply rates are soft, recruiters are spending too much time on
  low-yield outreach, and the firm needs better process performance without
  hiring internally or committing to a large consulting engagement
- Painful job to be done: Diagnose why candidate outreach is underperforming and
  identify the few process and messaging changes most likely to improve response
  quality quickly

# Offer Design

Offer name: **Candidate Outreach Conversion Audit**

Core deliverable:

- A fixed-scope audit of the firm's current candidate outreach process focused
  on sourcing-to-reply conversion bottlenecks

Deliverables:

- 60-minute intake call to understand roles, recruiter workflow, and current
  outreach motion
- Review of existing outreach assets: email / LinkedIn sequences, sourcing
  criteria, targeting logic, follow-up cadence, and recruiter handoff points
- Written bottleneck diagnosis showing where outreach is breaking down
- Priority-ranked recommendations for the top 3-5 changes to test first
- Rewritten sample outreach sequence for one representative role or candidate
  segment
- 45-minute readout call with Q&A and implementation guidance

Suggested delivery window:

- 5 business days from intake to readout

Scope boundaries:

- One firm, one outreach motion, and one representative role family for the
  initial offer
- Includes diagnosis and examples, not full implementation
- No live outreach execution, recruiter management, sourcing operations, or
  CRM/ATS rebuild
- No guarantee of hires, response rates, or placement outcomes
- No broad talent strategy, employer branding, or full funnel recruiting
  redesign

# Pricing Hypothesis

- Model: Fixed fee
- Range: **$1,500-$3,000** for the initial audit
- Pricing rationale: This is high enough to validate willingness to pay, but
  still small enough for a boutique agency founder to approve from a warm intro
  without a long sales cycle. The lower end fits an early pilot for social
  proof; the upper end is justified when the firm has multiple recruiters,
  active req volume, and obvious revenue leakage from weak outreach.
- What would change the price: evidence on how painful the problem feels to
  buyers, how much variation exists across firms, and whether buyers want
  implementation support immediately after the audit

# Rejected Framings

- **Done-with-you outreach sprint first**: too much delivery overhead for the
  founder's current time constraints, and it requires more buyer trust before
  paid validation
- **Broad recruiting-process consulting**: too vague to sell quickly and too
  easy for buyers to postpone
- **Done-for-you recruiting / placement support**: drifts into agency execution
  rather than a narrow diagnostic wedge and creates messy expectations around
  outcomes
- **Generic AI outreach optimization service**: sounds interchangeable and does
  not anchor on the buyer's concrete pain
