# Offer Design: Concierge Reporting Pack for Niche Ecommerce Operators

## Recommendation

Position the offer as a **done-for-you monthly insight and decision-support
service** for small-to-mid-size niche ecommerce brands that have revenue data
but do not have an operator who can consistently turn it into actions.

The offer should be sold as a **concierge reporting pack**, not as generic
analytics setup. The core promise is:

"We turn messy store, marketing, and product data into a short monthly operator
briefing with clear actions on what to fix, cut, repeat, and watch next."

This framing fits the current uncertainty because buyers may value the outcome,
while delivery effort can vary widely by store complexity.

## Why This Direction Is Plausible

1. Many niche operators have dashboards already but still lack interpretation.
2. Founders and small teams often want answers to practical questions, not more
   tooling.
3. A reporting pack can be scoped as a recurring service before building
   software.
4. Concierge delivery gives flexibility while demand and willingness to pay are
   still unvalidated.

## Core Buyer

Best initial customer profile:

- Founder-led or lean ecommerce brands.
- Roughly 50 to 500 orders per month.
- Selling through Shopify or a similar platform.
- Running paid acquisition on at least one channel.
- Feeling uncertainty around profitability, repeat purchase, SKU performance, or
  campaign efficiency.

Avoid initially targeting:

- Very early stores with too little data.
- Enterprise merchants with complex BI expectations.
- Multi-region, multi-warehouse businesses unless pricing is custom.

## Main Job To Be Done

"Help me understand what happened in the business this month, why it happened,
and what I should do next without hiring an analyst."

## Offer Structure

### Core Deliverable

Each monthly pack should include:

- Executive summary in plain English.
- KPI snapshot: revenue, contribution signals, AOV, conversion, repeat rate, top
  SKUs, channel mix.
- 3 to 5 key findings.
- 3 to 5 recommended actions ranked by likely business impact.
- Watchlist of emerging risks or anomalies.
- Optional async Q&A support for follow-up interpretation.

### Inputs

Minimum required inputs:

- Store platform data.
- Ad platform exports or view access.
- Product catalog or SKU mapping.

Optional inputs:

- Email/SMS platform performance.
- Inventory data.
- Cohort or subscription data.
- Financial or contribution margin inputs.

### Service Levels

To handle pricing uncertainty, define tiers by complexity rather than by vague
value language.

#### Tier 1: Starter Pack

Best for simple stores with one storefront and limited channels.

- 1 store
- Up to 2 marketing channels
- Up to 100 active SKUs
- Monthly report
- 1 revision / Q&A round

#### Tier 2: Growth Pack

Best for operators who need more cross-channel interpretation.

- 1 store
- Up to 4 channels
- Up to 500 active SKUs
- Monthly report
- Trend comparisons and deeper product/channel cuts
- Loom walkthrough or review call

#### Tier 3: Custom / Complex Store

For brands with multiple stores, bundles, subscriptions, or operational
complexity.

- Multi-store or multi-brand
- Large SKU counts
- Custom source integration
- Margin, inventory, or cohort views
- Custom analysis questions

This tier should always be custom-quoted.

## Pricing Design

Because buyer value is plausible but unvalidated, and fulfillment effort may
swing heavily by store complexity, pricing should combine **clear entry
pricing + explicit complexity boundaries + setup fees + custom overages**.

### Suggested Initial Pricing

- Starter Pack: $500 to $900 per month
- Growth Pack: $1,000 to $2,000 per month
- Custom / Complex: custom quote, likely starting at $2,500+ per month

### Setup Fee

Charge a one-time setup fee to absorb onboarding variance.

- Starter: $250 to $500
- Growth: $500 to $1,000
- Custom: quoted separately

This is important because setup effort is likely where complexity surprises
appear first.

### Overage / Scope Controls

Define scope triggers up front:

- Additional store or region
- Additional ad account or marketing channel
- SKU count above tier threshold
- Requests for ad hoc deep dives
- Requests for weekly rather than monthly reporting
- Need for margin modeling or inventory analysis

You can price these as either fixed add-ons or hourly analyst support.

## Packaging Principles

1. Sell decisions, not dashboards.
2. Keep the recurring deliverable standardized.
3. Isolate variable effort into setup, add-ons, and custom tiering.
4. Make complexity visible before the sale.
5. Preserve manual service flexibility while learning what buyers consistently
   want.

## Recommended Sales Framing

Use language like:

- "Monthly operator briefing for ecommerce founders"
- "Done-for-you reporting that ends with recommended actions"
- "Analytics without hiring an in-house analyst"

Avoid language like:

- "Business intelligence solution"
- "Custom analytics system"
- "Fractional data team"

The simpler framing is easier to buy and easier to scope.

## Risk Areas

1. **Pricing mismatch**: simple stores may resist retainers; complex stores may
   consume too much time.
2. **Data messiness**: inconsistent attribution, SKU naming, or missing cost
   data can expand delivery work.
3. **Perceived overlap with existing dashboards**: buyers may think they already
   have this unless recommendations are emphasized.
4. **Hidden consulting creep**: once trust is built, clients may ask broader
   strategy questions beyond reporting.

## How To Reduce Risk Early

1. Start with a narrow ICP: niche Shopify brands with simple ops.
2. Require a short pre-sale complexity intake.
3. Separate onboarding from recurring delivery in both pricing and scope.
4. Offer a paid pilot month before pushing annual or long commitments.
5. Template the report heavily so margin improves over time.

## Suggested Pilot Version

An effective validation offer would be:

- One-time paid diagnostic or first-month reporting pack.
- Fixed scope.
- Delivered within 5 business days of data receipt.
- Includes a written report plus one review call.

Example pilot pricing:

- $750 flat for a simple store
- $1,500 flat for a moderate-complexity store

This helps test willingness to pay before committing to monthly retainers.

## What Success Looks Like

The offer is working if prospects say:

- "This saves me from digging through dashboards myself."
- "The recommendations are more useful than the raw metrics."
- "I can see exactly what I'd do next month based on this."

## Final Recommendation

Proceed with a **tiered concierge reporting offer** anchored around a
standardized monthly deliverable, with pricing segmented by complexity and
protected by setup fees and scope boundaries.

Do **not** lead with fully custom analytics work. Lead with a repeatable
operator briefing product, then upsell complexity only when justified.

The best near-term shape is:

- Paid pilot diagnostic
- Starter monthly pack for simple stores
- Growth monthly pack for moderate complexity
- Custom quote for everything else

This is the cleanest way to capture plausible buyer value while protecting
against pricing uncertainty and delivery sprawl.
