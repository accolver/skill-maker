# Channel Plan: Paid Benchmark And Template Product For Therapists

## Channel Thesis

The strongest starting channel is a niche newsletter launch to the therapist
audience that already trusts the publisher. This offer is information-dense,
moderately priced, and credibility-sensitive, so conversion plausibility is
highest when the recommendation arrives from an existing trusted voice instead
of cold acquisition. The benchmark angle gives the audience a reason to buy now
because it helps them compare their practice against peers, while the template
bundle gives immediate practical value after purchase.

## Primary Channel

Primary channel: trusted therapist newsletter launch.

Why this should go first:

- Trust already exists between the newsletter and the audience.
- The audience is directly relevant to the product.
- The product can be explained clearly in email without requiring a long sales
  cycle.
- The channel is fast to test with measurable signals within days.

Recommended launch sequence:

1. Publish a pre-launch teaser to frame the problem: most therapists do not know
   whether their pricing, intake flow, cancellation policy, or client
   communication materials are above or below market.
2. Run a waitlist or interest-capture email to measure intent before the main
   push.
3. Send the main sales email focused on outcomes: compare your practice, reduce
   guesswork, and get templates you can use immediately.
4. Send a proof email with 3 to 5 concrete benchmark insights or anonymized
   sample findings.
5. Send a final-call email with deadline, launch pricing, or bonus expiration.

## Supporting Channels

Supporting channels should only strengthen the newsletter launch:

- Landing page: central conversion page linked from all emails, with sample
  charts, deliverables, FAQ, and checkout.
- Lightweight partner amplification: a small number of aligned therapist
  educators or consultants can share the launch if they already have warm
  overlap with the audience.
- Social reposts from the newsletter brand: used only to echo the email launch,
  not as a primary acquisition engine.

Channels to avoid at the start:

- Broad paid ads, because trust is lower and creative/testing burden is higher.
- Cold outbound, because this product is better suited to one-to-many trust
  transfer.
- Marketplace or directory distribution, because differentiation is likely weak
  without brand context.

## Asset Checklist

- Clear offer positioning statement: benchmark report plus usable templates for
  private-practice therapists.
- Product page with pricing, deliverables, who it is for, and what is included.
- Sample benchmark visuals or anonymized example metrics.
- Template preview list with 1 to 2 visible examples.
- FAQ covering methodology, audience fit, and whether solo or group practices
  should buy.
- Three-email launch sequence minimum: main launch, proof, final call.
- Optional pre-launch interest email.
- Checkout flow and post-purchase delivery email.
- Tracking links for each email send.

## Metrics And Kill Criteria

Primary metrics for the newsletter launch:

- Interest email click-through rate: target at least 3% of recipients.
- Waitlist-to-purchase rate: target at least 8%.
- Main launch email click-to-purchase conversion: target at least 4% of
  clickers.
- Overall email-to-purchase conversion on the main send: target at least 0.8% of
  recipients.
- Refund or dissatisfaction rate: keep below 5%.

Secondary metrics:

- Partner shares secured: 2 to 5 warm amplifiers.
- Landing page conversion from email traffic: target at least 3%.
- Revenue per 1,000 newsletter recipients: use as a benchmark for repeatability.

Kill criteria after one bounded launch test:

- Cut or materially reposition the offer if the main launch email gets strong
  clicks but weak purchases, indicating message-product mismatch.
- Cut the newsletter-first launch approach if overall email-to-purchase
  conversion stays below 0.3% after the full sequence.
- Cut partner outreach as a focus area if fewer than 2 warm partners agree to
  share or partner traffic converts materially worse than core newsletter
  traffic.
- Do not expand into paid acquisition until the owned newsletter launch hits or
  exceeds the baseline purchase thresholds.
