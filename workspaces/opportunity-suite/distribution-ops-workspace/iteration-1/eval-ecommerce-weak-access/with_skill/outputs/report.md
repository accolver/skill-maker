# Channel Plan For Ecommerce Reporting Product

## Channel Thesis

The product solves a real merchant workflow problem, but the founder lacks
direct buyer access and does not have an owned audience. That makes founder-led
content or cold outbound a weak starting point because trust and list quality
are missing. The strongest starting channel is a partner path through agencies,
fractional ecommerce operators, and app-adjacent service providers who already
advise merchants on reporting, attribution, finance, or operations.

This channel fits the situation because the founder's workflow knowledge is
useful to intermediaries even before there is broad brand recognition. Partners
can supply trust, access, and early customer feedback faster than building an
audience from zero.

## Primary Channel

**Primary channel:** Partner or reseller path focused on merchant-facing
agencies and operators.

Target partner types:

- ecommerce agencies handling retention, paid media, CRO, or lifecycle work
- fractional ecommerce operators and consultants
- bookkeeping, FP&A, and analytics service firms serving Shopify brands
- implementation partners for adjacent apps where reporting gaps are painful

Why this is the best first channel:

- trust already exists between partner and merchant
- partner can explain the value in workflow language merchants already use
- partner conversations can happen without a large audience
- a small number of active partners can produce signal faster than broad
  awareness efforts

Initial partner pitch:

- help your merchants answer recurring reporting questions faster
- reduce manual spreadsheet work and ad hoc client requests
- give the partner a differentiated reporting workflow they can offer clients

Suggested 30-day sequence:

1. Build a list of 40 target partners in one narrow segment, starting with
   Shopify-focused agencies and fractional operators.
2. Create a short partner brief showing the merchant problem, ideal use case,
   and what a pilot looks like.
3. Run direct outreach to book discovery calls with partners, not merchants.
4. Convert 5 to 8 partners into pilot conversations.
5. Ask each interested partner for 1 to 3 merchant introductions or a
   white-glove pilot opportunity.
6. Run pilots with introduced merchants and capture proof points.
7. Use those proof points to tighten the partner pitch and expand into the next
   partner segment.

## Supporting Channels

Supporting channels should only strengthen the partner path.

**1. Narrow outbound to owned target list**

Use outbound for partners, not end merchants. This is a focused list-building
and outreach motion to agencies, consultants, and service providers that already
serve the buyer.

**2. Niche newsletter or community launch**

Instead of building a general audience, place problem-led content inside
communities where partners already gather. Good examples are Slack groups,
operator communities, agency newsletters, or Shopify ecosystem events. The goal
is credibility with partners, not top-of-funnel scale.

**3. Marketplace or directory presence**

This is a credibility layer, not the main acquisition motion. A lightweight site
page, app listing, or partner page helps partners feel safe introducing the
product. It should support conversion after intros, not be treated as a
discovery engine early on.

Channels to avoid first:

- broad cold outbound to merchants
- generic SEO/content marketing
- paid acquisition
- social audience-building as the main distribution bet

These channels require either trust, audience, budget, or long time horizons
that the current situation does not support.

## Asset Checklist

- one-page partner brief explaining merchant pain, workflow, and pilot structure
- short demo tailored to agencies/operators rather than generic merchant
  messaging
- partner landing page with clear use cases and pilot CTA
- simple ROI or time-saved calculator for merchant reporting workflows
- outreach copy for partner discovery, follow-up, and intro requests
- pilot onboarding checklist for merchants introduced by partners
- proof capture template for testimonial, workflow outcome, and time-saved notes
- CRM sheet tracking partner stage, intros, pilots, and outcomes

## Metrics And Kill Criteria

Success metrics for the first bounded test:

- 40 target partners contacted in the first sprint
- at least 25% reply rate from well-matched partners
- at least 20% meeting rate from contacted partners
- at least 5 serious partner conversations
- at least 3 partners willing to make merchant introductions or run a pilot
- at least 3 merchant pilots started from partner access
- at least 1 pilot converts into paid usage or a clearly committed expansion
  path

Kill criteria for the primary channel:

- fewer than 10% partner replies after 40 highly relevant outreaches
- fewer than 3 partner meetings after the first 40 outreaches
- zero merchant introductions after 5 quality partner conversations
- pilots start but partners show no repeatable reason to introduce more
  merchants
- no credible path to paid conversion after 3 merchant pilots

If kill criteria are hit, the next-best fallback is a narrow niche-community
launch aimed at operators and consultants, using workshops, teardown content, or
reporting templates to attract partner-like intermediaries before going direct
to merchants.
