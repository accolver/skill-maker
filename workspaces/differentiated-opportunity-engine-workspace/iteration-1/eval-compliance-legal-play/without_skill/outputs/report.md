# Recommended Opportunity

Pursue an **AI-assisted compliance documentation and mock-exam readiness service
for small SEC/FINRA-adjacent firms**, sold as a narrow, high-trust productized
service rather than broad consulting or content-led coaching.

In practice, this means helping small financial firms clean up, maintain, and
pressure-test the things they already know they need: written supervisory
procedures, annual reviews, marketing/comms review workflows, vendor oversight
records, training logs, branch checklists, evidence folders, and mock exam
preparation packs. The offer should be framed as **documentation operations and
preparedness support**, not legal advice and not outsourced supervision.

## Why This Fits You

You already have the two hardest assets for this niche:

- Real compliance judgment from prior consulting work
- Warm contacts in a trust-sensitive market

It also matches your constraints:

- **8 to 10 hours/week:** productized compliance support can be scoped into
  fixed monthly retainers or defined projects
- **Low desire to create content:** this can be sold through referrals, direct
  outreach, and private demos instead of audience building
- **Need to stay legal:** documentation support and mock-readiness are easier to
  position safely than acting as legal counsel or a designated compliance
  function
- **AI leverage:** AI is strong at first-draft policies, gap checklists,
  evidence organization, meeting-note summarization, testing templates, and
  issue tracking

## Best Initial Offer

Start with one narrow offer:

**"Compliance Documentation Tune-Up + Mock Exam Pack"**

Deliverables:

- Review current compliance documents and records against a structured checklist
- Identify gaps, inconsistencies, stale language, and missing evidence
- Produce first-draft updates for policies, logs, attestations, and review
  calendars
- Build an organized exam-readiness folder with a request list and status
  tracker
- Run one mock regulator-style document request / Q&A session
- Provide a prioritized remediation memo for the principal or CCO

Suggested buyer:

- Small RIAs
- Small broker-dealer affiliated offices
- Hybrid firms with limited in-house compliance bandwidth
- Breakaway or newly operational firms that have basic documents but weak
  maintenance discipline

Why this offer is strong:

- Pain is obvious and recurring
- Buyers understand the value quickly
- Work can be templated
- AI meaningfully reduces prep time
- It does not require you to publish constantly

## Why This Beats Other Options

Compared with broad consulting:

- Easier to describe
- Easier to price
- Less scope creep
- Easier to systematize with AI and templates

Compared with compliance software:

- Faster to launch
- Lower technical risk
- Better fit for limited weekly hours
- Lets you sell judgment plus execution, not just tooling

Compared with content or courses:

- Better fit for your stated preferences
- More likely to convert from warm relationships
- Higher trust and pricing per client

## How AI Actually Helps

Use AI behind the scenes, not as the product promise.

High-value internal uses:

- Convert notes and existing manuals into draft updated procedures
- Compare current docs against your internal checklist and flag likely gaps
- Create tailored request lists, annual review outlines, and testing plans
- Summarize meeting notes into action items and evidence needs
- Standardize naming, indexing, and folder structures for exam readiness
- Draft client-facing remediation memos from your marked-up observations

Keep the human role explicit:

- You define the checklist
- You review every output
- You make final judgment calls
- Client signs off on adoption and legal interpretation goes to counsel where
  needed

That positioning keeps AI as a speed layer, not an unauthorized substitute for
professional judgment.

## Legal / Regulatory Guardrails

To stay conservative, structure the business around these rules:

- Do **not** market the service as legal advice unless you are licensed to
  provide it
- Do **not** hold yourself out as outsourced CCO, supervisor, or principal
  unless the role is legally permitted and you intentionally accept that
  responsibility
- Do **not** let AI generate final client work product without your review
- Do **not** promise regulatory outcomes or "regulator-proof" compliance
- Use a clear engagement letter stating scope, limits, assumptions, and client
  responsibility for implementation
- Refer legal interpretation questions, entity-specific legal issues, and edge
  cases to securities counsel
- Maintain strong confidentiality and vendor diligence for any AI tools touching
  client information
- Minimize sensitive data exposure; prefer redaction and least-privilege
  workflows
- Keep an audit trail of what was drafted by AI and what you reviewed/approved
  if your workflow becomes more automated

This is not formal legal advice, but it is the safest commercial posture for the
type of opportunity you described.

## Go-To-Market Without Content

Use a private, relationship-led motion:

- Reconnect with warm contacts and ask who is underwater on annual reviews,
  documentation cleanup, or exam prep
- Offer a paid pilot with a fixed scope and turnaround
- Show before/after samples using sanitized mock materials
- Build referral partners among securities attorneys, compliance consultants
  with overflow work, and operations providers

Simple outreach angle:

"I’m offering a narrow compliance documentation and exam-readiness service for
small firms that need their records, procedures, and evidence cleaned up fast. I
use AI internally to speed drafting and organization, but all judgment and final
review stay with me. If you know a firm behind on annual review or worried about
an exam, I’d be happy to scope a fixed-fee cleanup."

## Practical Packaging

Start with only 2 offers:

1. Fixed-fee tune-up project
2. Small monthly maintenance retainer

Example structure:

- Project: documentation tune-up + mock exam pack
- Retainer: monthly updates, testing calendar, evidence hygiene, and on-call
  document support

This keeps sales simple and fits limited hours.

## Biggest Risks

- Scope drift into bespoke consulting or legal interpretation
- Clients expecting you to own implementation instead of support it
- Overusing AI on sensitive materials without sufficient controls
- Taking on firms with weak economics or high regulatory messiness

You can reduce risk by staying narrow, using clear contracts, and declining
messy edge cases early.

## Bottom Line

The best opportunity is **a narrow, AI-assisted compliance documentation and
mock-exam readiness service for small financial firms**.

It fits your experience, works with warm contacts, avoids the need for public
content creation, can be delivered in limited weekly hours, and uses AI in a way
that is genuinely helpful without forcing you into a legally aggressive
position.

If you pursue only one thing, pursue that.
