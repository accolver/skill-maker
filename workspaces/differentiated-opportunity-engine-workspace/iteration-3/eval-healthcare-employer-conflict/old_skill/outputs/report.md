## Differentiation Profile

- Skills and domain experience: Staff-level engineering judgment, deep
  operational knowledge of denial management and prior authorization workflows,
  understanding of where provider groups leak margin, and enough AI/automation
  skill to turn messy policy/process work into repeatable software.
- Assets and access advantages: Strong problem selection advantage in a
  high-value niche. Knows the operational language of revenue cycle, appeals,
  auth friction, and payer/provider workflow bottlenecks.
- Distribution and network leverage: Weak direct distribution by design. Cannot
  use employer customers, does not want consulting or sales calls, and has only
  about 4 hours per week. Must prefer self-serve, async distribution through
  content, communities, search, marketplaces, and lightweight outbound that does
  not rely on calls.
- Constraints: No PHI, no employer confidential data, no contact with employer
  customers, no consulting-heavy model, no sales-call-dependent motion, limited
  weekly time.
- Credibility signals: Staff engineer in a healthcare claims startup with
  firsthand workflow knowledge in a costly niche.
- Unfair advantages: Better than a generic AI founder at identifying which
  denial and prior auth problems are expensive enough to buy, which workflows
  are painful enough to automate, and which product promises sound credible to
  operators.

## Best Bet

Best bet: `Specialty-Specific Denial Leakage Analyzer: Toolkit to Async SaaS`.

Why this is the best near-term path:

- It fits the strongest edge: identifying where money is lost in denial and
  prior auth workflows.
- It can start legally with public knowledge plus buyer-supplied de-identified
  exports, with no PHI required.
- It does not require consulting or sales calls. The initial offer can be bought
  and used asynchronously.
- It matches a 4-hour-per-week schedule better than a services business.
- It compounds into a defensible asset: taxonomy, templates, decision rules, and
  eventually product telemetry.

Pressure test:

- Demand clarity: Strong. Denials and auth delays map directly to lost cash and
  staff time.
- Distribution fit: Moderate, not great. The business needs a narrow ICP and
  specialty focus so search, communities, and async demos can work.
- Execution difficulty: Moderate. A spreadsheet/template-first version is easy;
  a robust SaaS should wait until repeated usage proves the workflow.
- Compliance and platform risk: Manageable if the product uses only public payer
  rules, generic workflow knowledge, and de-identified user uploads. Do not
  touch PHI, proprietary payer contracts, or employer confidential material.
- Service-first wedge?: Not ideal here. The usual service wedge would improve
  learning speed, but the user's no-calls and no-consulting constraints make an
  async paid toolkit the better first wedge.

Recommended narrow positioning:

- Pick one specialty with painful prior auth and denial complexity such as
  imaging, infusion, behavioral health, or DME.
- Sell a single promise: "Find your top preventable denial/auth leakage patterns
  from a de-identified export in 15 minutes."
- Require uploads to be fully de-identified or start with a manual CSV template
  containing no patient identifiers.

Legal guardrails:

- Use only public payer policies, public CMS guidance, original frameworks, and
  customer-provided de-identified data.
- Do not reuse employer documents, internal heuristics copied from employer
  systems, or any non-public customer knowledge.
- Do not target current employer customers.
- Review employment agreement, invention assignment, moonlighting, and
  non-solicit terms before launch.

## 7-Day Validation Plan

Day 1:

- Choose one specialty and one buyer: for example, independent imaging centers
  and their billing managers.
- Write a one-sentence promise, a landing page headline, and a strict legal
  scope statement: no PHI, no employer data, no calls required.

Day 2:

- Create the v0 artifact: spreadsheet template, denial category library, and AI
  prompt pack for classifying denials and recommended actions.
- Include sample rows generated from synthetic data only.

Day 3:

- Publish a simple landing page with Stripe or waitlist plus a 2-minute async
  demo.
- Create one short teardown article: "The 7 denial/auth leakage patterns most
  likely to be preventable in [specialty]."

Day 4:

- Post in compliant async channels: specialty billing forums, LinkedIn,
  healthcare operations communities, and founder/operator groups. Do not contact
  employer customers.
- Offer a paid beta at a low introductory price for the toolkit, not a
  consultation.

Day 5:

- Message 15 to 25 non-employer-adjacent prospects asynchronously with a short
  note and landing page. No calls. Ask one binary question: "Would this be worth
  paying for if it worked on a de-identified export?"

Day 6:

- Collect objections and usage friction. Revise the taxonomy, spreadsheet
  inputs, and output format based on what confuses people.
- If possible, get 1 to 3 de-identified sample files from buyers or create a
  structured intake form they can fill instead.

Day 7:

- Decide based on signals: preorders, paid betas, strong written interest, or
  repeated requests for the same output.
- If signals are positive, scope the smallest recurring product: upload CSV,
  classify leakage, output prioritized actions.

Success threshold for week 1:

- At least 3 strong written expressions of intent from target buyers outside
  employer conflicts.
- At least 1 paid beta, preorder, or equivalent commitment.
- Evidence that the same narrow output is repeatedly valued.

## Build Spec

Product shape:

- Phase 1: Self-serve toolkit sold asynchronously.
- Phase 2: Lightweight web app for de-identified denial/auth export analysis.

Core v0 features:

- CSV template with required non-PHI fields only.
- Denial/auth taxonomy for one specialty.
- Rule-based plus LLM-assisted classification of preventable leakage patterns.
- Output report with top leakage categories, likely root causes, and suggested
  interventions.
- Prompt pack and SOP snippets for appeals, documentation checks, and auth
  workflow cleanup.

Suggested stack:

- Static landing page plus Stripe payment link.
- Simple app in a familiar full-stack framework.
- File upload with explicit de-identification requirements.
- SQLite or Postgres for lightweight storage.
- LLM calls only for summarization/classification, with deterministic rules
  around allowed fields.

Data and compliance requirements:

- No PHI fields accepted.
- No storage of raw files unless necessary; prefer ephemeral processing.
- Terms page stating that uploads must be de-identified and that the product is
  operational guidance, not legal advice or billing guarantee.

First product milestone:

- One specialty.
- One upload format.
- One output report.
- One paid async offer.

## Kill Criteria

- Stop if no one will pay or preorder after 2 weeks of narrow outreach and async
  distribution.
- Stop if buyers only want bespoke consulting, meetings, or hands-on services.
- Stop if the only viable product requires PHI or employer-conflicted access to
  be useful.
- Stop if the workflow cannot produce a clearly valuable output from
  de-identified data.
- Stop if legal review of employment terms suggests meaningful IP, moonlighting,
  or non-solicit risk for this category.
- Pivot to the payer policy tracker path if buyers respond to workflow guidance
  but resist data upload.
