### The Critical Insight

$3,300/month is the minimum guaranteed income they need from their assets to
cover essential expenses for life. This is the "paycheck" portion. The question
is: how much of their assets needs to be allocated to produce this guaranteed
income, and what's left over for growth ("playchecks") and liquidity?

### Sequence of Returns Risk — Robert's Worst Nightmare

This is the conversation you need to have with Robert, because it validates his
2008 fear with math:

**Scenario A: No market crash**

- $780,000 at 6% average return for 4 years (until both have SS) = ~$985,000
- Minus withdrawals of ~$57,000/year × 4 = $228,000
- Approximate remaining: ~$757,000

**Scenario B: 2008 repeats in year 1**

- $780,000 drops 30% = $546,000
- Minus $57,000 withdrawal in year 1 = $489,000
- Even if market recovers 20%, 15%, 10% in years 2-4…
- Approximate remaining: ~$580,000 — they've lost nearly $200,000 compared to
  Scenario A
- AND they've locked in losses by withdrawing during the downturn

This is **sequence of returns risk** — the mathematical reality behind Robert's
fear. A 30% drop in year 1 of retirement is devastating because you're selling
shares at the bottom to fund living expenses. Even if the market recovers,
you've permanently reduced your asset base.

**This is your most powerful talking point.** Robert already lived this. When
you show him the math, he'll see that his fear is not irrational — it's
mathematically justified. And the solution is to guarantee the income floor so
he never has to sell shares in a down market.

### Product Recommendations — Detailed Analysis

#### 1. Fixed Indexed Annuity (FIA) with Guaranteed Lifetime Withdrawal Benefit (GLWB) — PRIMARY RECOMMENDATION

**Why this fits Robert & Carol:**

- 0% floor addresses his 2008 fear directly — he can NEVER lose money from
  market drops
- Income rider provides guaranteed lifetime income to fill the $3,300/month gap
- Joint-life option ensures Carol continues receiving income if Robert dies
  first
- Index-linked growth gives them inflation-fighting upside without downside risk
- Tax-deferred growth on the 401(k) rollover continues inside the FIA

**Proposed structure:**

- **Source of funds:** 401(k) rollover to IRA, then fund FIA
  (~$450,000-$500,000)
- **Income rider:** Joint-life GLWB, deferral period 4-5 years (until Robert is
  66-67)
- **Expected guaranteed income:** At 5% GLWB rate on
  ~$500K with 7-8% simple roll-up for 4-5 years, income benefit base could grow to ~$640,000-$700,000
- **Guaranteed monthly income:** ~$2,667-$2,917/month (joint-life)
- **Combined with SS:** $2,400 (Robert) + $1,100 (Carol) + ~$2,800 (FIA) =
  ~$6,300/month — close to covering $6,800 expenses

**Important notes:**

- Must show GUARANTEED values, not hypothetical projections
- Run illustrations from at least 2-3 carriers for comparison
- Typical surrender period 7-10 years — suitable given their time horizon
- 10% free annual withdrawal provides some liquidity

#### 2. MYGA (Multi-Year Guaranteed Annuity) — SAFE MONEY / BRIDGE STRATEGY

**Why this fits:**

- Park some money safely at guaranteed rate (currently 4.5-6%) during the 3-4
  year bridge period before both are on SS
- Better rate than savings account with tax deferral
- Provides predictable income source during the transition period
- Addresses Robert's need to "not lose money" with a guaranteed rate

**Proposed structure:**

- **Source of funds:** Portion of 401(k) rollover or Carol's IRA
  ($100,000-$150,000)
- **Term:** 3-5 years
- **Purpose:** Bridge income and safe money bucket
- 10% free withdrawal annually for income needs

#### 3. Hybrid Life Insurance with LTC Rider — ADDRESSING THE MOTHER'S NIGHTMARE

**Why this fits:**

- Directly addresses Robert's #1 emotional concern (mother's LTC experience)
- Asset-based LTC provides benefits whether he needs care or not
- If he needs LTC: policy pays LTC benefits (typically 2-4x the premium)
- If he doesn't need LTC: death benefit goes to Carol/beneficiaries
- If he changes his mind: return of premium option (get your money back)
- Better than traditional LTC insurance: no "use it or lose it" — money is never
  wasted

**Proposed structure:**

- **Source of funds:** Joint brokerage account ($85,000) or portion of IRA
- **Single premium:** $75,000-$100,000
- **LTC monthly benefit:** ~$4,000-6,000/month for 3-6 years (varies by carrier)
- **Death benefit:** If LTC not used, beneficiaries receive $150,000-$200,000+
- **Consider for both Robert AND Carol** — Carol is 59 and could lock in better
  rates now

**Important notes:**

- Requires underwriting — health questions will matter
- Get pricing from multiple carriers
- This is the product that will emotionally resonate most with Robert

#### 4. Consider a SPIA for a Portion of Income (Alternative/Supplement)

If the FIA income rider doesn't fully close the gap, or if you want to
demonstrate the maximum guaranteed income option:

- **SPIA:** $100,000-$150,000 premium, joint-life with 20-year certain
- Could add $500-$700/month in immediate guaranteed income
- The mortality credits make this mathematically optimal for maximizing income
- Consider this for when Robert is closer to 66-67 and both are on SS

### Social Security Optimization Strategy

This is a critical conversation topic for the meeting. Robert is considering
claiming at 62 vs. 66. The math matters:

| Claiming Age | Monthly Benefit | Cumulative by Age 80 | Cumulative by Age 85 | Cumulative by Age 90 |
| ------------ | --------------- | -------------------- | -------------------- | -------------------- |
| 62 (now)     | $1,900          | $410,400             | $524,400             | $638,400             |

| 70           | ~$3,168         | $380,160             | $570,240             | $760,320             |

**Break-even analysis:**

- If Robert has family longevity (his mother lived long enough to need 4 years

**Recommendation to discuss:**

- If Robert's health is good and his mother's longevity suggests he could live
