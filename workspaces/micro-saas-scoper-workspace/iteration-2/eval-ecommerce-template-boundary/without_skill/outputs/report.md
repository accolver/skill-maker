# Ecommerce Reporting Micro-SaaS Scope

## Recommendation

Do not start with a broad ecommerce analytics suite. That is too wide for a
micro-SaaS because it expands into attribution, forecasting, inventory, ad
performance, customer segmentation, finance, and BI workflows at once.

Start with a narrow reporting wedge:

**"Daily profit and revenue reporting for small Shopify brands."**

This is a sharper product because it solves a frequent, painful job with a clear
buyer, a clear data source, and a simpler first version.

## Target User

- Owner-operators and small ecommerce teams
- Primarily Shopify stores doing meaningful weekly order volume
- Users who currently check multiple dashboards or spreadsheets to answer simple
  performance questions

## Core Problem

The user wants quick answers to questions like:

- How much revenue did we generate yesterday?
- What was our gross profit after product costs and shipping?
- Which products or variants drove the result?
- Are we up or down versus the previous period?

Existing tools are often either:

- Too generic and dashboard-heavy
- Too enterprise-oriented
- Too dependent on manual spreadsheet work

## MVP Product Definition

The MVP should focus on one outcome:

**Automatically deliver a trusted daily ecommerce performance report.**

### MVP Features

- Shopify connection
- Daily sync of orders, refunds, discounts, taxes, and shipping revenue
- Simple cost inputs for COGS by product or variant
- One daily summary view with:
  - Revenue
  - Orders
  - Average order value
  - Refunds
  - Gross profit estimate
  - Top products
  - Comparison vs previous day and previous 7-day average
- Daily email or Slack summary
- Date-range report page for recent history

## Explicit Non-Goals

These should be out of scope for the first version:

- Multi-channel analytics across Shopify, Amazon, Meta, Google, TikTok, and
  marketplaces
- Marketing attribution
- Cohort retention analysis
- LTV prediction
- Inventory planning
- Cash flow forecasting
- Customer segmentation engine
- Custom dashboard builder
- Advanced role-based permissions
- Full finance reconciliation

These are attractive, but they turn a small reporting product into a large
analytics platform.

## Why This Boundary Works

- Clear starting data source: Shopify
- Clear recurring value: daily reporting
- Easy to explain: "know yesterday's revenue and profit in one message"
- Narrow enough to ship quickly
- Creates a foundation for later expansion into ads, channel comparisons, or
  deeper profitability analysis

## Differentiation Angle

The product should not compete on having more charts. It should compete on:

- Faster time to insight
- Profit visibility, not just revenue visibility
- Low setup burden
- Push delivery via email/Slack instead of requiring dashboard checking

## Suggested Positioning

"A simple daily revenue and profit report for Shopify brands. Connect your
store, set product costs, and get a clear performance summary every morning."

## MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is successful if a user can:

- Connect Shopify in minutes
- Trust the numbers enough to stop using a manual spreadsheet for the daily
  summary
- Receive a daily report they actually read
- Answer basic performance questions without opening multiple tools

## Reasonable Phase 2 Additions

If the MVP works, expand carefully in this order:

1. Marketing spend inputs or ad platform integrations for blended MER/ROAS views
2. Multi-store support
3. Better product and category profitability breakdowns
4. Weekly executive summaries
5. Simple anomaly alerts

## Final Scope Decision

The proposed broad analytics suite is too large for an initial micro-SaaS.

The right scope is:

**A Shopify-first daily ecommerce reporting tool focused on revenue, refunds,
AOV, and gross profit visibility for small brands.**

That is narrow, valuable, and realistic to build and validate.
