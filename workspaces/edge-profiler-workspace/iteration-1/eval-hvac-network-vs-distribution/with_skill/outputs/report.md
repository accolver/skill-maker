# Profile Summary

This user has real domain familiarity in HVAC distribution operations and direct
adjacency to a local HVAC service business through a family relationship. The
strongest edge is not broad market distribution; it is operational knowledge
plus a plausible path to warm introductions with owners through the brother's
network. The user can also build software and automations, which matters because
it allows rapid creation of internal tools, quoting workflow aids,
dispatch/admin automations, and niche SaaS prototypes without depending on
outside technical talent.

The edge is meaningful but narrow. There is no owned audience, no evidence of an
existing buyer list, and no proof yet that the brother's introductions convert
into repeated sales access. That means the user has a credible wedge for
customer discovery and early pilots, but not durable distribution yet.

# Usable Edge

- 8 years in HVAC distribution operations, which implies real workflow
  familiarity with parts, purchasing, supply chain, counter or back-office
  processes, and how contractors buy and operate.
- Ability to build software and automations directly, which shortens time from
  idea to prototype and enables service-heavy or productized automation offers.
- Warm adjacency to HVAC service owners through the brother, giving a credible
  path to owner conversations and pilot outreach.
- Likely stronger operator empathy than a generic SaaS founder because the user
  understands how HVAC businesses and adjacent suppliers actually work.

# Restricted Edge

- Any confidential knowledge, pricing detail, internal playbooks, customer
  lists, vendor terms, or operational data from past HVAC distribution roles
  should not be treated as monetizable edge unless it is clearly public or
  personally owned.
- The brother's business itself is not automatically a transferable distribution
  asset. Access may be limited to introductions, and goodwill may not scale
  beyond that relationship.

# Fake or Overstated Edge

- "My brother can introduce me to owners" is not durable distribution by itself.
  It is warm access for discovery and initial selling, not a repeatable channel
  yet.
- "8 years in HVAC distribution" is not automatically a moat. It becomes
  commercially useful only when paired with a sharp problem, buyer trust, and a
  way to reach similar buyers repeatedly.
- "I can build software and automations" is not enough on its own. Technical
  skill without distribution still leaves go-to-market as the main bottleneck.

# Weak Spots

- No owned audience.
- No evidence of current independent brand or trust with HVAC owners.
- No proof that owner introductions are numerous, repeatable, or high-intent.
- Unclear whether the user's strongest access is to service owners, office
  managers, dispatch teams, or distributor-side operators.
- Unclear whether the user wants a services business, productized automation
  offer, or vertical SaaS business.

# Follow-Up Questions

- How many HVAC owners can the brother realistically introduce in the next 30 to
  60 days?
- Are those owners residential, commercial, or mixed, and how large are their
  businesses?
- Do you have direct relationships from your distribution years that are
  personally owned and legally usable?
- Which workflows do you understand best: quoting, purchasing, inventory,
  dispatch, maintenance agreements, technician scorecards, collections, or
  something else?
- Do you want to start with a done-for-you automation service or build a
  repeatable product from day one?
