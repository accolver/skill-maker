# HVAC Missed-Call Recovery Concierge: 7-Day Validation Plan

## Best Bet

Offer small HVAC businesses a "missed-call recovery concierge": when the office
misses an inbound call, the service automatically texts the lead within 60
seconds, qualifies the need, captures job details, and hands off booked
appointments or hot callbacks to the owner/dispatcher.

Why this is a strong bet:

- HVAC owners already lose revenue from missed calls, especially after hours,
  weekends, and during busy dispatch windows.
- The pain is easy to understand in dollars: one recovered install, repair, or
  maintenance booking can justify the service.
- The user has warm owner access through a family member, which makes fast
  conversations and honest feedback more likely within 7 days.
- Commitment can be tested without building a full product by using mockups,
  manual concierge fulfillment, and a narrowly scoped pilot ask.

## Core Hypothesis

If HVAC owners believe missed calls are costing them enough revenue, they will
commit quickly to a simple recovery service by doing at least one of the
following within 7 days:

- agreeing to a live pilot,
- sharing recent missed-call volume or call logs,
- introducing the buyer/decision-maker,
- or paying / signing a letter of intent / placing a refundable deposit.

## Ideal Customer Profile

- Owner-led or family-run HVAC company
- 3 to 20 techs or roughly 2 to 15 office/field staff total
- Inbound phone is the primary lead channel
- No 24/7 live answering coverage
- Feels operational pain during nights, weekends, seasonal spikes, or lunch-hour
  gaps

## Fastest Offer Framing

Position it as revenue recovery, not AI.

Suggested one-line pitch: "When your shop misses a call, we text the customer
immediately, qualify the job, and send back a booked job or callback-ready lead
before they call the next HVAC company."

## What To Validate

The main question is not "do they like it?" It is "will they commit?"

Priority signals, strongest to weakest:

1. Pays a setup fee, pilot fee, or refundable deposit
2. Agrees to start a pilot on a real business line this week
3. Shares call logs / missed-call counts / booking workflow without resistance
4. Makes an intro to office manager or dispatcher and schedules follow-up
5. Verbally says it is valuable, but gives no access, no data, and no next step

## 7-Day Execution Plan

### Day 1: Prepare the test

- Create a 1-page overview with:
  - problem,
  - how the service works,
  - sample recovery text flow,
  - pilot promise,
  - pilot ask.
- Define a very small pilot:
  - missed calls during one time window,
  - one phone number,
  - manual concierge fulfillment is acceptable.
- Decide the ask before interviews:
  - best ask: $250 to $500 paid pilot,
  - fallback ask: refundable $100 deposit or signed pilot agreement.

### Day 2: Conduct 3 to 5 owner conversations

- Start with the warm family connection first.
- Ask for specifics:
  - How many calls do you miss in a busy week?
  - What happens after hours?
  - How often do callers move to the next company?
  - What is a booked repair/install worth?
  - Who owns the phone/dispatch workflow?
- Do not over-explain technology.
- Show the sample flow and move quickly to the pilot ask.

### Day 3: Tighten positioning from objections

- Note repeated objections such as:
  - "we already text back manually"
  - "CSR handles this"
  - "after-hours only matters"
  - "I need my office manager involved"
- Narrow the offer if needed:
  - after-hours only,
  - weekends only,
  - install leads only,
  - maintenance plan callbacks only.

### Day 4: Run a manual concierge simulation

- For the friendliest prospect, simulate the service manually.
- Use a missed-call scenario and draft the exact text exchange.
- Deliver the output they would receive:
  - caller name,
  - issue summary,
  - urgency,
  - preferred appointment window,
  - callback recommendation.
- The goal is to make the value tangible before automation exists.

### Day 5: Ask for commitment

- Convert the strongest conversations into a real next step.
- Ask one clear question:
  - "If we put this on your line next week, will you test it for a paid pilot?"
- Accept only concrete next steps as validation.

### Day 6: Re-contact maybes

- Follow up with owners who expressed pain but stalled.
- Use a short message with a deadline:
  - "I’m choosing 1 to 2 HVAC pilot partners this week. If recovering missed
    calls is a priority, can we lock a 20-minute setup call by Friday?"

### Day 7: Score the result

- Review all conversations against commitment signals.
- Decide: proceed, narrow, or stop.

## Interview Script

Use this structure:

1. "How do missed calls get handled today?"
2. "When do you miss the most calls?"
3. "What does one lost job usually cost you?"
4. "If a caller gets a text back in under a minute and you get the job details,
   what would that be worth?"
5. "Would you test this on your real line next week if we kept the pilot
   simple?"

## Success Criteria For 7 Days

Proceed only if at least one strong signal appears:

- 1 paid pilot,
- or 2 prospects willing to start a live pilot with operational access,
- or 3 prospects willing to share logs and schedule implementation discussions
  with the actual operator.

Weak validation only:

- compliments,
- feature suggestions,
- "circle back later",
- general claims that it sounds useful.

## Recommended Pilot Design

- Scope: after-hours and missed inbound calls only
- Fulfillment: manual concierge behind the scenes is fine
- Deliverable: instant text follow-up plus structured lead summary to
  owner/dispatcher
- Pricing test: $250 to $500 for a 2-week pilot, credited toward future service
  if successful

This keeps the test focused on whether owners will pay to recover revenue, not
whether you can build the full system immediately.

## Risks

- Owners may say yes to the concept but avoid giving phone access.
- Office managers or dispatchers may be the practical gatekeepers.
- Existing answering services may reduce urgency in some shops.
- Missed-call pain may be seasonal; urgency could vary if demand is currently
  soft.

## Recommendation

This is a strong 7-day validation candidate because the problem is painful,
measurable, and easy to demo manually. The right goal is not broad discovery.
The goal is one concrete commitment from a real HVAC owner: paid pilot, deposit,
or operational access to run a live test.
