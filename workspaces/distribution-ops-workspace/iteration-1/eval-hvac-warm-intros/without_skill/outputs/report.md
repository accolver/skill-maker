# HVAC Missed-Call Recovery Concierge: Channel Plan

## Goal

Use a warm family-member introduction to start conversations with local HVAC
owners about a missed-call recovery concierge service that helps convert
unanswered calls into booked jobs.

## Offer Framing

- Core offer: when the shop misses a call, a trained concierge immediately
  texts/calls back, qualifies the lead, and pushes for booking.
- Outcome language: fewer lost leads, more booked estimates/service calls, no
  need to add front-desk headcount.
- Best promise to test: "recover revenue from calls you already paid to
  generate."

## Ideal Prospect

- Owner-operated or locally managed HVAC businesses.
- 3-30 techs or a meaningful call volume where missed calls happen after hours,
  during peak season, or while staff are on other lines.
- Shops already spending on Google Local Services Ads, PPC, SEO, yard signs, or
  referral traffic.
- Pain indicators: owner answers phones personally, complaints about admin load,
  slow callbacks, or heavy seasonal spikes.

## Primary Channel: Warm Family Intro

This is the highest-leverage path because trust matters more than reach for a
service offer.

### Intro source role

- Family member makes the initial bridge.
- Position it as a practical revenue conversation, not a sales pitch.
- Ask for permission-based intros only.

### Intro script for family member

"I know someone helping local service businesses recover missed calls and turn
them into booked jobs. It seemed relevant because HVAC owners lose good leads
when the phone is tied up or after-hours calls come in. Open to a short intro?"

### Intro format priority

1. Group text with owner + you.
2. Personal text to owner, then handoff.
3. Email intro if that is how they normally communicate.
4. Phone intro only if the relationship is especially close.

## Owner Contact Sequence

### Step 1: Warm intro message

Once introduced, reply fast with a low-friction note:

"Thanks for the intro. I work on missed-call recovery for local service
businesses, especially shops where every inbound lead matters. If you ever have
calls going unanswered during the day, after hours, or during busy stretches, I
can show you a simple way to recover and book more of them. Worth a 15-minute
chat next week?"

### Step 2: Discovery call

Goal: confirm pain, volume, and urgency.

Questions:

- How are missed calls handled today?
- What happens after hours or when the line is busy?
- Who is responsible for callbacks?
- Roughly how many inbound calls do you think go unanswered in a normal week?
- Which calls matter most: emergency service, install estimates, maintenance, or
  all of the above?
- Are you already paying for lead flow that might be going to waste?

### Step 3: Simple ROI framing

Keep the math easy:

- Missed calls per week x contact rate x booking rate x average job value.
- Show downside of inaction, not just upside.
- Example: if 15 calls are missed weekly and even 2-3 become booked jobs, the
  service may pay for itself quickly.

### Step 4: Pilot close

Offer a small pilot instead of a big commitment.

- 14-30 day test.
- One number or one service area if needed.
- Weekly summary of missed calls recovered, conversations started, and jobs
  booked.

## Supporting Channels

### 1. Referral expansion from first owner conversations

- Ask each warm-contact owner for 1-2 similar operators if the conversation goes
  well.
- Best ask: "Who else in HVAC or home services do you know who probably deals
  with the same missed-call issue?"

### 2. Local credibility assets

- One-page PDF or short doc: what the service is, how it works, pilot structure,
  and example ROI.
- Short case-style narrative, even if initially framed as a hypothetical example
  for HVAC.
- Simple FAQ addressing call handling, booking workflow, after-hours coverage,
  and customer experience.

### 3. Follow-up SMS

Useful because owners often ignore email.

Template: "Circling back on missed-call recovery. If unanswered calls are
slipping through during busy periods, I can outline a simple pilot for your
shop. Want me to send the 3-bullet version?"

### 4. Email recap

Use only as support after text or call.

- Subject: `Idea to recover missed HVAC leads`
- Body should summarize pain, expected benefit, and pilot next step in under 120
  words.

## Messaging Angles To Test

- Revenue recovery: convert lost inbound demand into booked work.
- Speed-to-lead: first business to respond often wins the job.
- After-hours capture: nights and weekends are where high-intent calls get lost.
- Staff relief: front office does not need to carry the whole load.
- Paid lead protection: do not waste ad spend on missed phones.

## Objections And Responses

- "We already call people back." Response: "Makes sense. The key question is how
  fast and how consistently, especially when the office is slammed or closed."
- "My CSR handles it." Response: "This is usually most valuable when the CSR is
  overloaded, on lunch, off-hours, or on another line."
- "I don't know if we miss enough calls." Response: "That is exactly why a short
  pilot works. It tells you whether there is real leakage without a big
  commitment."
- "I don't want customers getting a weird outsourced experience." Response: "The
  service has to sound like your shop, follow your rules, and focus on fast
  helpful recovery, not generic call-center scripts."

## Recommended Funnel

1. Build a list of warm-reachable owners through the family member.
2. Prioritize by trust strength, business size, and visible lead volume.
3. Ask for 5-10 permission-based intros.
4. Respond to each intro within 10 minutes when possible.
5. Run a 15-minute discovery call.
6. Send a same-day recap with pilot framing.
7. Follow up on day 2 and day 7 by text.
8. Ask interested owners for one adjacent referral.

## Simple Tracking

Track in a sheet or CRM:

- Owner name
- Intro source
- Date introduced
- Contact status
- Discovery call booked
- Main pain point
- Estimated missed-call volume
- Pilot offered
- Pilot accepted
- Referral given

## Success Metrics

- Intro acceptance rate.
- Discovery-call booking rate from intros.
- Pilot conversion rate from discovery calls.
- Referrals generated per owner conversation.
- Estimated recovered revenue during pilot.

## Near-Term Execution Recommendation

- Start only with warm intros, not cold outbound.
- Keep the first ask to a 15-minute conversation, not a full pitch.
- Sell a pilot, not an annual contract.
- Optimize around speed, trust, and concrete revenue recovery.
- After 3-5 owner conversations, refine the script based on the most common
  objections and strongest ROI story.
