# Channel Plan: HVAC Missed-Call Recovery Concierge

## Channel Thesis

The strongest starting channel is warm introductions from the family member to
HVAC owners they already know. This offer solves a painful, easy-to-understand
revenue leak for local service businesses, and owner trust matters more than
broad awareness at this stage. Warm access should produce faster signal, higher
reply rates, and more honest objections than cold outreach or generic ads.

## Primary Channel

**Primary channel:** Warm owner intros through the family member's local
network.

**Why this channel wins first:**

- Highest trust relative to other available options
- Direct owner access instead of fighting through front-desk screening
- Fast path to discovery calls and pilot offers
- Strong fit for a concierge-style service that may need explanation and
  objection handling

**Target buyer profile:**

- Owner-operated or founder-led HVAC companies
- Roughly 3-20 techs or a comparable local footprint
- High inbound call dependence
- No dedicated after-hours booking process, weak call handling, or obvious
  missed-call leakage

**Offer framing for intro:**

"We help HVAC shops recover jobs that slip through when calls go unanswered or
follow-up is slow. We handle missed-call text-back and recovery as a concierge
service, so owners can book more jobs without changing their whole stack."

## Supporting Channels

**1. Referral compounding from first warm pilots**

Use each good owner conversation and each successful pilot to request 1-2
introductions to similar owners. This extends the same trust-based channel
instead of introducing a new motion.

**2. Lightweight proof assets for intro conversion**

Use a short one-pager, sample recovery workflow, and simple ROI calculator to
help the family member make intros and help owners understand the offer quickly.

**3. Narrow local follow-up outbound to referred-adjacent accounts**

Only after initial warm conversations. This is not broad cold outreach. It is
targeted follow-up to owners in the same market or peer set where the warm
source can credibly mention context.

## Asset Checklist

- 2-sentence intro blurb the family member can forward by text or email
- One-page offer sheet covering problem, service, setup, and pilot structure
- Simple ROI model based on missed calls, booking rate, average ticket, and
  margin assumptions
- 10-15 minute discovery call script
- Pilot offer with clear scope, timeline, and price or rev-share structure
- Objection handling notes for common concerns: existing receptionist, software
  overlap, after-hours volume, owner skepticism
- Case-study template for first pilot results
- Referral ask script for satisfied owners

## Metrics And Kill Criteria

**Success metrics for the first 30 days:**

- 10-15 warm introductions requested from the family member
- At least 6 actual owner conversations created from those requests
- At least 4 discovery calls completed
- At least 2 pilot offers made
- At least 1 pilot closed
- Warm intro to meeting rate: target 40%+
- Meeting to pilot rate: target 30%+

**Kill criteria for this channel test:**

- Fewer than 5 intros are realistically available from the current warm source
- Fewer than 25% of warm intros turn into owner conversations after 10 intros
- Fewer than 2 discovery calls happen after 10 intros
- Repeated signal that owners do not view missed-call recovery as urgent enough
  to test
- The family member is willing in theory but not actually sending intros within
  a 2-week window

If these thresholds miss, pause expansion and reassess the wedge, intro source
quality, or offer framing before adding more channels.

## Execution Sequence

1. Confirm the family member's actual intro capacity and list 10-15 HVAC owners
   they can credibly reach.
2. Prepare the forwardable intro blurb, one-pager, and ROI calculator.
3. Ask for 5 intros first, not the full list, to get early signal quickly.
4. Run short owner discovery calls focused on missed-call pain, current
   workflow, and booking leakage.
5. Convert the best-fit owners into a tightly scoped pilot.
6. Capture baseline metrics and early results from the pilot.
7. Use positive signal to request second-degree referrals from the first owners.
8. Only layer in narrow outbound follow-up once warm proof exists.
