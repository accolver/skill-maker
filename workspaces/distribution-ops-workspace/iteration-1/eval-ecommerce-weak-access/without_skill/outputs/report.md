# Channel Plan: Ecommerce Reporting Product for Merchants

## Situation

- Offer: an ecommerce reporting product for merchants.
- Founder strength: strong workflow and operations knowledge.
- Constraint: weak buyer access, no audience, limited built-in distribution.
- Implication: avoid channels that require brand trust, big budgets, or warm
  network density at the start.

## Core Strategy

Use high-intent, proximity-to-pain channels first. The product should be sold
where merchants already ask reporting questions, compare tools, or hire
operators to solve analytics problems.

Primary wedge:

- Position the product as a faster, clearer alternative to spreadsheet
  reporting, agency-built dashboards, and confusing BI setups.
- Sell outcomes, not software: weekly clarity, margin visibility, SKU/channel
  performance, and faster decisions.
- Start with service-assisted distribution: lightweight setup, reporting audit,
  or dashboard migration bundled into the first sale.

## Best Channels Ranked

## 1. Partner Channel: Agencies, Fractional Operators, and Ecommerce Consultants

Why this fits:

- Partners already have merchant trust.
- One relationship can unlock many buyers.
- Founder workflow expertise is highly credible to operators and consultants.

How to execute:

- Build a partner list of small ecommerce agencies, fractional CFOs, paid media
  agencies, retention agencies, and Shopify consultants.
- Offer a white-label or partner-friendly version with clear referral economics.
- Lead with a simple pitch: "Your clients need cleaner reporting without more
  spreadsheet labor."
- Create a short partner deck with sample reports, onboarding flow, and client
  fit criteria.

Target partner profile:

- Serves 10 to 100 SMB ecommerce brands.
- Has recurring client relationships.
- Frequently answers reporting, attribution, or performance questions.

Expected outcome:

- Best early scalable channel.
- Slower first conversion than direct outreach, but stronger compounding if a
  few partners adopt.

## 2. Founder-Led Outbound to High-Fit Merchants

Why this fits:

- No audience required.
- Fastest path to live conversations.
- Lets messaging sharpen from real objections.

How to execute:

- Narrow ICP to one segment first: for example Shopify brands doing $1M to $20M
  GMV with 3 to 20 person teams.
- Prioritize merchants likely feeling reporting pain: multi-channel sellers,
  brands with repeat purchase motion, teams using multiple apps, brands hiring
  analysts or media buyers.
- Use short, problem-specific outbound referencing likely pain: channel
  profitability, inventory blind spots, CAC/LTV confusion, or weekly executive
  reporting burden.
- Offer a teardown or reporting audit instead of a generic demo.

List sources:

- Shopify ecosystem directories.
- Agency client pages.
- LinkedIn searches for ecommerce operators.
- Job posts mentioning reporting, analytics, lifecycle marketing, or BI.

Expected outcome:

- Best channel for first 5 to 10 customers.
- Labor-intensive, but strong for learning.

## 3. Communities Where Operators Ask for Help

Why this fits:

- Merchants openly discuss reporting pain.
- Founder can win by being useful before selling.
- Lower barrier than building a broad audience from scratch.

Where to show up:

- Shopify and DTC Slack groups.
- Ecommerce subreddits and niche operator communities.
- LinkedIn comments around ecommerce operations and finance content.
- Founder/operator roundtables and virtual events.

How to execute:

- Answer questions with concrete frameworks, report templates, and metric
  definitions.
- Turn repeated answers into reusable content assets.
- Offer free audits selectively when the pain is explicit.
- Avoid generic posting cadence; focus on high-signal replies and direct
  follow-up.

Expected outcome:

- Good for credibility and pipeline support.
- Usually not enough alone, but effective paired with outbound and partnerships.

## 4. Problem-Led Content With Capture

Why this fits:

- Content alone is slow with no audience.
- But tactical assets can convert if tied to specific merchant problems and
  capture intent.

Best content types:

- "Weekly ecommerce KPI pack" template.
- "What merchants should track beyond platform ROAS."
- "How to spot false growth from blended reporting."
- Benchmarks or report teardown examples.

Distribution approach:

- Publish on LinkedIn and founder profile.
- Repurpose into community answers and outbound follow-ups.
- Gate only the highest-value artifact, such as a reporting template or audit
  checklist.

Expected outcome:

- Supports trust, demos, and partner enablement.
- Not the primary acquisition engine in the first phase.

## 5. App Ecosystem and Marketplace Surfaces

Why this fits:

- Buyers in app ecosystems are already solution-aware.
- Useful once onboarding and positioning are clearer.

Examples:

- Shopify App Store if product shape allows it.
- Integration partner directories.
- Co-marketing with adjacent tools in retention, finance, or inventory.

Expected outcome:

- Medium-term acquisition lever.
- Usually weak at day 1 unless reviews, integrations, or partner pull already
  exist.

## Channels to Avoid Early

- Broad paid social: expensive and low trust for a reporting product.
- SEO-first strategy: too slow without existing authority.
- Large webinars and brand campaigns: weak distribution without audience.
- Enterprise sales motion: long cycles and harder access.
- Generic cold ads to merchants: poor message-market precision early.

## Positioning Guidance

The product should be framed against painful alternatives, not abstract
analytics value.

Best message territories:

- "Replace manual weekly reporting."
- "See true channel and SKU performance in one place."
- "Give operators and founders decision-ready reporting, not dashboard clutter."
- "Cut analyst and agency reporting labor."

Avoid:

- Broad BI language.
- Claims that depend on perfect attribution.
- Messaging that sounds like another dashboard tool.

## 90-Day Plan

## Days 1-30

- Pick one ICP slice.
- Build a list of 100 target merchants and 50 target partners.
- Create 3 outreach angles tied to reporting pain.
- Create 2 core assets: sample report and reporting audit checklist.
- Run founder-led outreach every week.
- Join 3 to 5 operator communities and contribute manually.

Success metric:

- 15 to 20 qualified conversations.

## Days 31-60

- Double down on the best-performing pain angle.
- Convert early learnings into tighter landing page and demo narrative.
- Pilot 3 to 5 partners.
- Publish a small set of tactical content pieces based on live objections.

Success metric:

- First repeatable source of demos, either outbound or partners.

## Days 61-90

- Formalize partner program if early pull exists.
- Turn best content asset into lead capture.
- Build referral asks into onboarding and customer success.
- Test one ecosystem surface such as directory placement or integration
  co-marketing.

Success metric:

- Clear primary channel with acceptable conversion economics.

## Recommended Channel Mix

If only three channels are pursued initially:

1. Founder-led outbound.
2. Agency and consultant partnerships.
3. Community participation plus tactical content reuse.

This mix matches the founder's strength in workflow knowledge, minimizes
dependence on existing audience, and creates both immediate learning and
medium-term leverage.

## Final Recommendation

The best plan is to start with direct merchant outreach and partner-led
distribution, using community participation as a credibility layer and content
as a support asset rather than the core engine. The founder should sell a
painful reporting outcome, bundle setup help early, and treat the first phase as
a search for the narrowest buyer segment with the strongest reporting urgency.
