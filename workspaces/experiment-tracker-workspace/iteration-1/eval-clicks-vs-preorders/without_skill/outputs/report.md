# Experiment Log Summary

## State

The newsletter send generated strong top-of-funnel interest: 800 clicks and
substantial qualitative praise. That suggests the message, audience targeting,
or core idea resonated enough to earn attention and curiosity.

However, the experiment produced zero preorders at the stated price. That means
interest did not convert into purchase intent under the current offer. The main
takeaway is not "no demand," but "no validated demand at this price and offer
structure."

## Interpretation

The signal is mixed but useful:

- Positive signal: people cared enough to click and respond.
- Negative signal: nobody accepted the current buying proposition.
- Most likely gap: the value-to-price equation was not convincing enough for a
  preorder.

Possible reasons include:

- The price is too high for the current perceived value.
- The offer asks for commitment too early.
- The landing page may create curiosity without creating urgency or trust.
- People may like the concept but not understand the concrete outcome well
  enough to buy.

## Recommended Next Experiment

Run a pricing-and-commitment test, not another awareness test.

Recommended experiment:

- Keep the same audience and core positioning.
- Test a lower-friction offer before asking for a full-price preorder.
- Compare the current preorder against one or two alternatives such as:
  - a lower introductory price,
  - a refundable deposit or reservation,
  - a waitlist with explicit pricing feedback capture.

Best next experiment:

Create an A/B test where one variant keeps the original preorder price and the
other uses a lower-commitment offer, such as a small refundable deposit. Measure
conversion rate from click to commitment.

## Why This Experiment

This isolates the main uncertainty: whether the failure came from weak demand or
from excessive purchase friction at the stated price. Since clicks were already
strong, the next step should test willingness to commit, not ability to attract
attention.

## Decision Rule

- If the lower-friction variant converts, demand likely exists but the original
  price/commitment was too aggressive.
- If neither variant converts, the issue is more likely core value proposition,
  audience fit, or problem severity.

## Bottom Line

The experiment shows strong interest but no proof of willingness to pay at the
current preorder price. The next experiment should reduce commitment friction
and directly test price sensitivity.
