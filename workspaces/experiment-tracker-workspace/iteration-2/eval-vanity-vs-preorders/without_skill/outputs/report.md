# Experiment Summary

The send generated strong top-of-funnel interest: about 800 clicks and a lot of
positive qualitative feedback. That means the message, audience, or problem
framing was compelling enough to earn attention.

The key result is still a failed purchase test at the stated price: zero
preorders. The current evidence suggests the bottleneck is not awareness, but
conversion at the pricing and commitment step.

## Current State

- Signal quality is mixed.
- Clicks and praise are encouraging, but they are vanity signals unless they
  lead to paid commitment.
- Zero preorders indicates that the offer, price, timing, trust, or target
  customer fit is not yet strong enough.
- The most likely interpretation is that demand may exist, but not at this exact
  offer and stated price.

## Recommended Next Experiment

Run a pricing and commitment sensitivity test rather than another awareness
test.

Suggested experiment:

- Keep the same audience and core messaging as much as possible.
- Test a lower-friction offer, such as a discounted founding-customer preorder,
  refundable deposit, or limited early-bird price.
- Measure paid commitment, not clicks.
- If possible, compare two price points or offer structures to learn whether the
  issue is price level versus offer design.

## Decision Rule

- If a lower-friction version produces real preorders or deposits, the main
  problem was likely price and/or commitment threshold.
- If conversion is still zero, the problem is deeper than pricing alone and the
  value proposition or target segment should be reworked before more promotion.

## Bottom Line

This experiment shows interest without purchase intent at the current price. The
next experiment should directly test whether reducing price or commitment
unlocks real buying behavior.
