# Offer Thesis

Offer a narrow "compliance evidence concierge" service for small financial firms
that already know they need to produce policy, control, and audit-ready
evidence, but do not have enough internal time to assemble it cleanly. The
promise is not legal advice or compliance sign-off; the promise is a faster,
more organized evidence package and accountability rhythm that helps the firm
walk into reviews, audits, or client diligence with less scramble.

This wedge fits the user's constraints. Warm contacts make a trust-based service
sale more realistic than a cold software pitch, and limited weekly time favors a
tightly scoped concierge workflow over broad consulting.

# ICP

- Buyer: COO, operations lead, compliance manager, or founder at a small
  financial firm
- Segment: Small RIAs, broker-dealer-adjacent firms, fund admins, specialty
  finance shops, or fintech operators with lean teams and recurring diligence or
  audit pressure
- Why now: They have an upcoming audit, client diligence request, SOC/controls
  review, annual compliance cycle, or board/investor request and do not have
  time to chase documents across people and folders
- Painful job to be done: Turn scattered documents, screenshots, policy
  versions, control evidence, and owner follow-ups into a clean evidence trail
  without burning senior team time

# Offer Design

- Primary shape: Concierge hybrid that can later productize
- Why this shape beats the obvious alternative: It beats a pure audit/diagnostic
  because the buyer's pain is not just "tell me what's missing" but "help me
  actually assemble the evidence." It beats software-first because warm contacts
  will buy hands-on help faster than they will adopt a new tool, and the user's
  limited time is better spent running a repeatable manual workflow than
  building product too early.
- Core outcome: A structured evidence package and tracking system for one
  defined compliance scope

Proposed offer name: `Evidence Readiness Sprint`

Deliverables:

- One kickoff call to define the exact review, audit, or diligence scope
- A tailored evidence request list organized by control area or request category
- A shared tracker showing requested items, owners, status, gaps, and follow-ups
- Weekly async nudges and document-chasing support for a fixed sprint window
- Basic organization of submitted materials into a clean folder structure with
  naming conventions
- A gap summary that flags missing, stale, or unclear evidence for the client to
  resolve internally or with counsel/compliance professionals
- A final readiness packet containing the tracker, document index, and
  open-items summary

Scope boundaries:

- Covers evidence collection, organization, tracking, and packaging for one
  defined review cycle
- Does not provide legal advice, regulatory interpretation, or sign-off on
  adequacy
- Does not act as chief compliance officer, outside counsel, or formal auditor
- Does not draft custom legal policies from scratch beyond lightweight
  formatting or organization help
- Limited to a fixed sprint, with additional scopes sold separately

Suggested workflow:

1. Define the review event and evidence categories.
2. Build the request list and tracker.
3. Chase inputs asynchronously with one named client owner.
4. Organize evidence and flag gaps.
5. Deliver the readiness packet and recommend next operational steps.

This design is especially suited to a low-hours operator because much of the
value comes from structure, follow-up, and packaging rather than deep bespoke
analysis. The service can be sold in small batches to warm contacts and later
converted into templates, checklists, and a benchmark product.

# Pricing Hypothesis

- Model: Fixed-fee sprint
- Range: `$2,500-$6,000` per sprint for one review cycle, with an optional
  `$500-$1,500` fast-turn or additional-scope add-on
- Pricing rationale: The value comes from saving expensive internal staff time,
  reducing deadline chaos, and improving review readiness. The lower end fits
  smaller firms with simple evidence scopes; the upper end fits multi-owner
  chases, denser document sets, or higher urgency.
- What would adjust price: Volume of evidence categories, number of
  stakeholders, urgency, and whether the client wants live meeting support
  during the review window

# Rejected Framings

- Broad compliance consulting: Too vague, too labor-intensive, and too easy to
  drift into unbounded advisory work
- Legal-advice framing: Unsafe and misaligned with the constraint to avoid
  regulated-role overreach
- Outsourced CCO/compliance officer service: Creates role confusion and higher
  trust/liability expectations than this wedge should carry
- Software-first evidence platform: Too much build risk and too slow to validate
  compared with a manual concierge offer sold through warm contacts
- Generic AI compliance assistant: Sounds undifferentiated and can create
  accuracy/trust concerns without solving the immediate evidence-assembly
  problem
