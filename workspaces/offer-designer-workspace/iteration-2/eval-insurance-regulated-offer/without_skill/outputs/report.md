# First Sellable Offer: AI Note-Prep Sprint for Solo Insurance Agents

## Recommendation

Start with a small, productized service that helps solo insurance agents turn
warm-contact context into faster, cleaner pre-call notes and follow-up drafts
without crossing into licensed advice.

Recommended first offer:

**Warm Contact Note-Prep Sprint**

Promise: help a solo agent save 2 to 4 hours a week by preparing organized
pre-call notes, question prompts, and compliant follow-up drafts for warm leads
they already know.

## Why This Offer Fits

- The buyer already has warm contacts, so they do not need a lead-generation
  offer first.
- The buyer only has 5 hours a week, so the offer must be narrow, repeatable,
  and easy to deliver.
- "Note-prep help" is operational support, not coverage advice, product
  recommendations, or suitability guidance.
- Solo agents often lose time to admin before and after calls; this offer
  targets that bottleneck.
- A small sprint is easier to sell than a big transformation promise in a
  regulated category.

## Target Buyer

- Solo insurance agent or very small agency
- Has an existing book, referrals, or warm inbound contacts
- Feels buried by follow-up and meeting prep
- Wants help using AI but does not want to build systems alone

## Core Problem

The agent spends too much time turning scattered CRM notes, emails, and past
conversations into usable call prep and post-call follow-up. That slows response
time and reduces consistency.

## Offer Structure

### What They Get

- A lightweight intake to define their current workflow, voice, and boundaries
- Setup of one simple AI-assisted note-prep workflow
- 3 reusable prompt templates:
  - pre-call summary builder
  - discovery-question generator
  - follow-up draft generator
- 10 done-for-you note-prep packs for real warm contacts
- A short SOP showing how to run the workflow in under 10 minutes per contact
- A compliance boundary sheet with approved wording such as "administrative
  support only" and clear exclusions

### Deliverables Per Note-Prep Pack

- contact snapshot from provided source material
- missing-information checklist
- non-advisory discovery questions
- meeting objective options
- follow-up email or text draft for agent review

## Explicit Boundaries

To avoid licensed-advice framing, the offer should clearly exclude:

- policy recommendations
- carrier comparisons
- suitability judgments
- coverage interpretations for a client
- binding, quoting, or application decisions
- anything presented directly to the end consumer as advice

Safe positioning language:

- "meeting preparation support"
- "administrative AI workflow setup"
- "drafting support for agent-reviewed communications"
- "organization of existing client and prospect information"

Avoid language like:

- "close more policies with AI advice"
- "automated recommendations"
- "best coverage suggestions"
- "client-specific insurance guidance"

## Delivery Model

Keep it simple and low-time:

### Week 1

- 30-minute kickoff
- collect 3 to 5 examples of current notes or follow-ups
- set voice and compliance boundaries
- build first prompt set

### Week 2

- produce 10 note-prep packs from the agent's real warm-contact list
- refine templates based on feedback
- deliver SOP and handoff video or written walkthrough

Estimated delivery time for you:

- setup and customization: 2 hours
- producing first 10 packs: 2 hours
- revisions and handoff: 1 hour

Total: about 5 hours

## Pricing

Best first price:

**$300 to $500 one-time sprint**

Why:

- low enough for a fast yes from a solo agent
- high enough to validate demand
- aligned with a 5-hour weekly capacity

Simple anchor:

- If the sprint saves even 2 hours a week, many solo agents will see immediate
  value.

Possible pricing ladder after initial sales:

- Sprint: $300 to $500
- Monthly retainer for ongoing note-prep support: $250 to $600 per month
- Team version later: custom pricing

## Best Sales Angle

Lead with time saved and consistency, not AI sophistication.

Suggested positioning:

"I help solo insurance agents turn messy warm-lead notes into ready-to-use call
prep and follow-up drafts, so they can respond faster without adding admin
time."

## Simple Sales Message

"I offer a two-week Warm Contact Note-Prep Sprint for solo insurance agents. You
send me your existing notes, emails, or CRM context for warm leads, and I set up
a simple AI-assisted workflow plus 10 ready-to-review prep packs and follow-up
drafts. This is admin and meeting-prep support only, not client advice or
coverage recommendations."

## What Makes It Sellable First

- narrow scope
- easy to explain
- outcome is tangible within 2 weeks
- low risk for the buyer
- can be fulfilled manually before any complex automation
- creates obvious upsell paths if buyers want ongoing help

## Risks and Mitigations

### Risk: buyer expects lead generation

Mitigation: state that the offer starts with existing warm contacts only.

### Risk: buyer expects compliance or legal review

Mitigation: state that the agent remains responsible for all final
communications and regulated decisions.

### Risk: custom work expands too far

Mitigation: cap the sprint at 10 contact packs and 1 workflow.

### Risk: unclear ROI

Mitigation: frame success as admin time saved, faster response, and more
consistent follow-up.

## Exact First Offer

**Offer Name:** Warm Contact Note-Prep Sprint

**Target Buyer:** solo insurance agents with warm contacts and limited admin
time

**Format:** 2-week productized service

**Price:** start at $400 one time

**Includes:**

- 1 kickoff call
- 1 AI note-prep workflow
- 3 prompt templates
- 10 note-prep packs
- 1 SOP
- 1 revision round

**Primary Outcome:** save time preparing for conversations and following up with
warm contacts

**Compliance Positioning:** administrative drafting and meeting-prep support
only; all client-facing content reviewed and approved by the licensed agent

## Next Best Step

Sell this first to 2 to 3 agents manually. Track:

- time saved per contact
- turnaround time
- which deliverable they value most
- objections around compliance or scope

That feedback should shape the second version, not more complexity up front.
