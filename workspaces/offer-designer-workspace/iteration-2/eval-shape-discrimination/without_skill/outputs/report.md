# Offer Design: Fastest Path to Paid Validation

## Recommendation

The fastest path to paid validation is a **done-with-you recruiter outreach
sprint**, not a hiring-process audit.

Reason: the founder already has warm access to boutique recruiters, which makes
it possible to test willingness to pay quickly with a concrete, outcome-oriented
service. An audit is easier to explain, but it is more advisory, less urgent,
and usually farther from an immediate buying decision.

## Core Offer

**Name:** Boutique Recruiter Outreach Sprint

**Promise:** In 7 days, the founder helps a recruiter turn their existing
network into a cleaner, more consistent client-outreach motion with sharper
messaging, a targeted prospect list, and live outreach support.

**Positioning:** For boutique recruiters who know they should be doing more
outbound but do not have the time, system, or message discipline to do it
consistently.

## What The Buyer Gets

1. A 60-minute kickoff to understand niche, clients, placements, and current
   outreach.
2. A tightened offer and messaging angle for employers.
3. A small, high-fit prospect list.
4. Outreach copy for email and LinkedIn.
5. A lightweight cadence for follow-up.
6. Hands-on support launching the first wave.
7. End-of-sprint review with results, lessons, and next-step recommendation.

## Why This Beats The Audit For Validation

1. Easier to sell on urgency: "let's help you start conversations this week."
2. Easier to price because the buyer sees deliverables and action.
3. Faster feedback loop on both demand and value perception.
4. Better fit for warm intros because it sounds practical, not abstract.
5. Creates clearer signals for iteration: acceptance rate, objections, meeting
   interest, and willingness to continue.

## Offer Shape

**Format:** Done-with-you sprint

**Length:** 7 days

**Scope guardrails:**

1. One niche or recruiter desk focus.
2. One target client profile.
3. One outreach channel primary, one secondary.
4. One sprint only, not ongoing agency work.

## Suggested Pricing

Use a simple paid pilot price to validate demand quickly.

**Recommended pilot price:** `$500-$1,500`

Pick the exact number based on founder credibility and buyer sophistication. The
goal is not maximum revenue; it is confirming that the market will pay for this
shape of help.

If the founder is worried about friction, use:

**Intro pilot:** `$750` for the first 3 clients

That is high enough to validate seriousness and low enough to reduce buying
hesitation.

## Risk Reversal

Use a light guarantee, not a heavy outcome guarantee.

**Example:** If we finish the sprint and you do not leave with finalized
messaging, a target list, and a live outreach plan you can run immediately, I
will keep working with you until those assets are complete.

This keeps the promise controllable without guaranteeing placements or meetings.

## Sales Narrative

Use this framing in warm conversations:

"I am testing a short, hands-on sprint for boutique recruiters who want more
client conversations but do not have a reliable outbound process. In one week, I
help you sharpen the pitch, build a focused prospect list, and launch outreach
you can actually keep using. I am looking for a few pilot clients before turning
it into a repeatable offer."

## Ideal First Buyers

Prioritize recruiters who are:

1. Independent or small-firm operators.
2. Good at delivery but weak on systematic business development.
3. Already convinced outbound matters.
4. Close enough to revenue pain that a short sprint feels useful now.

Avoid buyers who want broad strategy, branding, or a full sales system rebuild.

## What To Measure

For paid validation, track:

1. Warm intro to call conversion.
2. Call to paid pilot conversion.
3. Price resistance.
4. Which promise gets the strongest reaction.
5. Whether buyers ask for continued help after the sprint.

## Fallback / Secondary Offer

The **hiring-process audit** should be a secondary option, not the lead offer.

Use it only when the prospect resists execution support but still wants help
diagnosing outbound or business-development gaps.

**Role of the audit:**

1. Downsells prospects who are not ready for the sprint.
2. Creates a feeder into the sprint.
3. Helps capture revenue from more analytical buyers.

## Final Decision

Lead with the **done-with-you recruiter outreach sprint**.

It is the best match for the founder's warm-access advantage and the strongest
option for fast paid validation because it is concrete, time-bounded, and close
to a result buyers already care about.
