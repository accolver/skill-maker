# Annuity & Life Insurance Product Knowledge

## Annuity Products

### Fixed Annuities (MYGA — Multi-Year Guaranteed Annuity)

**What it is:** A contract that pays a guaranteed fixed interest rate for a set
period (2-10 years). Think of it as a CD issued by an insurance company.

**Best for:** Conservative clients who want predictable, guaranteed growth with
no market risk.

**Key features:**

- Guaranteed interest rate for the full term (e.g., 5.40% for 3 years)
- Principal is 100% protected
- Tax-deferred growth (no taxes until withdrawal)
- No annual fees
- Typical free withdrawal: 10% of account value per year without penalty
- Surrender charges apply for early withdrawal beyond free amount
- Death benefit = full account value to beneficiaries

**When to recommend:**

- Client has CDs maturing and wants better rates with tax deferral
- Client needs safe money parking for 3-7 years
- Client wants a guaranteed rate without market exposure
- As part of an annuity ladder strategy

**Typical rates (subject to change):** 4.5%-6.0% for 3-5 year terms

**Carrier comparison factors:** Rate, surrender period, AM Best rating, free
withdrawal provision, bailout rate feature, RMD-friendly

### Fixed Indexed Annuities (FIA)

**What it is:** A fixed annuity that earns interest based on the performance of
a market index (e.g., S&P 500), with a guaranteed floor of 0% (never lose money
from market drops).

**Best for:** Clients who want growth potential with principal protection — the
"best of both worlds."

**Key features:**

- Principal guaranteed against market loss (0% floor)
- Interest credited based on index performance, subject to cap, participation
  rate, or spread
- Multiple index strategy options (S&P 500, Nasdaq, custom indices)
- Optional income riders for guaranteed lifetime income
- Tax-deferred growth
- Typical surrender periods: 7-10 years
- Free withdrawal: usually 10% per year

**Crediting methods explained:**

| Method                         | How It Works                             | Example                                     |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Annual point-to-point with cap | Gains capped at a maximum rate           | S&P up 15%, cap 8% → you earn 8%            |
| Participation rate             | You earn a percentage of index gains     | S&P up 10%, 50% participation → you earn 5% |
| Spread/margin                  | Index gain minus a fixed spread          | S&P up 12%, 2% spread → you earn 10%        |
| Monthly sum cap                | Each month's gain is capped, then summed | Monthly cap 2.5%, volatile months help/hurt |

**Income rider details:**

- Added for annual fee (typically 0.75%-1.25% of income benefit base)
- Income benefit base grows at guaranteed roll-up rate (e.g., 7-8% simple)
  during deferral
- Guaranteed lifetime withdrawal benefit (GLWB) starts at 4.5%-6.5% of income
  base depending on age
- Income for life, even if account value reaches zero
- Joint-life option available (slightly lower payout rate)

**When to recommend:**

- Client wants market-linked growth without downside risk
- Client needs guaranteed lifetime income in 5-10 years
- Client is in accumulation phase but risk-averse
- As the "paycheck" portion of a retirement plan

### Single Premium Immediate Annuity (SPIA)

**What it is:** A one-time premium payment that converts immediately into a
guaranteed income stream, starting within 12 months.

**Best for:** Clients who need income NOW and want to maximize payout by
leveraging mortality credits.

**Key features:**

- Highest payout rate of any annuity type (due to mortality credits and
  principal return)
- Income begins within 1-12 months of purchase
- Life-only option = highest payout (but nothing to heirs if you die early)
- Period certain options (10, 15, 20 years) protect against early death
- Life with period certain = most popular (income for life, guaranteed minimum
  to heirs)
- Joint life option for married couples
- Cash refund / installment refund options available

**Payout options:**

| Option                    | Payout Level | Death Benefit                                                     |
| ------------------------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Life only                 | Highest      | $0 — payments stop at death                                       |
| Life with 10-year certain | High         | If death before 10 years, beneficiary gets remaining payments     |
| Life with 20-year certain | Moderate     | If death before 20 years, beneficiary gets remaining payments     |
| Joint life                | Lower        | Continues to surviving spouse for their lifetime                  |
| Cash refund               | Moderate     | If total payments received < premium, beneficiary gets difference |

**When to recommend:**

- Client is already retired and needs income immediately
- Client has a pension gap to fill
- Client wants to "pensionize" a portion of their savings
- Client is healthy and wants to maximize mortality credits

**Key talking points:**

- "You cannot outlive this income — it's guaranteed for life"
- "Social Security is essentially a government SPIA — you already own one"
- "The mortality credits mean you earn more than you could on your own"

### Deferred Income Annuity (DIA)

**What it is:** A SPIA purchased years before income begins. You pay now, income
starts at a future date (e.g., age 75 or 80).

**Best for:** Clients who want to secure future income at today's rates,
especially for advanced-age longevity protection.

**Key features:**

- Lower premium than SPIA for the same income (because of deferral period)
- Excellent longevity insurance — protects against living into 90s
- No market risk, no fees during deferral
- QLAC option for qualified money (delays RMDs)
- Limited or no liquidity during deferral

**When to recommend:**

- Client wants to ensure income in late retirement (age 80+)
- Client has qualified assets and wants to reduce RMDs before age 75
- As the "longevity insurance" layer of a retirement plan

---

### Qualified Longevity Annuity Contract (QLAC)

**What it is:** A specific type of DIA purchased with qualified (IRA/401k) money
that is excluded from RMD calculations until payments begin.

**Key rules:**

- Maximum premium: $200,000 (adjusted for inflation; was raised from $145,000 by
  SECURE 2.0)
- Income must begin by age 85
- Purchased with IRA or 401(k) funds only
- Premium excluded from RMD calculations until payments start
- Return of premium option available

**When to recommend:**

- Client has large IRA/401(k) balances and doesn't need all the RMD income
- Client wants to reduce taxable income in early retirement
- Client concerned about longevity risk in late retirement

## Life Insurance Products

### Term Life Insurance

**What it is:** Pure death benefit protection for a set period (10, 15, 20, 30
years). No cash value.

**Best for:** Clients who need maximum death benefit at the lowest cost,
typically during working years.

**Key features:**

- Lowest premium per $1,000 of coverage
- Level premiums for the selected term
- Convertible to permanent insurance (most policies)
- No cash value accumulation
- Coverage ends at term expiration (or premiums skyrocket)

**When to recommend:**

- Young families with mortgage and children
- Business owners needing key person coverage
- Income replacement during earning years
- Temporary need (e.g., until kids finish college)

---

### Whole Life Insurance

**What it is:** Permanent life insurance with guaranteed death benefit,
guaranteed cash value growth, and potential dividends.

**Best for:** Clients who want lifelong coverage with guaranteed cash value
accumulation and estate planning benefits.

**Key features:**

- Guaranteed death benefit for life
- Guaranteed cash value growth
- Fixed premiums that never increase
- Potential dividends (mutual company policies)
- Cash value accessible via loans or withdrawals
- Tax-free death benefit to beneficiaries
- Tax-deferred cash value growth

**When to recommend:**

- Estate planning / wealth transfer
- Clients who want forced savings with guarantees
- Business succession planning (buy-sell agreements)
- Charitable giving strategies
- Supplemental retirement income via policy loans

---

### Indexed Universal Life (IUL)

**What it is:** Permanent life insurance with flexible premiums and cash value
growth linked to a market index (similar to FIA logic), with a 0% floor.

**Best for:** Clients who want permanent coverage with growth potential and
premium flexibility.

**Key features:**

- Flexible premiums (within limits)
- Cash value growth linked to index performance
- 0% floor protects against negative returns
- Caps/participation rates limit upside
- Tax-free death benefit
- Tax-advantaged cash value access via loans
- Can be over-funded for tax-free retirement income (with proper structuring)

**When to recommend:**

- High-income clients seeking tax-advantaged cash accumulation
- Clients who want both death benefit and living benefits
- Supplemental retirement income strategy (max-funded IUL)
- Business owners wanting tax-efficient compensation strategies

**Caution:** IUL is more complex than whole life. Illustrations can be overly
optimistic. Always stress-test with lower assumed rates.

## Retirement Risks That Products Address

| Risk                     | What It Means                        | Product Solution                                        |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Longevity risk           | Outliving your money                 | SPIA, DIA, FIA with income rider                        |
| Market risk              | Losing principal in a downturn       | FIA, MYGA (no market loss)                              |
| Sequence of returns risk | Bad market in early retirement years | FIA, SPIA (removes withdrawal sequence risk)            |
| Inflation risk           | Purchasing power erosion             | FIA (index-linked growth), inflation-adjusted SPIA      |
| Interest rate risk       | Low rates eroding fixed income       | FIA (captures upside), annuity laddering                |
| Long-term care risk      | Catastrophic care costs              | Hybrid life/LTC, annuity with LTC rider                 |
| Tax risk                 | Higher future tax rates              | Roth conversion + annuity strategy, tax-deferred growth |
| Withdrawal rate risk     | Taking too much too fast             | SPIA/DIA (guaranteed payout regardless of account)      |
